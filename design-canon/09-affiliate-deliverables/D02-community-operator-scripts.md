# D02 — Community-Operator Outreach Scripts

**Deliverable:** Three scripts for outreach to community operators (Discord/Slack/Circle/Geneva/Skool admins running rooms for SDRs and AEs).
**Voice ratio:** 80 Rowan / 20 Michael. Operator-to-operator.
**Sender:** Founder, by name. (Per canon §12.5 — partners get the human.)
**Length target:** 90–110 words each.
**Canon refs:** `AFFILIATE_BRAND_CANON.md` §3.3, §4, §5, §12.

## Use

- **Cold** — first message, no prior contact.
- **Warm** — they replied to a post, attended a session, mutual intro, etc.
- **Reply-to-no-reply** — one bump, then we go quiet.

Personalization required in every send: community name, one specific signal you actually saw (a thread, a post, a member quote), and the founder's first name. Brackets are not a substitute for reading the room.

---

## 1. Cold — community operator

> Subject: Workshop pilot for early-career reps in your community
>
> [Name] —
>
> I run AESDR — a 12-lesson sales survival course for first-1-to-2-year SDRs and AEs in startup SaaS. The reps your community hosts look like exactly who we built it for.
>
> Rather than pitch a generic affiliate arrangement, I'd rather test one live workshop into [community]. We measure registration-to-purchase honestly, compare cohort quality against any other channel you've run, and only extend if the numbers are real.
>
> Non-exclusive, time-boxed, no carousel. Worth a 20-minute call to see if the fit is actually there?
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

## 2. Warm — community operator

> Subject: Following up on [specific signal]
>
> [Name] —
>
> Saw your [thread / post / session] on [specific topic]. The pattern you described is the exact gap AESDR teaches around — particularly Course 4 (Navigating Manager Madness) and Course 7 (Prospecting & Pipeline).
>
> I'm running a small workshop-first partner motion — one live session into a partner's audience, tracked end-to-end, with no exclusivity. [Community] keeps coming up as a high-fit room.
>
> Open to a 20-minute call this week or next? I'd rather test than pitch.
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

## 3. Reply-to-no-reply — community operator

> Subject: Bumping once, then I'll go quiet
>
> [Name] —
>
> Bumping the workshop-pilot note once. Two reasons it might still be worth a look: one, it's non-exclusive and time-boxed, so the cost of testing is low. Two, the partners we're piloting with are picky about ICP — [community] still reads as a tight fit.
>
> If the timing's wrong or the answer's no, no offense taken — we part as adults. Otherwise I'm around this week or next.
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

---

## Notes

- The opener avoids "I hope this finds you well" and any LinkedIn-formal scaffolding by design.
- `Course 4` / `Course 7` references are real curriculum titles per `variants/variant-a-editorial-split.html`. Don't make up course numbers.
- "No carousel" (cold #1) and "we part as adults" (#3) are intentional brand fingerprints — they signal "we're not the LinkedIn-influencer flavor of this pitch."
- Founder signs by first name only. No title. Title-as-signature reads as overcompensating for an early-stage program.

## Open

- Calendar tooling: Cal.com vs SavvyCal vs plain reply-with-times. Default until decided: reply-with-times in the *response* to interest, not in the cold. Cold has no booking link.
- If a partner replies "send me a deck" instead of taking a call, do we send a 1-pager (pitch deck-lite) or hold out for a call? Default: send a 1-pager (D21 — partner-positioning brief) plus a 20-min call link.
