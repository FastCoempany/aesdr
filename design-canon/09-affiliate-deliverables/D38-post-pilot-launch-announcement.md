# D38 — Post-Pilot Launch Announcement

**Deliverable:** The communication AESDR sends to the broader market once the pilot phase has produced enough operating-data to justify a public-facing launch beyond partner channels. **One announcement template, three render targets** (email, landing-page hero block, social posts) so the message is consistent across surfaces. Ships when AESDR transitions from "in pilot with [partner]" register to "open for direct enrollment with [N] partner pilots' worth of operating data behind it" register.
**Audience:** AESDR's broader market — early-career SaaS SDRs and AEs who haven't been routed through a specific partner. The audience pool that will populate the AESDR-direct funnel after pilots stabilize. **Not** the original pilot partners' audiences (those are post-pilot ambassadors per D36, communicated to differently).
**Voice ratio:** 80 Rowan / 20 Michael per canon §3.3 (workshop-related register). Verdict mode. The Michael register lives in the PS line and the "what we learned that surprised us" beat.
**Format:** Markdown source. Renders to (1) HTML email for AESDR's existing list, (2) embeddable HTML block on `aesdr.com` homepage, (3) social posts in the same shape as `09d-social-pre-approved-posts.md` but founder-side instead of partner-side.
**Send timing:** Triggered when **all three** are true: at least 3 successful EXTEND-tier pilots completed (per D32); at least 2 ambassadorships active (per D36); cohort dashboard (D37 View 3) shows convergence on conversion-rate baselines, not divergence. **No earlier.** Per canon §1.4, borrowed-trust doctrine — and AESDR's own market trust is borrowed from the pilots that came before. Premature launch announcements are how brands lose the discipline that earned them the right to launch.
**Canon refs:** `AFFILIATE_BRAND_CANON.md` §1 (foundational doctrines), §1.6 (honesty discipline), §3.3 (voice ratios), §4.4 (subject-line discipline), §10.2 (approved claims), §10.3 (forbidden claims), §13 (honesty discipline), §14 (taglines), §16 (approval gates).

> **Placeholder convention:** `[N_PILOTS]`, `[N_AMBASSADORS]`, `[N_GRADUATES]` (total alumni count), `[FIRST_PILOT_DATE]`, `[LAUNCH_DATE]`, `[FOUNDER_FIRST_NAME]`, `[REGISTRATION_URL]`. Filled per launch.

## What this announcement does and doesn't claim

This is the most-public AESDR-direct surface in the deliverable set, which means it's also the surface most at risk of drifting toward the SaaS-norm "we're so excited to announce" register. Three discipline checks before any draft:

| Discipline check | What lands | What's forbidden |
|---|---|---|
| Honesty discipline (canon §13) | Specific pilot count, specific time window, specific things we learned that surprised us | "Hundreds of reps" / "Industry-leading" / unverifiable scale claims |
| Approved claims (§10.2) | Structural promise (12 lessons, 5 tools, etc.), the lift from operator interviews + pilot data, "built by people who carried bags" | Income claims, ROI promises, "transform your career" register |
| Founder-backstage (canon §12) | Founder may sign this announcement (canon §12.4 — a named milestone is one of the rare cases founder visibility is earned) | Founder photo; founder personal-brand framing; "from the founder" hero block |

**Founder visibility is the deliberate departure here.** Per canon §12.4 ("named milestone"), the launch *is* a milestone — AESDR has cleared pilot phase. The announcement may carry the founder's first name in the sign-off (a first per canon §12.5, where audience-facing communications are signed `AESDR Workshop` or `[HOST]`, never the founder). **This requires founder pre-approval before send.**

## Subject line + pre-header (Target 1 — email)

> **Subject:** *AESDR is open. Here's what we learned in the pilots first.*

**Discipline check (canon §4.4):**
- 9 words — at the upper end of 4–8 ceiling. Justified because the second clause is the contract: this email earns its place by actually saying what we learned.
- Sentence case. No emoji. No "🎉 We're open!"
- The body must deliver "what we learned" specifically — not abstractly. If it can't, the subject is wrong.

> **Pre-header:** *Three pilots in. [N_GRADUATES] graduates. Now open for direct enrollment, with the operating data behind it.*

## Body (template)

### Header

`AESDR` wordmark, `--display` italic 900wt, `--ink`, on `--cream`. No partner co-brand lockup — this is AESDR-only.

### Salutation

> *Hi —*

If on the email render target, no first-name personalization (we don't have first names on the broader list reliably).

### Body

> *AESDR ran for `[N_PILOTS]` pilots before opening direct.*
>
> *Three SaaS-content rules we held to during pilot phase, that we're keeping going forward:*
>
> ***— Workshop-first.*** *Every partner audience saw the program through a 60-minute live workshop, not a tracked link. The workshop earns the sale; the link merely attributes it. We're keeping that structure for direct enrollment too — open workshops, monthly cadence, free to attend, with the same offer at the close.*
>
> ***— Operator over guru.*** *We ran the entire pilot phase founder-backstage, host-fronted. We're keeping the structure. The brand is the curriculum, not the founder, not the host. AESDR was built to outlast any one person delivering it.*
>
> ***— Honest disqualification.*** *Every pilot's offer surface named who shouldn't enroll. No exceptions. The retention math after the 14-day refund window held up at `[REFUND_RATE_PERCENT]` — within the band we expected for a no-questions-asked program. Not zero (zero would mean we were over-filtering); not double-digit (which would mean we were under-filtering).*
>
> *Three things we learned in pilot phase that we didn't expect:*
>
> ***— The recommended pace is recommended, not real.*** *We designed the curriculum for a 6-8 week pace at 1-2 lessons per week. Actual graduate pace: `[GRADUATE_PACE_RANGE]`. Not because the program is harder than expected — because real reps have real quarters. We're keeping the recommended pace as a recommendation, not pretending the real pace is something it isn't.*
>
> ***— The lessons reps recognized themselves in landed harder than the frameworks did.*** *The recognition moments — the canon §3.2 register, the embarrassingly-specific honesty about what the role actually does to people — drove enrollment more than the framework slides did. Reps decide to enroll on recognition, not on instruction. We're surfacing more of the recognition register in the next curriculum revision, not less.*
>
> ***— L&D-routed enrollment was higher than estimated.*** *We thought a smaller share of enrollees would come through manager-approved reimbursement. Actual: `[LD_ENROLLMENT_RATIO]`. The L&D-approver brief (a one-page PDF written for the conversation with a manager) carried more weight than we forecasted. We're keeping it; we're improving it.*
>
> *AESDR is now open for direct enrollment at* `aesdr.com`. *List pricing — `$249` SDR, `$299` AE, `$1,499` 10-seat Team. Lifetime access. 14-day refund. Same terms every pilot ran on.*

## What this announcement is NOT

- **Not a "pilot wrap-up" thank-you tour.** Pilot partners get D34 + D36 communications individually; D38 is the broader-market announcement, not the partner-acknowledgment vehicle.
- **Not an "AESDR raised funding" / "AESDR has a new investor" announcement.** That's a different category and would require a separate canon-revision intake (D35) before any such language ships.
- **Not a "feature update" announcement.** AESDR ships curriculum updates continuously per canon §"lifetime access is literal"; those don't get announced as launch milestones.
- **Not the founder's personal-brand launch.** Founder visibility (sign-off, social posts) earns its place by virtue of canon §12.4 milestone-eligibility. After the 30-day launch window, founder retreats to default backstage per canon §12.1.
- **Not a recurring announcement template.** This is a *one-time* deliverable for the pilot-to-direct transition. Subsequent broader-market communications (e.g., a year-1 retrospective) require a separate deliverable per canon §17 (don't repurpose the launch template into a quarterly send).

## Compliance check (canon §10)

- **FTC §10.1:** No partner attribution on this surface (AESDR-direct). Footer states this is the launch announcement — clear and accurate. **Pass.**
- **CAN-SPAM (§10.4):** Footer carries postal address, unsubscribe, and accurate "why you're receiving this" line. **Pass.**
- **Approved claims (§10.2):** Pilot count, graduate count, refund rate, pace range, L&D ratio — all use placeholder fields filled at send time from real pilot data. **Pass conditional on real data substituting every placeholder before send.** Per canon §13, no number ships in this announcement that hasn't been verified against the cohort dashboard (D37 View 3) the same week.
- **Forbidden claims (§10.3):** No "guaranteed promotion," "earn back your investment," or income claims. The pace range is observational and time-bounded, not promissory. **Pass.**

## Open

- **Number of pilots threshold.** Default: 3+ EXTEND-tier. Lower bias: 3 pilots is enough operating-data to substantiate the announcement claims; fewer risks claiming convergence we don't have. Higher bias: more pilots = more confidence; but also delays the launch past the point AESDR's market trust starts to atrophy. Default: 3.
- **Whether the founder appears named in the social posts.** Default: **yes** for first-instance per canon §12.4; founder retreats from social after the 30-day window, returning to backstage default.
- **Whether to ship a "thank you to the pilot partners" embedded section in the email.** Default: **no** in the broader-market send. Pilot partners get individual D34 + D36 communications. A "thank you" embedded in the broader send dilutes both messages and risks reading as social proof rather than relationship.
- **Whether the homepage launch-hero stays past 30 days if traffic is strong.** Default: **30-day cap regardless.** A perpetually-launching brand reads as not-actually-launched. Per canon §13, we ship the announcement, archive it cleanly, and move on.
- **Whether to ship the announcement in stages** (founder-list-only first, broader market 7 days later). Default: **single send.** Staging implies we're hedging on confidence in the announcement. If the announcement is right, send it once; if it isn't, don't send it.
- **Whether to repeat the announcement to non-openers.** Default: **no.** Same anti-fake-urgency stance as D18 — one announcement, one window. Re-sending implies the message itself didn't earn its place.
- **Whether the social posts get paid distribution.** Default: **no.** Per canon §5.2 + §11, paid LinkedIn / X promotion is forbidden for AESDR's primary distribution. Organic only. If paid distribution is later ratified, requires canon-revision intake (D35).
