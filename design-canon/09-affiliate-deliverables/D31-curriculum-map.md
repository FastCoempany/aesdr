## The structure in one paragraph

AESDR is **12 lessons across three four-lesson clusters: Foundations, Range, Identity.** Each lesson is approximately 45–60 minutes including the worksheet and tool. The five named tools (canon §14: *"12 lessons. 5 tools. 1 you."*) anchor the takeaway side of the program — five operating sheets you put on your desk and use. The clusters reflect the through-line of the program: build the operating standard (Foundations), extend the range of moves you can make (Range), become the rep who closes (Identity).

## The three clusters

### Cluster 1 — Foundations (Lessons 1–4)

> *(--display italic 700, 28pt, --ink. Cluster framing line in --hand 22pt, --crimson, rotation -1deg, set as a margin annotation rather than body — Michael register, canon §3.2.)*

**Cluster framing — the operating standard nobody handed you on day one.**

Foundations is where the lens forms. You leave this cluster with the diagnostic frame for your own activity, your own pipeline, and your own discovery work. If the rest of the program is "what to do," Foundations is "how to read what's happening." Skip it and the rest of the program is moves without a frame.

| # | Lesson title | Length | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| **01** | The operating standard | ~50 min | You leave with a working definition of "good" you can use Monday morning — not a feeling, an operating standard. |
| **02** | Activity vs judgment | ~55 min | You can read your own activity through the X-axis (judgment) lens, not just the Y-axis (volume) lens. The framework from D09 slide 07 is the working tool. |
| **03** | Discovery as evidence | ~60 min | You stop confusing discovery questions with discovery evidence. You leave with a checklist for "what does this stage actually require to advance?" |
| **04** | Pipeline integrity | ~55 min | You can tell the difference between the pipeline in CRM and the pipeline that closes. The framework from D09 slide 11 is the working tool. |

**Cluster 1 tool:** **The Operating Standard Sheet** (`tool-1-operating-standard.html` in production). One-page, fillable. The reference you put on your desk after Lesson 4.

### Cluster 2 — Range (Lessons 5–8)

> *(Cluster framing in --hand, --crimson:)*
>
> **Cluster framing — the moves you weren't told existed.**

Range is where the move set extends. You stop running the same three plays into every situation. You build an objection vocabulary, a negotiation rail set, a multi-thread habit, and a forecast-honesty practice. The cluster's premise: most reps lose deals not from wrong tactics but from a too-thin set of moves to choose from.

| # | Lesson title | Length | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| **05** | Objection patterns | ~55 min | You recognize the 8 patterns that account for ~85% of objections in early-career SaaS sales, and you know which 2 each of yours falls into. |
| **06** | Negotiation rails | ~50 min | You can hold pricing, terms, and timeline in a buyer conversation without freelancing. You leave with a rail set, not a script. |
| **07** | Multi-thread or die | ~50 min | You can name a deal's "single-threaded" risk and act on it within 48 hours of identification. You leave with a multi-threading discipline. |
| **08** | Forecast honesty | ~55 min | You can talk to your manager about a pipeline that shrunk between 1:1s without losing the room. You leave with the forecast-revision conversation as a tool. |

**Cluster 2 tools:** **The Objection Pattern Bank** (`tool-2-objection-bank.html`) and **The Multi-thread Map** (`tool-3-multi-thread-map.html`). Two of the five.

---

### Cluster 3 — Identity (Lessons 9–12)

> *(Cluster framing in --hand, --crimson:)*
>
> **Cluster framing — becoming the rep who closes, not the rep who tries hard.**

Identity is where the operating manual becomes operating identity. You stop performing sales and start doing it. The cluster covers the relational and structural conditions of the role — managing your manager, reading your comp plan, surviving ramp, becoming someone whose pipeline you'd bet on. The cluster's premise: reps who survive ramp and reps who don't usually look identical on activity in month one. The difference shows up in identity.

| # | Lesson title | Length | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| **09** | Manager management | ~50 min | You can run a 1:1 instead of being run by one. You leave with a 1:1 prep template that's the manager's job, not yours, but you do it anyway. |
| **10** | Comp math | ~45 min | You can read your own comp plan, including the parts that aren't written down (clawbacks, accelerator triggers, what counts as "closed-won" for commission purposes). |
| **11** | Ramp survival | ~55 min | You can identify the three patterns that get reps fired from ramp before they're caused by them. You leave with the ramp-month operating cadence. |
| **12** | Becoming the person who closes | ~60 min | You leave with a rolling self-assessment you run weekly. Not a "self-help check-in." A diagnostic. |

**Cluster 3 tools:** **The 1:1 Prep Template** (`tool-4-one-on-one.html`) and **The 12.3 72-Hour Strike Plan** (`tool-5-strike-plan.html`). Three plus two = the five tools.

## What the curriculum is not

Per canon §13 honesty discipline, the absences:

- **Not a sales-introduction course.** AESDR assumes you have the seat. If you're trying to break into sales, this isn't the course.
- **Not a sales-leadership course.** AESDR is built for the SDR-to-AE-to-first-year-AE arc. Manager-track content isn't here.
- **Not industry-specific.** AESDR is calibrated for early-career SaaS sales generally. If your problem is SaaS-vs-services-vs-fintech-specific, AESDR is the operating manual; the industry tactics live elsewhere.
- **Not motivational.** The clusters' framing lines (in Michael register) are the closest the curriculum gets to "feel-good." The lessons themselves are operating content.
- **Not a credential program.** No certificate. See FAQ Q08.

## Open

- **Lesson lengths.** Currently approximate (~45–60 min each). Production may yield specific runtimes per lesson — recommend updating once final cuts are locked. Approximate-only is acceptable in v1.
- **Tool numbering vs lesson numbering.** Currently the five tools land after lessons 4, 5, 7, 9, 11 — slight asymmetry. Worth surfacing: the tools are not one-per-lesson. The phrase *"12 lessons. 5 tools. 1 you."* (canon §14) communicates this honestly. Do not invent 12 tools to make the math symmetric.
- **Whether to publish a per-lesson "what you'll be able to do after this lesson" outcome statement separately from the cluster outcome.** Default: **outcome per lesson is in the table above; outcome per cluster is implicit in the cluster framing line.** Adding both layers risks marketing-bloat. The table's "Outcome" column is the canonical per-lesson outcome statement.
- **Cluster framing lines.** Currently 3 (one per cluster). Cleared as Michael register margin annotations per canon §3.2. If a partner audience reaction shows the framing lines are doing too much work, default fallback: render the framing as ink Display italic instead of Caveat crimson, and lift Michael register elsewhere.
- **Whether the curriculum map should ship as a 1-pager or 2-pager PDF.** Default: **1-pager landscape** for the partner kit, **2-pager portrait** for the L&D brief attachment (more whitespace, easier to print and circulate inside enterprise). Both render from this same markdown source.
- **Tool 12.3 — the ".3" naming.** This is canonical from production (`tool-5-strike-plan.html` is named for chapter 12.3). Not a typo. Defending the artifact's name from people who would normalize it.
