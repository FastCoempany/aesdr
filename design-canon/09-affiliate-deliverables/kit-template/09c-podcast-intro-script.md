# 09c — Podcast / Live Intro Script (template)

> **What this is:** Two scripts — (1) a 2-minute live workshop intro you read at the top of the workshop per D09 slide 02, and (2) a 60-second podcast / video intro for promoting the workshop on your audio surfaces. Per D40 §2.1 + canon §7.1, the live intro is capped at 2 minutes and is partner ad-libbed from this script (not read verbatim). The podcast intro is shorter and more flexible.

> **Tone:** Operator-to-operator. 70/30 Rowan/Michael per canon §3.3 (workshop registration page row — closest match for live intros). Verbatim script, partner ad-libbed delivery.

> **Approval status:** Pre-cleared for partner use with placeholders filled. The locked elements below are non-negotiable per canon §7.1.

## Script 1 — Live workshop intro (2 minutes, capped)

Read at the top of the live workshop, immediately before handing to `[HOST_FIRST_NAME]`. Per canon §7.1, partner intros are 2 minutes max — over is a contract issue, not a stylistic preference.

> *Hi everyone — thanks for being here. I want to introduce `[HOST_FIRST_NAME]` and AESDR briefly, then I'm going to step out of the way.*
>
> *I came across AESDR because `[YOUR_REASON]` — one or two sentences. It's the only sales-training resource I've seen built specifically for the part of the role where you're trying to figure out what good actually looks like and nobody's telling you. That gap was real for me, and from the conversations I've had with `[YOUR_AUDIENCE_DESCRIPTOR]`, I know it's real for a lot of you.*
>
> *Three quick rules for the next 60 minutes:*
>
> *— Drop your questions in the chat. `[HOST_FIRST_NAME]` will get to them at the end.*
>
> *— The replay will be available for 72 hours after we wrap.*
>
> *— There's an offer at the close that's specific to this group. That's it.*
>
> *Over to you, `[HOST_FIRST_NAME]`.*

### Live intro — discipline notes

- **Cap is 2 minutes.** Practice it once. If your first read is 2:30, cut a sentence; do not run long.
- **Do not pitch the offer.** Saying "and there's a great offer at the end" is fine; saying "and you'll get $X off if you stay until the end" is not — that's pricing language and requires the approval round per D40 §3.
- **Do not discuss pricing.** Even if asked. Say "AESDR will cover that at the close" and move on.
- **Do not ad-lib about AESDR's content.** The "I came across AESDR because..." line is yours; everything after that has rails. Per canon §7.1, this is the deal — partner intro is partner-fronted but content-bounded.
- **Hand off cleanly.** "Over to you, `[HOST_FIRST_NAME]`" is the stage direction. Don't say "alright, take it away!" or "the floor is yours, friend!" Hand off plainly and stop talking.

## Script 2 — Podcast / video promotional intro (60 seconds)

Used on your podcast intro, your video promo for the workshop, your YouTube short, your Instagram/TikTok pre-roll. Read or perform; the script is a starting point, not a verbatim lock — but the locked elements below stay.

> *This week's episode is a quick announcement, not a full conversation. I'm hosting a live workshop for `[YOUR_AUDIENCE_DESCRIPTOR]` with AESDR — a 12-lesson sales-training program built for early-career SaaS reps. Most sales-training rewards confidence over competence; AESDR is the opposite. Operating manual, not motivation engine.*
>
> *60 minutes live. Replay for 72 hours after. Free to attend. There's an offer at the close — pilot pricing on the program.*
>
> *Affiliate disclosure: I get a commission if you enroll through my link. The commission doesn't change the price you pay.*
>
> *Link is in the show notes. `[REGISTRATION_URL]` if you're listening to this somewhere without notes.*

### Podcast intro — discipline notes

- **Length cap: 60 seconds.** Audio audiences forgive a long episode; they don't forgive a long ad. Keep it at 60.
- **Disclosure is mandatory** within the first 10 seconds OR clearly named within the intro per canon §10.1. The script above puts it just before the link — also acceptable per FTC's "clearly and conspicuously" rule.
- **Do not do "5-star testimonial" framing.** No "I've been using AESDR for X months and I'm a different rep" — you haven't, and that's a fake claim per `00-canon-excerpt.md` §4.1.
- **Do not promise a code in the audio.** The pricing code is on the registration page; promising it audio-only ("use code [X] for an extra 20% off!") is forbidden per `02-pricing-and-promo-sheet.pdf` §2.

## What you may NOT change in either script

- **The "Operating manual, not motivation engine" line.** Canon §14 tagline. Lift verbatim.
- **The "I came across AESDR because..." structure.** The honest origin story is the brand move per canon §1.6. "I'm partnering with AESDR because they have a great affiliate program" is forbidden — that's a different positioning than the canon allows.
- **The "free to attend" + "72-hour replay" beats.** Canon §7.1.
- **The disclosure language.** Canon §10.1 + your pilot agreement §3.3.
- **Banned vocab from §4.1** anywhere in your delivery — even improvised.

## Approval workflow if you want to deviate

If you want a longer intro (e.g., a 90-second podcast intro with more context), submit your draft 48 hours pre-publish per D40 §3. AESDR responds within 24 business hours.

The most common approved deviation: a 90-second podcast intro that adds one sentence about the specific operating problem the workshop addresses for *your* audience. That's a fit signal AESDR welcomes.

The most common declined deviation: any intro that frames AESDR as "the answer to your sales career" or makes income-claims-by-implication ("the reps who finish AESDR start hitting their numbers"). Forbidden per canon §10.3.
