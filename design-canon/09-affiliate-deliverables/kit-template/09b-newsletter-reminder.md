# 09b — Newsletter Reminder Send (template)

> **What this is:** Pre-cleared reminder send, sent during promotion week 2 (3–5 days before the workshop). Shorter, more specific, no re-explanation of what AESDR is — assumes the reader saw `09a-newsletter-launch.md`. Per D40 §2.1, pre-cleared for partner use with placeholders filled.
>
> **Send timing:** 3–5 days before the workshop. Send only to non-registrants from the launch list, OR to your full list if your audience opens reminders at meaningfully higher rates than first sends.
>
> **Tone:** 80/20 Rowan/Michael per canon §3.3. The Michael register lives in the PS line. Verdict-shaped subject; specific body; honest disqualification preserved.
>
> **Length:** ~180–220 words including disclosure. Shorter than `09a` by design — reminders earn their place by being specific, not by re-litigating.

## What you may NOT change

- **Subject line.** Locked. The honest "X days until" framing is the brand move; "Don't miss out!" framing is forbidden per canon §4.1.
- **The "Most useful 60 minutes for you if" block.** Locked. Lifts the canonical disqualification register.
- **The PS line.** Locked. The "no third email coming" beat is the canonical anti-fake-urgency move per canon §9.2.
- **The disclosure line.** Locked.
- **Banned vocab in your one-sentence addition** — see `00-canon-excerpt.md` §4.1.

---

## What you may add (with caution)

- **A real specific signal from your audience** — e.g., "Three replies to the launch send asked about pipeline integrity specifically; this workshop's framework names exactly that." Lands well; reads as operator-to-operator.
- **A pull-quote from a registered member who replied to launch** — only with their explicit permission and only if the quote does not make a forbidden claim per `00-canon-excerpt.md` §10.

## What you may NOT add

- A countdown timer / "only X seats left" framing. False scarcity is forbidden per canon §9.2.
- A second offer ("plus, a free 1:1 with the host if you register today"). The workshop is one offer; bonuses contradict canon §13.
- Banned vocab from §4.1 anywhere — including in your one-sentence opener.
- "Last chance" / "final reminder" framing. Per canon §13, last chance only earns its place when there's no follow-up — and the reminder is the follow-up.
