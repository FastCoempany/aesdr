**Audience:** AESDR-internal — founder, ops. Never shared with the partner. The conversion conversation itself is partner-facing and uses different language; this document is the AESDR-side strategy and decision rubric behind that conversation.

## What an ambassador is (and isn't)

An **ambassador** is a partner who graduated from the pilot model into a longer-term, lower-overhead relationship with AESDR. Ambassadors:

- Run **multiple workshops** across a quarter or year, not single pilots.
- Carry a **specific commercial term** that's different from D22's pilot agreement (longer attribution, possibly different commission rate, possibly a small fixed retainer).
- Have **earned canon-fluency** — they know what they can say without approval, and AESDR trusts them within the rails.
- May appear in **founder-visible contexts** per canon §12.4 ("rare and deliberate" — an established ambassador is one of the cases where founder visibility is earned).

Ambassadors are NOT:

- **An "elite tier"** AESDR upsells partners into. Per canon §1.3, the ambassador relationship is earned, not sold.
- **Permanent or guaranteed.** Ambassador relationships can sunset; the same EXTEND/REVISE/KILL discipline that ends pilots ends ambassadorships, just on a longer cadence.
- **Free of approval gates.** Lower per-asset overhead, not zero. Per canon §16, paid placements + recorded assets always need pre-approval.
- **Exclusive.** Per canon §10.4 D22 inheritance — non-exclusivity is permanent in both directions.

The number of ambassadors at any time is small. Canon §1.3 founding-vineyard discipline: small rows, careful pruning, honest yield. **Target ambassador count at steady state: 3-5.** More than that, and we're running an affiliate empire — different model, different canon, different doctrine.

## The conversion gate (when to invite, when not to)

A pilot partner becomes a candidate for ambassador conversion only if **all six** of the following are true after the pilot. Less than six = stay in pilot mode for one more cycle.

| Gate | Threshold | Source |
|---|---|---|
| 1. D32 decision | **EXTEND** with no REVISE conditions | D32 §4 |
| 2. Net-revenue-per-founder-hour | Above the canon §"effective hourly rate" baseline | D32 §1 + §3 |
| 3. ICP fit (D33 §6) | "tight ICP" verdict | D33 §6 |
| 4. Brand audit (D33 §7) | Zero "anti-canon moves" letting through | D33 §7 |
| 5. Partner relationship signal | Founder gut-check on §6.5 of D27: *will I want to talk to this person again next quarter?* — answer is yes, with specific reasons | D27 §6.5 |
| 6. Partner's audience signal | At least one D17 high-intent reply per 10 attendees during the pilot, with operating-quality questions | D17 log |

If any one fails, the partner runs another pilot before ambassador conversation. Per canon §1.6, we tell them so honestly: *"Let's run pilot 2 with the [specific change] from postmortem before we look at a longer relationship."*

## The conversion timing

| When | What happens |
|---|---|
| **Pilot end + 7 days** | D32 memo finalized. EXTEND decision recorded. |
| **Pilot end + 9 days** | D34 EXTEND-version sent to partner. The amendment to D22 covers the next pilot's scope. |
| **Pilot end + 30-60 days** | If pilot 2 also EXTENDs and §"conversion gate" remains all-six, founder opens the ambassador conversation. |
| **Pilot end + 90 days** | If both pilots cleared all six gates, ambassador agreement drafted (D22-variant; see §5 below). |

The 30-60 day waiting period is not bureaucracy — it's per canon §1.4 (borrowed trust is a merciless mirror). Two pilots is enough data; three is theater.

**Exception:** an ambassador conversation may open earlier if the partner explicitly asks ("hey, I'd like to do this longer-term — what does that look like?"). In that case, the founder still applies the six-gate check before responding. If the gates clear, the partner-prompted timing is fine. If they don't clear, the honest answer is: *"Let's run pilot 2 first — here's why."*

## Ambassador agreement structure (the D22-variant)

The ambassador agreement is a **separate written agreement**, not an extension of D22. Differences from D22 (which is the per-pilot template):

| D22 (pilot) | Ambassador agreement |
|---|---|
| 30-day pilot window | Quarterly term, auto-extending only on written approval |
| Commission 30% net | Commission may differ — typically 30% with possible small retainer ($500-$1,500 per quarter) for ambassador-specific deliverables |
| 30-day attribution | 60-day attribution within ambassador term |
| Per-asset pre-approval (D22 §8.2) | Pre-approval bands: copy lifted from pre-cleared kit (no approval needed); copy generated by partner (24-hour async approval); paid placement (full 48-hr approval) |
| One workshop per pilot | Quarterly workshop cadence — typically 1-2 workshops per quarter, partner-scheduled with AESDR availability |
| Single co-branded page (D26) | Permanent partner-promo page surface (e.g., `aesdr.com/[PARTNER_SLUG]/` — long-lived, refreshed per workshop) |
| Termination per D22 §11 | 30-day notice on either side; cause-based termination unchanged |
| Approval gates per canon §16 | Same approval gates; ambassador retains canon §6.6 lockup license while agreement active |

The agreement is drafted as `D22a-ambassador-agreement.md` in the pilot folder when ambassadorship lands. **Counsel reviews v1 of the ambassador agreement before first execution, same as D22.**

## What ambassadors get (that pilot partners don't)

- **Lower per-asset approval friction.** Pre-cleared band described above.
- **Quarterly cadence flexibility.** Ambassadors schedule workshops within mutual availability rather than single-pilot timelines.
- **Permanent partner-promo surface** (`aesdr.com/[PARTNER_SLUG]/`). Updates per workshop.
- **First look at new AESDR programs.** When AESDR ships a new course, lesson, or tool, ambassadors see it before the public market does — for context and feedback, not as a reseller windfall.
- **Founder-appearance eligibility** per canon §12.4. Specific events — a podcast crossover, a joint workshop where the founder is announced (rare), an alumni-class graduation appearance — become permissible. The default still leans backstage; the door just opens.
- **Mutual reference status.** AESDR will verifiably reference the ambassador in conversation with future partners (with their permission). Reciprocally, AESDR is fine being named in their materials.

## What ambassadors do not get

- **Discount-stacking authority.** Pilot pricing rules (`02-pricing-and-promo-sheet.pdf`) still apply. Ambassadors don't get a "extra 10% off for my audience" code per canon §13.
- **Co-creation rights.** Ambassadors don't co-author AESDR curriculum. The brand-of-record creates the program; ambassadors carry it.
- **List access.** Per canon §1.6 + D22 inheritance — list exchange is not part of any AESDR partner agreement.
- **Personal-brand integration.** Ambassadors are operators we work with; they do not become "AESDR Ambassadors" as a co-branded role they can list on LinkedIn-as-credential. Per canon §1.5.
- **Founder-on-demand.** Founder appearance per canon §12.4 is event-specific, not on-call.
- **Veto rights on other partners.** Non-exclusive doctrine survives ambassadorship.

## The conversion conversation (founder-led)

When the six gates clear and the timing is right, the founder opens the ambassador conversation. Operator-to-operator. 30-45 minutes. The founder runs it; ops takes notes.

### 6.1 Pre-call: founder writes a draft term sheet

Before the call, the founder fills the `D22a-ambassador-agreement.md` template (which is essentially D22 with the §5 deltas above). The draft is **not** sent to the partner before the call — the call is where the relationship-fit gets verified. Sending the draft first turns the call into a redline workshop.

### 6.2 The call

The founder's frame, plain English:

> *Two pilots in. Both ran clean. Want to talk about what a longer-term version of this looks like.*
>
> *AESDR runs a small number of ambassadorships at any given time — three to five. The relationship is structurally different from a pilot. Quarterly cadence instead of one workshop. Commission stays at 30%, plus we can talk about a small retainer for the ambassador-specific work. Permanent partner-promo page on the AESDR side. You get first look at new AESDR things before the broader market sees them.*
>
> *In return: the relationship's longer, the canon stays the same, the disclosure rules don't change, the non-exclusivity doesn't change.*
>
> *We're not asking you to commit on this call. The honest version is: are you interested? If yes, I send the draft term sheet within 24 hours. If no, we keep running pilot-by-pilot — that's also fine.*

### 6.3 What the founder is listening for

The conversation is its own evaluation. Per canon §1.4 borrowed-trust doctrine, ambassador candidates either pass or fail on signal during this call:

| Signal | Pass | Fail |
|---|---|---|
| Reaction to the canon's permanence | Treats it as feature ("the canon's why I'm in") | Treats it as constraint ("can we talk about loosening...") |
| Reaction to non-exclusivity | Comfortable, doesn't ask for exclusivity | Asks for category exclusivity or first-right-of-refusal |
| Reaction to founder visibility | Comfortable with rare appearance, not pushing for permanent feature | Pushes for founder-on-demand |
| Reaction to the retainer offer | Calibrated response (some pushback fine) | Treats it as the deal's centerpiece |
| Reaction to "we're not asking you to commit" | Specific follow-up questions about scope | Pressure for an immediate yes |

A FAIL signal on the call is a HOLD — same discipline as D27. Run another pilot; revisit in 30-60 days.

### 6.4 Post-call

Within 24 hours of a PASS-signal call:

- Founder sends the drafted `D22a-ambassador-agreement.md` for partner-side review.
- Counsel reviews if any partner-side redline crosses material terms (commission rate, attribution window, IP, indemnification).
- Standard redline workflow per D22 §16 applies.
- First ambassadorship-track workshop scheduled within 60 days.

## Sunsetting an ambassadorship

Ambassadorships end honestly, the same way pilots do. Per canon §13 + §1.6.

Triggers:

- **Two consecutive low-yield quarters** (commission accrued + audience-quality both below ambassador-baseline).
- **Canon contradiction** that ambassador won't reconcile (partner pushes for off-canon copy after being asked twice; D22-equivalent §11.1).
- **ICP drift** on the ambassador's audience (the audience that worked in pilots stops being early-career SaaS).
- **Mutual desire to wind down.** Either party may end on 30-day notice for any reason.

Sunset conversation: founder-led, in-person or video, not email. The "we part as adults" canonical phrase (canon §14) applies. Reference to the original D34 EXTEND-decision rationale; what changed since; what AESDR keeps from the relationship. Honest, brief, no relationship-eulogy energy.

A sunset is not a kill. The door stays open for future re-engagement (per canon §13 — AESDR's pilot honesty discipline applies to ambassadorships as well as pilots).

## Open

- **Whether to publish a "what an AESDR ambassador is" page** at `aesdr.com/ambassadors`. Default: **no for v1.** Publishing the structure invites would-be ambassadors to apply, which inverts the founding-vineyard doctrine (we cultivate; partners don't apply). Reconsider only if 5+ ambassadorships are running and there's specific signal that a transparent landing improves quality of outreach.
- **Whether ambassadors get any AESDR equity / advisory share.** Default: **no.** Per canon §1.5, ambassadors are operators, not advisors. Equity-style relationships introduce a different governance footprint we're not ready to maintain.
- **Whether ambassadors share an internal Slack / Discord with AESDR.** Default: **case-by-case.** Some ambassadors will benefit from direct channel; others won't. Default to email + occasional 1:1, not standing channel.
- **Whether the small retainer ($500-$1,500 / quarter) is universal** or by-archetype. Default: **case-by-case** per the §"What ambassadors do" deliverables. Bootcamp coaches with deeper involvement may earn higher retainer; community operators with lighter involvement may not need one.
- **Whether ambassadorships count toward §"Open questions" canon-revision triggers.** Default: **yes, with a higher signal threshold** — patterns from a single ambassadorship don't yet justify canon updates; patterns across two ambassadorships might.
