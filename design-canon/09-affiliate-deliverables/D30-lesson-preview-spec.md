# D30 — Lesson Preview Clip + Transcript Spec

**Deliverable:** Production specification for AESDR's canonical lesson-preview asset: one 10–15 minute clip drawn from one lesson's source material, plus a verbatim transcript, plus three takeaway bullets. The asset partners can lift to give their audience a feel for the actual caliber of instruction. One canonical clip, produced once, used across every pilot.
**Audience:** Three audiences, same source asset, three render targets:
- **Partners** — embed the clip on their newsletter / Substack / podcast notes / member channel during the promotion window. Lifted from the partner kit (D40 file `08-lesson-preview-clip-and-transcript/`).
- **Prospects** — the clip appears on the workshop registration page (D26) above-the-fold for visitors who want a "what does the program actually feel like" pre-decision watch.
- **L&D approvers** — the transcript is attached to the L&D-approver brief PDF as the "see the actual material" exhibit.
**Voice ratio:** The clip *contains* AESDR voice in the native lesson register. The *spec* itself runs 90/10 internal docs register.
**Format:** This document is the spec; the deliverable is a 10–15 min video file (MP4, 1080p, captioned), a transcript (markdown + PDF), and a three-bullet takeaway block (markdown, embeddable). Per canon §6.5 + §8.5.
**Canon refs:** `AFFILIATE_BRAND_CANON.md` §1.1 (workshop-first), §1.5 (operator over guru), §3.3 (voice ratios), §6.4 (iris reservation — applies to clip thumbnail), §6.6 (co-branding zone), §6.8 (custom iconography — for the clip's title card), §6.9 (visual QA), §7.2 (host casting profile), §13 (honesty discipline), §16 (approval gates — host ad-lib that becomes a recorded asset).

> **Placeholder convention:** `[CLIP_VIDEO_URL]`, `[CLIP_DURATION_MINUTES]`, `[HOST_FIRST_NAME]`, `[HOST_LAST_NAME]`, `[PARTNER_SLUG]` (when partner-co-branded variant is generated). Most placeholders are filled once per canonical clip — the clip is produced once, not per-pilot.

---

## Why one preview clip, not twelve

The temptation is to build a preview per lesson. Resist it. Per canon §1.1 (workshop-first, not link-first) and §1.5 (operator over guru): one clip, made well, is the asset. Twelve clips, made fast, are content. The partner audience needs to feel the operating-manual register once; they don't need a content library to browse.

**The chosen source is `658.85 / L11` — the Sober Selling lesson.** Working title for the clip on every public-facing surface is **"The Cost of Being On."** The clip alludes to the lesson territory (alcohol, sales-culture pressure, the operating cost of always-on energy) without naming "Sober Selling" or saying "alcohol" until the host says it on camera. Reasons in §2 below.

## 2. Why the Sober Selling source — working title "The Cost of Being On"

The canonical preview is drawn from `658.85 / L11` — the Sober Selling lesson — rendered for the public-facing partner kit under the working title **"The Cost of Being On."** Selection rationale:

- **Most distinctive AESDR voice.** L11 is the lesson no other sales-training program in the market has. Pipeline frameworks, objection patterns, comp-plan math — every competitor offers some version of those. Alcohol-and-sales-culture, head-on, with an annotation reading *"21+. not metaphor."* — that's only AESDR. Per canon §1.5 (operator over guru) and §1.6 (honesty as differentiator), the preview should showcase the most-distinctive territory, not the most-imitable one.
- **Hardest to imitate after the fact.** A competitor watching the preview cannot easily produce a competing version. The lesson's voice, framing, and stance on a subject most courses won't touch is the brand fingerprint.
- **Pain match across the ICP.** Per the affiliate-seeding research report ICP matrix, the "what you do when no one's watching" territory hits across every persona (SDR, AE, career-switcher, manager-sponsored buyer). Alcohol culture in sales is not segment-specific.
- **Voice mix sample.** The L11 source contains a mix of Rowan-register operating-doctrine narration (the work-hard-drink-harder myth, the longevity argument) and Michael-register honesty about specific behavior (the hungover dial block, the conference-bar moment, the teenage-self-image leftover). The clip catches both.
- **Allusive working title.** *"The Cost of Being On"* names the territory without saying *"alcohol,"* *"drinking,"* or *"sober selling"* until the host says so on camera. That deliberately delays the reveal — viewers self-select toward watching the full clip rather than skipping based on a title-only scan.
- **Workshop-as-teaser doctrine compatibility.** The preview maps to D09's slide-11 concept (*"What you do when no one's watching"*) without restating it. A viewer who attended the workshop sees the same territory enriched; a viewer who didn't sees the territory cold.

If pilot data shows a different lesson outperforms the L11 preview, this spec gets a v2 — but **only one canonical lesson at a time.** Multiple parallel previews dilute the pattern.

## 3. Clip structure (the 10–15 minute arc)

The clip is **not** the full L11.1 video re-cut. It is a purpose-built composition that uses L11.1 source material to demonstrate the lesson register in 10–15 minutes. The reveal of the lesson's specific subject (alcohol) happens in the host's narration, not in the title card or thumbnail.

| Time | Segment | Status |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00 – 0:45 | **Title card + host intro** — *"The Cost of Being On"* | Locked verbatim |
| 0:45 – 2:30 | **The territory** — host names the operating cost of "always-on" sales-culture energy. No subject reveal yet. | Locked source from L11.1 |
| 2:30 – 4:30 | **The specific reveal** — host names the subject (drinking). Per L11.1 source: *"For years, alcohol has been woven into AE culture / baked into SDR culture..."* | Locked source from L11.1 |
| 4:30 – 6:00 | **The myth, named** — host walks the "old way / new way / reality" frame from L11.1's first interactive | Locked source from L11.1 |
| 6:00 – 7:30 | **Reality interlude** — host's specific war story or canonical Michael moment. ≤ 90s. | Ad-lib within rails (canon §3.2 register) |
| 7:30 – 9:30 | **The longevity argument** — sober selling as competitive edge, not abstinence | Locked source from L11.1 |
| 9:30 – 11:00 | **Self-assessment moment** — host poses one question to the room without expecting an on-camera answer | Locked source from L11.1 |
| 11:00 – 12:00 | **Close + pointer to full curriculum** | Locked verbatim outro |

**Total: 12 minutes target, 10–15 minute hard window.**

The clip is built from L11.1's source recording with light editorial cuts — pause-trims, false-start removal. **No B-roll inserts** beyond a single text title card and a single text closing card. The clip is **not** re-shot. Per canon §16 + §7.2, the clip is "host ad-lib that becomes a recorded asset" — and the host's natural delivery is what carries the brand. Re-shooting for "production polish" introduces artificiality the brand cannot afford.

## 5. Audio + visual production discipline

### 5.1 Audio
- Host audio at `-16 LUFS` integrated, `-1 dB true peak`. Plain spec, broadcast-adjacent, comfortable on phone speakers and good headphones equally.
- No music bed under the host. Per canon §6.5, music-as-tone-control is the SaaS-norm move we counter-position against.
- One-frame fade-in at clip start, one-frame fade-out at clip end. No swooshes, no whooshes, no riser stings.

### 5.2 Visual
- Camera framing: medium close-up, eyes upper-third, host facing camera on neutral cream-toned backdrop (canon §6.1 palette extension). No virtual background, no Zoom-blur, no logo-watermark loop.
- Cuts: minimal. The clip's editorial style is the lesson register — pause-respecting, conversational-but-direct, no "engaging cut every 3 seconds" energy.
- Captions burned in or available as SRT — accessibility non-negotiable.
- **No framework-slide inserts.** The clip is host-on-camera throughout, with title card at open and close card at end. L11 doesn't carry a framework diagram; respecting that source-material truth keeps the preview honest. Any text-on-screen during the clip is captioning only.

### 5.3 Forbidden production moves
- B-roll stock footage of "office life" (handshakes, laptop closeups, sunset cityscapes, generic bar shots, cocktails). Forbidden per canon §6.5 + §6.9.1 (thumbnail test). Especially forbidden in this clip: any visual that depicts alcohol as imagery — the lesson treats the subject as operating doctrine, not aesthetic.
- Animated infographics for "engagement." Per canon §6.5 — icons must read as custom AESDR; SaaS-norm infographic libraries fail this immediately.
- Subscriber-callout end card ("subscribe for more!"). Canonical AESDR doesn't run a content engine; the close points to the offer, not to a YouTube subscribe button.
- Music swell at the close. The verdict-shaped outro carries on its own.
- "[HOST_FIRST_NAME]'s top 3 tips for staying sober" thumbnail framing. Per canon §1.5, AESDR is operating manual, not listicle content.
- Any depiction of the host drinking or refusing a drink. The lesson treats the subject; the clip is not a personal-disclosure document about the host.

## 6. Transcript spec

The transcript is **verbatim**, not paraphrased. Includes spoken words and host pauses (marked `[pause]`). Per canon §13, the transcript is the lesson on the page; if the host stumbled, the stumble is in the transcript.

### 6.1 Format

Markdown source renders to PDF (canon §8.5 PDF anatomy):
- Cream background, 24mm margins, mono eyebrow `AESDR · 658.85 / L11 · "THE COST OF BEING ON" · TRANSCRIPT v1`.
- `--display` italic 36pt headline: *The Cost of Being On — Lesson Preview Transcript.*
- `--serif` 14pt body, line-height 1.6.
- Timecodes in `--mono` 11pt `--muted` at the start of each speaker turn or pause.

### 6.2 Required transcript discipline

- **Word-for-word** including filler ("uh," "you know") on first instance, then optionally cleaned starting at the third instance per session if the editorial pass demands it. Default: keep all of it.
- **Time-code every speaker turn** at minimum every 30 seconds. Easier search, easier accessibility.
- **No editorial summarization in-line.** Section headers within the transcript are allowed (`## The territory`, `## The specific reveal`, `## The reality interlude`, `## The longevity argument`) but the body under each header is the host's words.

### 6.3 Required disclaimers

Top of transcript document:

> *Verbatim transcript of AESDR's canonical "The Cost of Being On" preview clip ([CLIP_DURATION_MINUTES] min), drawn from `658.85 / L11` source material. Timecodes reference the source video. This transcript is part of the AESDR lesson preview asset and may be lifted in full or in pull-quote form for partner promotion, with attribution to AESDR and disclosure per canon §10.1.*

## 7. Takeaway block

A three-bullet block embeddable into newsletters, podcast notes, and the D26 partner-promo page outcomes-block adjacency. **The block does not say "alcohol" or "sober selling"** — same discipline as the title card. It alludes to the territory; the clip itself names the subject.

> *Three takeaways from AESDR's canonical lesson preview, "The Cost of Being On":*
>
> *— **The operating cost of "always-on."** The reps who burn out aren't the ones who don't try hard. They're the ones who don't have a plan for the part of the job that happens after the workday ends.*
>
> *— **The reps who get promoted are the ones still sharp on Friday afternoon.** Not the ones closing out the bar Thursday night. The math compounds in one direction; you choose which.*
>
> *— **Sales-culture pressure is real and most courses pretend it isn't.** AESDR doesn't. Lesson 11 in the catalog spends a full 150 minutes on what the lesson titles call out plainly: 21+. Not metaphor.*

The third bullet is the only place the annotation surfaces in the takeaway block. By the third bullet, the partner audience has earned the reveal. The block is partner-liftable verbatim. Partners may add a one-sentence prefix in their own voice ("This is the part that landed for me:" or "From AESDR's preview:"), but they cannot edit the bullets themselves per canon §16 + D40 §3 approval workflow.

## 8. Approval gate (canon §16 + §7.2)

Per canon §7.2 host casting profile + §16 approval gates, **a host ad-lib that becomes a recorded asset** is one of the five approval-gated surfaces. The "The Cost of Being On" preview clip is a recorded asset by definition. Approval discipline:

1. **Source recording approval.** Before the L11.1 source recording is treated as the basis for the canonical preview, the founder approves the host's delivery against the §3 structural arc above. If a beat doesn't land, re-record before edit. The subject matter (alcohol, sales-culture pressure) is sensitive enough that the founder reviews tone for honesty-without-preaching before the cut begins.
2. **Cut approval.** The 12-minute editorial cut is reviewed by founder before the file is shipped to the partner kit folder. Iteration cycle: one round of named edits, then approve or re-record.
3. **Partner co-branding variant approval.** Each partner who lifts the clip with their lockup gets a static-thumbnail render approved per canon §6.6.

Per canon §6.9.3, **first instance of any new layout pattern: founder pre-approval before first use.** The lesson-preview clip's title card extends the editorial-split pattern from D09 slide 01 — same pattern, new context. Per the §6.9.3 reading, that's pattern-extension, not new pattern, so no separate pre-approval gate beyond the §1 source-recording approval above.

## Visual treatment notes

**Layout pattern:**
- **Title card / static thumbnail:** editorial split per canon §6.3 + §8.6 (deck title-card pattern). Crimson left, cream right, ghost numeral, AESDR wordmark.
- **Body of clip:** host-on-camera throughout. No framework slides, no diagrams. The lesson source is itself host-on-camera.
- **Transcript PDF:** canon §8.5 PDF anatomy.
- **Takeaway block:** content-only; the block embeds into surfaces designed elsewhere (D26, partner newsletters).

**Palette:**
- Title card: `--cream` right panel, `--crimson` left panel, white type on crimson, `--ink` on cream.
- Transcript PDF: `--cream` background, `--ink` body, `--muted` for timecodes.
- Takeaway block: inherits surface (cream when on D26).
- No other tokens used.

**Type tokens:**
- Title card: `--display` italic (700, 56pt for working title; 900, 64pt for wordmark), `--cond` (11pt mono eyebrow; 13pt hosted-by line), `--serif` italic (16pt context line), `--mono` (10pt subtag).
- Transcript: `--display` italic 36pt headline, `--serif` 14pt body, `--mono` 11pt timecodes.
- Takeaway block: `--serif` 16–17pt body, `--display` italic 700 18pt for bold-anchor terms.

**Iconography:** Per canon §6.8 — seed-inventory ghost numerals on title card. No imported icons. No emoji in the takeaway block. **No alcohol imagery anywhere.** The clip's restraint on visual depiction of the subject is a brand discipline choice; the lesson treats the subject seriously, not as aesthetic.

**Iris usage:** None on the title card or thumbnail. Per canon §6.4, iris is reserved for primary CTAs — and the preview clip does not carry a CTA on its visual surface. The CTA in §3.1 outro is *spoken* by the host; the URL is a lower-third overlay in `--mono` `--ink`, not iris.

**Deliberate departures from canon:** None. The decision to draw from L11 (the most envelope-pushing lesson) rather than a more conventional lesson is consistent with canon §1.5 (operator-over-guru) + §1.6 (honesty differentiator) + canon's overall "what we say competitors won't" position.

**Five-question check (canon §6.9.1):**
1. **Thumbnail at 200×200:** Title-card editorial split + AESDR wordmark + ghost numeral 11 + display italic working title = identifiably AESDR. The working title's restraint (no "alcohol," no "sober," no shock-language) means the thumbnail reads as serious and editorial, not as tabloid. Pass.
2. **Token check:** Pass.
3. **Iris reservation:** Pass — zero iris on the clip's visual surfaces.
4. **Icon discipline:** Pass — type-only on the title card; no imported icons or emoji; no subject-matter imagery.
5. **Voice thumbnail:** *"The operating cost of 'always-on.' The reps who burn out aren't the ones who don't try hard — they're the ones who don't have a plan for the part of the job that happens after the workday ends."* — passes; verdict-shaped Rowan, identifiably AESDR. The "after the workday ends" framing is the canonical canon §1.5 operator-over-guru move.

## Open

- **Whether to ship a 60-second cut** alongside the 12-minute clip for social-platform-native distribution. Default: **not in v1.** Short-form social cuts encourage the SaaS-content-engine motion AESDR explicitly counter-positions against (canon §1.1 + §1.5). If pilot data shows partners are clipping their own 60-second versions and the cuts drift off-brand or off-tone (especially given L11's subject sensitivity), ship a canonical 60-second cut to anchor the pattern.
- **Whether to make the transcript searchable as a standalone web page** at `aesdr.com/preview` or `aesdr.com/the-cost-of-being-on`. Default: **not in v1.** A searchable transcript page becomes worthwhile once organic search starts contributing meaningful registration volume — pre-pilot, it's premature.
- **Per-pilot host headshot vs canonical host frame.** Default: **canonical frame.** Per canon §7.2, the host's job is to carry the brand, not their personal brand. A single canonical frame across pilots reads more operating-manual; per-pilot variation reads more guru-content.
- **Host stumble policy in the cut.** Default: **light editorial trim only — false starts cut, mid-sentence resets cut, but pauses kept.** Per canon §13, the verbatim transcript catches what's edited out; the cut respects the host's natural cadence.
- **Subject-sensitivity review.** Because L11 treats alcohol head-on, the founder review of the cut should also verify the clip does not inadvertently moralize, preach, or shame. The lesson's stance is *operating manual*, not *self-help intervention*. Recommend founder explicitly notes "tone passes the §1.5 operator-not-guru test" at cut approval.
- **Captions language.** Default: English only in v1. Reconsider once non-Anglophone partner enters the pipeline.
