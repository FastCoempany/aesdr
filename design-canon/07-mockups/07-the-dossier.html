AESDR Program Dossier — Curriculum Overview
REF: AESDR-2026-FOUO • DISTRIBUTION: ENROLLED ONLY • 12 ENTRIES
ENTRY 01
You will learn the framework that before you learn anything else. This is not about sales. This is about the operating system you run your life on.
I thought this was going to be about cold calls. It was about why I eat cereal for dinner three nights a week. Connected? Apparently yes.
ENTRY 02
The room you work in is either accelerating you or. Your apartment, your desk, your lighting — it is not decoration. It is infrastructure.
I was dialing from my bed. In sweatpants. Next to a pile of laundry I'd named Gerald. We had a whole relationship. Gerald is gone now.
ENTRY 03
There is a person standing between you and your number. That person is. And that person's ego has been lying to them since.
Spoiler: it's not your manager. It's not your AE. It's the version of you that thinks "potential" is a personality trait.
ENTRY 04
The drink in your hand at the conference is telling everyone exactly. You are being watched when you think you are relaxing. Every room is a stage.
I once did four tequila shots at a company SKO and told the VP of Sales my "real thoughts." I no longer work at that company. Unrelated. Completely related.
ENTRY 05
Your commission check is not income. It is a confession of how you. One month you're rich. The next you're doing math at the grocery store.
I bought a $300 jacket after a big month. Then I ate ramen for three weeks. The jacket looked great with the ramen stains though.
ENTRY 06
The person waiting at home while you "grind" is keeping score whether you. This job will cost you relationships you didn't know were.
My girlfriend asked me what I did all day. I said "pipeline." She said "that's not a real answer." She was right. We broke up. Also unrelated. Also completely related.
ENTRIES 07 – 12
The remaining entries are restricted to enrolled participants. These lessons cover company politics, the math behind, networking that actually works, and whether this career is even.
Lessons 7-12 are where it stops being a sales course and starts being a life intervention. I cried during one of them. At my desk. On camera. During a Zoom. I don't want to talk about it.
