Two voices. One program. 12 lessons.

Every great rep has two people inside them.

One commands the room. The other eats cereal over the sink at midnight wondering if SaaS was a good idea. We train both.

The Voice That Closes
01
Your apartment is your first sale. If you can't close your own environment, you cannot close anyone else's.
The space you operate from is not decoration. It is infrastructure.
02
The drink in your hand at the conference is a press release. Everyone is reading it.
You are being evaluated in every room you think you're relaxing in.
03
Your commission check is not income. It is a verdict on how you lived the last 30 days.
Feast-or-famine is not a market condition. It is a character trait you can fix.
04
The person at home keeping score while you "grind" will not wait forever. Respect the math.
This career will cost you relationships unless you learn which ones to protect and how.
05
"Potential" is not a skill. It is a word people use to describe someone who has not done anything yet.
Stop auditioning. Start performing. The two are not the same.
06
You did not choose this career. But you will choose who you become inside it. That decision is the curriculum.
We do not teach you to sell. We teach you to be the person who sells.
The Voice at 2am
01
My "home office" is my bed. The desk is a folding table from Target. There's a pile of laundry on the chair I named Gerald. Gerald is my coworker now. He's quiet. Great listener.
02
I had four drinks at the company SKO and told the CEO I could "definitely" run the enterprise segment. He asked me what ARR stood for. I said "Absolutely Ready to Rock." I was not ready to rock.
03
Last month I made $8,200. This month I made $2,100. I bought a $300 jacket during the $8,200 month. I'm now wearing the $300 jacket while eating ramen I bought with a coupon. Fashion.
04
My girlfriend asked what I actually do all day. I said "pipeline." She said "that's not a real answer." She was right. We broke up. I still don't have a better answer.
05
My manager said I have "so much potential." That was six months ago. I still have the same amount of potential. Like, exactly the same amount. It hasn't converted into anything. It's just... sitting there. Being potential.
06
I didn't choose SaaS sales. I applied to 47 jobs and this one called back first. Like a stray cat that pays commission. Sometimes. On a delayed schedule. With clawbacks.

This is not a sales course. It's an intervention for the whole person.

Your pipeline, your paycheck, your apartment, your relationships, your conference behavior, your identity, your future. 12 lessons. All of it. The life stuff IS the sales stuff.

I came in as Voice #2. I left as Voice #1 who still remembers what the ramen tasted like. That's the whole thing.
