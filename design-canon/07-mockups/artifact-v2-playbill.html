Act III
Director's Notes
Between performances. The stage manager's notebook, the blocking changes, the rewrites the lead must learn before next curtain.
For the cast of Jordan Rivera —
The run was strong. The upper half of the program delivers. The reviews for the lower half, however, are not going away on their own. We rewrite in four places before the next performance. Everything below is binding.
Act VI · Networking
highest priority
New blocking: one real conversation per week.
Not a connection request. A conversation — an exchange, a question asked, a comment left that is not about yourself. The network is built in the measures the audience does not see. Build it before you need it, or the last act plays to an empty house.
"Cross downstage left. Speak to the person who is not a prospect."
Act III · Coaching
recurring
Friday scene: ask for the feedback, specifically.
Every Friday, ask the AE for one piece of feedback. Cite the exact call, the exact email, the exact moment. "Open" reads as passive on stage. We need seeking. We need the initiative.
"Turn to face the mentor. Ask the question without apologizing for asking."
Act IX · Discipline
daily
Opening scene rewritten: 8:00–9:30 is prospecting.
Phone on DND. Slack closed. Email closed. Calendar blocked, defended, protected. Every day. The meeting comes second — always. The day starts on offense or it does not start at all.
"Lights up. Actor alone on stage with the phone. Other characters do not enter until 9:30."
Act VII · Pipeline
every performance
All four BANT criteria. Every lead. Non-negotiable.
Before the lead gets passed — budget, authority, need, timeline. If you don't know one, ask the hard question. Hope is not a line in this script anymore. Confidence comes from completeness, not from instinct.
"Four beats before the hand-off. Hit all four or don't cross the line."
— J. R.
Director, producer, lead. Accountable to all three.
