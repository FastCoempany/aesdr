The IDK Framework

What to say when you don't know the answer on a call

★

The Golden Rule

Never fake an answer. A wrong answer destroys trust permanently. "I don't know" said correctly builds more credibility than a shaky guess.

1.

Identify — Name what you don't know

Acknowledge the gap directly. Don't hedge, don't deflect. Say: "That's a great question — I don't have that specific data point in front of me."

"I want to give you the accurate number, not a guess."

2.

Defer — Commit to a follow-up

Give a specific timeline for when you'll have the answer. Vague promises ("I'll get back to you") are trust-killers.

"I'll have our solutions engineer confirm this and send you the detail by end of day tomorrow."

Always give a deadline. "Soon" is not a deadline.
3.

Keep Control — Pivot back to your strengths

Bridge to something you do know. Don't let the gap derail momentum. Transition with value:

"While I get that confirmed — what I can tell you now is how [related thing] works for teams like yours..."

The pivot is where amateurs lose the call. Pros bridge.
