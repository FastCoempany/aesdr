## Questions

### Q1: It's Monday morning. Your manager pings you with a "quick sync." What's your gut reaction?

A) "Here we go again — another meeting that could've been a Slack message." → Burnout  
B) "Cool, let me prep my numbers real quick." → Operator  
C) "I wonder what I did wrong this time." → Survivor  
D) "I'll use this to pitch my new outbound strategy." → Climber  

### Q2: You just missed quota for the second month in a row. What do you do first?

A) Update your LinkedIn headline and start applying. → Burnout  
B) Audit your pipeline stage by stage to find the leak. → Operator  
C) Hope nobody notices and grind harder next month. → Survivor  
D) Book time with your skip-level to discuss what support you need. → Climber  

### Q3: A prospect ghosts you after a great demo. Your next move?

A) Mark them as lost and move on — plenty of fish. → Burnout  
B) Send a structured breakup email with a clear next step. → Operator  
C) Send three more follow-ups this week, each slightly more desperate. → Survivor  
D) Ask your AE to loop in their VP for a different angle. → Climber  

### Q4: Your CRM has 200+ open contacts. How many have a real next step scheduled?

A) Maybe 10? I don't really use the CRM that way. → Burnout  
B) 60-70%. I clean my pipeline every Friday. → Operator  
C) I honestly don't know. I just call the list. → Survivor  
D) All of them — and I tag them by buying signal strength. → Climber  

### Q5: Your manager asks you to role-play a cold call in front of the team. Your reaction?

A) "I'd rather eat glass." → Burnout  
B) "Sure, can I pick the scenario?" → Operator  
C) "Okay..." *sweats internally* → Survivor  
D) "Can I go first? I've been testing a new opener." → Climber  

### Q6: How do you feel about your current commission structure?

A) "It's rigged. The accelerators only kick in at numbers nobody hits." → Burnout  
B) "I understand it well enough to reverse-engineer my monthly target." → Operator  
C) "I don't fully understand it, but the checks seem about right." → Survivor  
D) "I've modeled it in a spreadsheet. I know exactly what each deal is worth to me." → Climber  

### Q7: A new SDR joins your team and asks for advice. What do you say?

A) "Run." → Burnout  
B) "Block your calendar. Protect your prospecting time or meetings will eat you alive." → Operator  
C) "Just do what your manager says and you'll be fine." → Survivor  
D) "Let me share the playbook I built — it's not perfect but it's real." → Climber  

### Q8: You get a LinkedIn message from a sales "guru" offering a masterclass. Your reaction?

A) "Another one." *delete* → Burnout  
B) "I'll check if they have real quota-carrying experience." → Operator  
C) "Maybe I should sign up? I need help." → Survivor  
D) "I'll scan it for one usable tactic, then ignore the rest." → Climber  

### Q9: Your biggest deal this quarter just pushed to next quarter. What do you do?

A) Mentally check out for the rest of the month. → Burnout  
B) Adjust forecast, pull two mid-stage deals forward, communicate the change to my manager. → Operator  
C) Panic. Call the prospect five times. → Survivor  
D) Use it as leverage: "I need X resource to prevent this pattern next quarter." → Climber  

### Q10: It's Friday at 5pm. You're at 94% of quota. There's one call left to make. What do you do?

A) "94% is basically 100%. I'm out." → Burnout  
B) Make the call. 94% is not 100%. → Operator  
C) Stare at the phone for 20 minutes, then leave. → Survivor  
D) Make the call AND send three follow-ups for next week before I leave. → Climber

## Scoring

- **Mostly A's → The Burnout**: You're talented but running on fumes. The job hasn't broken your skills — it's broken your give-a-damn. You need a reset, not another pep talk.
- **Mostly B's → The Operator**: You're solid, consistent, and reliable. You hit quota more often than not. But you might be plateauing because you've optimized the wrong things.
- **Mostly C's → The Survivor**: You're getting through each day, but you're reactive instead of proactive. You have potential that's being buried under bad habits and bad management.
- **Mostly D's → The Climber**: You're playing the long game. You see the job as a stepping stone, not a sentence. You just need sharper tools and a framework to accelerate.
