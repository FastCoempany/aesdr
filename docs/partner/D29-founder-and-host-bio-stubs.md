# D29 — Founder & Host Bio Stubs

**Deliverable:** Three canonical bio stubs at four lengths each — the standardized bios used everywhere AESDR or its representatives are introduced. The document the partner consults when the host needs to be introduced on a podcast, when the founder needs a one-liner for a partner agreement, or when the Admissions alias needs to identify itself in a high-intent reply. One source of truth, no improvising on intros.
**Audience:** Three principal-level bios produced here:
- **Host** — audience-facing. Used on the registration page (D26 §3b "hosted by"), the replay page (D24 §2), the workshop deck title (D09 slide 01), the FAQ (D23 Q10), podcast interviews where the host appears as guest, and partner co-branded surfaces.
- **Founder** — partner-facing only. Per canon §12.1, the founder is invisible to audience; this bio appears only on the partner-vetting call brief (D27 prep), in D22 signatures, and in the partner-kit README (D40). Never on a public surface AESDR controls.
- **Admissions alias** — used by the founder when answering high-intent inbound (per canon §12.3). The alias has its own bio stub for the LinkedIn account, the email signature, and the calendar landing page. Same person as founder; different role-of-record presented to the audience.
**Voice ratio:** Host bio runs 80 Rowan / 20 Michael (canon §3.3 — closest to the workshop registration page register). Founder bio runs 90/10 (internal docs / partner-adjacent register). Admissions alias bio runs 100/0 plain (the alias does not perform voice — its honesty is the move per canon §12.3).
**Format:** Markdown source. Renders to (1) embeddable HTML blocks on registration / replay / FAQ / partner-promo pages, (2) a 1-page PDF in the partner kit (D40 file `07-host-bio-stubs.pdf`), (3) plain-text blocks copyable into podcast-host email replies, AESDR-controlled LinkedIn profiles, and AESDR's own about page if/when one ships.
**Canon refs:** `AFFILIATE_BRAND_CANON.md` §1.5 (operator over guru), §3.3 (voice ratios), §7.2 (host casting profile), §12 (founder-backstage doctrine), §12.1 (visibility matrix), §12.3 (Admissions alias), §13 (honesty discipline).

> **Placeholder convention:** `[HOST_FIRST_NAME]`, `[HOST_LAST_NAME]`, `[HOST_TENURE_YEARS]`, `[HOST_BACKGROUND_BEAT]` (one-line career summary), `[HOST_PRIOR_ROLES]` (comma-separated, e.g., "SDR at [co]; AE at [co]; Sales Manager at [co]"). `[FOUNDER_FIRST_NAME]`, `[FOUNDER_LAST_NAME]`, `[FOUNDER_BACKGROUND_BEAT]`, `[FOUNDER_PRIOR_ROLES]`. `[ALIAS_FIRST_NAME]` if the Admissions alias is given a fictional first name (default: stay role-only as `Admissions`). Swap globally before render. **All host placeholders are pending host casting** (canon §17 outstanding question 1, 4); founder placeholders are pending founder confirmation of public-name disclosure scope.

> **Stub-vs-bio distinction:** these are *stubs* — short, factual, written-once. They are not author-style bios with personal flourishes. The discipline is per canon §1.5 (operator over guru) and §7.2 (host casting profile — host carries the brand, not their personal brand). When the host's tenure increases or the founder's company evolves, these stubs update — the rest of the kit pulls from here.

## The three bios at four lengths

Each bio exists at four lengths, picked by surface. The same content compressed; the longest version is canon, every shorter version is a derivation.

| Length | Word count | Where used |
|---|---|---|
| **L** — long | 90–120 | Partner kit PDF, `/about` page if/when shipped, full FAQ Q10 (host only) |
| **M** — medium | 50–70 | Registration page §3b, replay page §2, partner-promo page §3b, founder bio in D22 §1 |
| **S** — short | 25–35 | Workshop deck slide 01 hosted-by line, podcast intro scripts, email signature blocks |
| **XS** — micro | 10–15 | LinkedIn headline, calendar landing page subhead, "hosted by [HOST] for AESDR" anchor lines |

## Bio 1 — Host

**Audience visibility:** Full. The host carries the brand publicly per canon §7.2. The host's name is on the registration page, the deck, the replay page, the email signatures of D13, and the FAQ.

### Host — L (long)

> *[HOST_FIRST_NAME] [HOST_LAST_NAME] hosts the AESDR program. [HOST_TENURE_YEARS] years in startup SaaS sales — through the SDR seat, the AE seat, and a sales-management chair. [HOST_BACKGROUND_BEAT]. [HOST_FIRST_NAME] has missed quota at least once and can talk about it; that's the prerequisite, not a footnote. The host's job at AESDR is to deliver the curriculum, not to sell their personal brand — the brand is the operating standard the lessons encode, and AESDR was built to outlast any one host.*
>
> *Approach: declarative, surgical, low-on-hype. The voice that closes (canon §3.1) — applied to teaching.*

### Host — M (medium)

> *[HOST_FIRST_NAME] [HOST_LAST_NAME] hosts the AESDR program. [HOST_TENURE_YEARS] years in startup SaaS sales across SDR, AE, and sales-management seats. [HOST_BACKGROUND_BEAT]. The host's job is to deliver the curriculum — AESDR is the operating standard, not a personal brand.*

### Host — S (short)

> *[HOST_FIRST_NAME] [HOST_LAST_NAME] — [HOST_TENURE_YEARS] years in startup SaaS sales (SDR → AE → sales management). Hosts the AESDR program.*

### Host — XS (micro)

> *[HOST_FIRST_NAME] [HOST_LAST_NAME], host of AESDR.*

### Host — what does NOT belong in any version

- LinkedIn-influencer credential lists ("speaker," "thought leader," "advisor to 50+ startups").
- Total-deals-closed numbers, total-revenue numbers, total-comp numbers.
- "Crushing quota" / "blew through ramp" / any banned-vocab move (canon §4.1).
- "[HOST_FIRST_NAME]'s mission is to..." energy. The host has a job; not a mission.
- Awards, podcasts hosted, books written, Forbes-30-under-30 mentions.
- Any superlative not earned by named specifics.

The bio reads short, plain, operator-to-operator. Per canon §7.2, the host is hired to *carry* the brand — and the bio doing too much work would be a sign the host isn't carrying their share.

## Bio 2 — Founder

**Audience visibility:** None. Partners-and-operators visibility: full. Per canon §12.1, the founder is invisible to audience by default. This bio surfaces only where partners read.

### Founder — L (long)

> *[FOUNDER_FIRST_NAME] [FOUNDER_LAST_NAME] is the founder of AESDR. [FOUNDER_BACKGROUND_BEAT]. AESDR exists because the operating manual the founder needed in their first SaaS sales role didn't exist — and because, after running the role and watching peers wash out of it, the founder found that "what good actually looks like" was a question nobody on the inside could answer with a curriculum.*
>
> *The founder operates AESDR backstage by canon doctrine (§12). Workshop attendees do not see the founder; partners do. High-intent post-workshop conversations route through Admissions, where the founder takes the call as principal, not as founder-of-record. The brand-of-record speaks publicly; the founder doesn't.*

### Founder — M (medium)

> *[FOUNDER_FIRST_NAME] [FOUNDER_LAST_NAME] founded AESDR. [FOUNDER_BACKGROUND_BEAT]. AESDR exists because the operating manual the founder needed in their first SaaS sales role didn't exist. Per canon doctrine, the founder is partner-visible and audience-invisible.*

### Founder — S (short)

> *[FOUNDER_FIRST_NAME] [FOUNDER_LAST_NAME] — founder of AESDR. [FOUNDER_PRIOR_ROLES_SUMMARY_ONE_LINE]. Partner-visible, audience-invisible by canon doctrine.*

### Founder — XS (micro)

> *[FOUNDER_FIRST_NAME] [FOUNDER_LAST_NAME], founder, AESDR.*

### Founder — what does NOT belong in any version

- Anything that reads as personal-brand marketing. The founder is not building a personal brand on AESDR.
- Quote-style "founder mission" statements.
- Photographs in any AESDR-controlled surface that's audience-facing. Per canon §12.1.
- Forbes / Inc / "Featured in" credibility lists. We earn credibility through the curriculum, not through media credits.
- Tenure-as-credential ("20-year sales veteran"). Either the operating-manual works for the buyer or it doesn't; tenure is a context, not a sale.

The founder bio's job is to confirm that there's a real person operating AESDR, with relevant context, and to clear the path for the partner conversation. It is not a closing surface.

## Bio 3 — Admissions alias

**Audience visibility:** Aliased. Per canon §12.3, the founder takes high-intent inbound as `[ALIAS_FIRST_NAME]` from AESDR Admissions. Same person, different presented role. The alias does not lie — anonymity is positioning, not deception. If a lead asks "are you the founder?", the answer is *"I'm the principal at AESDR."*

### Admissions — L (long)

> *Admissions is the role of record for AESDR's high-intent inbound — the conversations that come up after a workshop, after a checkout-page back-button, after a question in chat that the host couldn't answer in the time available. Admissions takes those conversations directly: short answers, real replies, no calendar-link autoresponder.*
>
> *Admissions is staffed by AESDR's principal. Per canon doctrine (§12.3), anonymity is a positioning choice, not deception. If a prospect asks the obvious question — are you the founder? — the answer is honest: "I'm the principal at AESDR." The alias separates the role from the person; the role is what the conversation is about.*

### Admissions — M (medium)

> *Admissions is AESDR's role of record for high-intent inbound — post-workshop questions, checkout-page back-buttons, replies to follow-up emails. Staffed by AESDR's principal; takes the conversation directly. No calendar-link autoresponders.*

### Admissions — S (short)

> *AESDR Admissions — staffed by the principal. Takes high-intent inbound directly.*

### Admissions — XS (micro)

> *AESDR Admissions.*

### Admissions — what does NOT belong in any version

- A made-up biographical history. The alias is a role, not a fictional character.
- Photo of a stock-photo person on the LinkedIn account or calendar page. Default: AESDR wordmark or initials in iris on cream.
- Phrases that imply Admissions is a team larger than one ("our team" — singular when accurate).
- "Founder" anywhere in the public Admissions footprint. The alias separates the roles by canon design.

If the Admissions alias is given a fictional first name (default: skip; stay role-only as `Admissions`), the name must not match a real person at AESDR-adjacent companies. Per canon §13, anonymity is positioning; deception is not.

## Visual treatment notes

**Layout pattern:** Bio stubs are content, not layout. They render *into* surfaces designed elsewhere (D26, D24, D23, D40). The single visual surface this document ships is the **partner-kit PDF** (D40 file `07-host-bio-stubs.pdf`) — a 1–2 page PDF carrying the L-version of all three bios, plus a Surface-by-surface table.

For the PDF render:

**Palette:**
- Background: `--cream`.
- Type: `--ink` body.
- Bio headers (`HOST`, `FOUNDER`, `ADMISSIONS`): `--display` italic 700, 32pt.
- Length labels (`L`, `M`, `S`, `XS`): `--mono` 11pt 700, .15em, uppercase, `--muted`.
- Bio body: `--serif` 16pt, line-height 1.6.
- Mono eyebrow + footer: `--mono` 10–11pt, `--muted`.
- Hairline rules between bios: `--light` 1px.

**Type tokens:** Per palette above. No Caveat — bios are operating-doctrine register; Michael is reserved for audience-facing voice surfaces (canon §3.4).

**Iconography:** None. Bios are type-only. No headshot frames, no founder photo placeholder block — per canon §12.1, founder photo absence is canon. Host headshot policy is set by the partner co-brand zone (canon §6.6) and lives in `10c-lockup-on-cream.png` of the kit, not in the bio document.

**Iris usage:** None. Bio stubs are reference content; iris is reserved for primary-CTA surfaces (canon §6.4).

**Deliberate departures from canon:** None. The audience-facing absence of the founder bio in `/about`, registration, replay, and FAQ surfaces is *itself* canon (canon §12.1). The Surface-by-surface table above documents it explicitly so partners and ops know what's intentional.

## Approval discipline (canon §16)

- **First instance** of any bio variant being published on a new surface (e.g., the first time the M-length host bio appears on a podcast site): founder pre-approval before publish.
- **Material edits** to any L-version bio: canon-revision-style PR per canon §17 — open a PR titled `bio: [host/founder/admissions]`, update the content, ripple shorter variants to match.
- **Minor edits** (typo, tenure update, a new prior role added): same workflow but expedited; founder approves over email.
- **Partner re-paraphrasing of bios** in their own promotional copy: forbidden per D40 §3 approval workflow. Partners lift verbatim or submit alternative copy for approval.

## Open

- **Whether the host bio mentions specific company names** the host worked at. Default: **yes for L**, **paraphrased for M and below**. The L variant is the canonical truth document; shorter variants compress. If the host's prior companies are recognizable, the L-bio benefits; if obscure, paraphrase to "early-stage SaaS startups."
- **Whether to ship a "host as guest" 30-second intro audio recording** alongside the bio stubs. Default: **not in v1.** A recorded intro is a podcast-side asset, partner-podcast-specific, and overkill for the partner-kit pin. Reconsider once 3+ partner podcast appearances are scheduled.
- **Whether the Admissions alias gets a real first name.** Default: **no — stay role-only.** A first-name-named Admissions alias risks reading as fake-personnel; a role-only "Admissions" reads as honest minimalism. Per canon §12.3, anonymity is positioning. Reconsider only if reply rates show prospects strongly prefer first-name personalization.
- **Whether to add a "Bio 4 — Brand of record"** for AESDR-the-entity (the abstract brand-of-record voice that signs `AESDR Workshop` on D10–D14, signs the footer of D26, signs the close of D09). Default: **not in v1.** "AESDR" is the brand; it doesn't need a biography. If a partner explicitly asks "who is AESDR?", the founder + host bios answer; an entity-bio would over-architect.
- **Whether the founder bio includes prior-company specifics**. Default: **paraphrased**. Per canon §12.1, founder backstage extends to public surfaces; partner-facing only earns the prior-company specificity in the L variant. Founder may override per-partner via the D27 conversation.
