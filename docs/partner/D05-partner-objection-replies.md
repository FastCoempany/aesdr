# D05 — Partner-Objection Reply Bank

**Deliverable:** 8 reply templates for the most common partner objections / counter-asks during cold outreach and pilot scoping.
**Voice ratio:** 80 Rowan / 20 Michael. Calm, declarative, never defensive.
**Sender:** Founder, by name. Always.
**Length target:** 60–90 words each.
**Canon refs:** `AFFILIATE_BRAND_CANON.md` §3, §6.6, §10, §13. Pricing/refund canon §2.

## Discipline

- Never argue with an objection. Acknowledge it, reframe it, propose the next step.
- Never grovel. Never over-explain. Never defend by listing features.
- Each reply earns the same `we part as adults` exit if the answer's still no.
- Brackets `[…]` are required edits per send — never leave them.

## 1. "Can you just send a flat fee instead of revenue share?"

> [Name] —
>
> Default for first pilots is commission-only, no fixed fee. Two reasons: it keeps incentives clean (we both want sales, not signups), and it lets us extend on real numbers instead of debating who paid for what.
>
> Happy to revisit a fixed-against-commission structure once we have one pilot's data to anchor it. If now's the wrong time, that's fine — we can park this and revisit in a quarter.
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

## 2. "What's the exclusivity situation?"

> [Name] —
>
> No exclusivity by default. The pilot is non-exclusive and time-boxed at 30 days; nothing in the agreement keeps you from running other partnerships in parallel.
>
> If we hit clear thresholds, I'm open to a 30-day segment-specific exclusivity (e.g., "no other early-career SaaS sales course inside [community] for 30 days") — but that's earned, not assumed. Pre-revenue isn't the right time for long lock-ins.
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

## 4. "Can I share my list / audience data with you?"

> [Name] —
>
> Short answer: probably not, and definitely not without your audience's specific consent.
>
> The pilot uses partner-specific UTMs and a partner-promo registration page, so we attribute by source without sharing PII either direction. Anyone who registers gives consent to AESDR directly on the form. Cleaner for compliance, cleaner for trust, cleaner for you.
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

## 5. "We already booked another sales course this quarter."

> [Name] —
>
> Got it — and not a problem. AESDR is built for the first 1–2 years specifically; if your audience has just consumed something similar, the timing's probably wrong now anyway.
>
> If you'd like, I'll park this and check back when your members are ready for the next thing. No follow-up cadence, no automation — just a real ping when the timing fits.
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

## 6. "We do affiliate empires here — big roster, big volume."

> [Name] —
>
> Honest answer: AESDR's not built for that motion right now. We're running a small, founder-led pilot — handful of partners, workshop-first, real attribution, real conversation. Less affiliate empire, more founding vineyard.
>
> If your audience is a tight ICP fit and you're open to a one-off pilot inside an otherwise high-volume program, happy to scope it. If the volume model is the only model that makes sense for your operation, we're probably the wrong fit and I'd rather say so now than disappoint later.
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

## 7. "What's your refund / churn rate? What if my audience asks for refunds?"

> [Name] —
>
> Refunds: 14 days, no-questions-asked. Anyone who asks gets one within 3 business days. Commission is paid on **net** revenue after refunds, so a refund quietly reverses the commission instead of putting us in a recovery conversation.
>
> Refund rate is something we'll know honestly inside this pilot — happy to share what we see, the same week we see it. If your audience tends to be cautious buyers, the 14-day window is usually a feature, not a bug.
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

## 8. "Why now? Why this format?"

> [Name] —
>
> Two answers, one short, one honest.
>
> Short: the gap is real — early-career reps in startup SaaS aren't getting structured coaching, and most of what's online is motivation in a trench coat. AESDR is built for that gap specifically.
>
> Honest: I'd rather test one workshop into a real audience and learn from objections than spray a launch and call the silence "demand." Workshop-first is slower for a week and faster for a quarter. If [community] has the right ICP, the test costs us a session and gives us both real data.
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

## Notes

- #6 carries the canon tagline `Less affiliate empire, more founding vineyard.` — that's not decoration; it's a counter-positioning move that filters out the wrong kind of partner.
- #8 carries `motivation in a trench coat` — Michael's voice doing one beat of work inside an otherwise Rowan reply.
- #4 (data sharing) is partly compliance, partly trust signal. Operators with any regulatory awareness will respect the answer; ones without may push back. Hold the line.
- Every reply offers an honest exit. None of them try to win.
