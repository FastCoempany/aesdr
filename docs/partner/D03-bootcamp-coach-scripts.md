# D03 — Bootcamp-Coach Outreach Scripts

**Deliverable:** Three scripts for outreach to bootcamp coaches (operators of paid SDR/AE bootcamps and structured cohort programs).
**Voice ratio:** 80 Rowan / 20 Michael. Coach-to-coach. Slightly more deferential than community-operator scripts because coaches have direct teaching authority over their alumni.
**Sender:** Founder, by name.
**Length target:** 90–110 words each.
**Canon refs:** `AFFILIATE_BRAND_CANON.md` §3.3, §4, §12. Per the research report (§ICP and partner matrices), bootcamp coach is the **highest-fit** archetype — high conversion, lower volume, often the strongest partner overall.

## Use

- **Cold** — first message, no prior contact.
- **Warm** — they replied to a post, you've crossed paths in a coaching circle, mutual intro.
- **Reply-to-no-reply** — one bump.

Personalization: bootcamp / cohort name, one specific outcome they publish (placement rate, alumni testimonial, named graduate), and the founder's first name.

---

## 1. Cold — bootcamp coach

> Subject: After placement, before confident ramp
>
> [Name] —
>
> I run AESDR — a 12-lesson sales survival course for SDRs and AEs in their first 1–2 years of startup SaaS. It's built for the gap most bootcamp grads land in: placed, paid, and quietly drowning in their first quarter.
>
> [Bootcamp] keeps coming up when I look for coaches who actually stay close to alumni after placement. If useful, I'd rather co-host a short workshop for your cohort or alumni network than pitch you a generic affiliate arrangement. One live session, tracked attribution, clean disclosure language, straightforward rev share, no exclusivity.
>
> Worth a 20-minute call?
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

## 2. Warm — bootcamp coach

> Subject: Co-host a workshop for your alumni
>
> [Name] —
>
> [Specific signal — a quote from their cohort, a thread you saw, a graduate's post]. That gap — placed but unstructured — is exactly the gap AESDR was built to close in lessons 1–6.
>
> The pilot motion is workshop-first, not link-first: one live co-hosted session into your alumni or current cohort, real tracking, real numbers. You see the registration-to-purchase math the same week we do. If it's not working, we end it; if it is, we extend with a clean rev share.
>
> Open to a 20-minute scoping call this week or next?
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

## 3. Reply-to-no-reply — bootcamp coach

> Subject: Bumping once, then I'll go quiet
>
> [Name] —
>
> One bump on the AESDR co-hosted workshop note. Three reasons coaches I respect have said yes to this format:
>
> 1. It's non-exclusive and time-boxed (30 days).
> 2. The workshop teaches something real — not a sales-pitch-in-a-trench-coat.
> 3. We track attribution honestly; you see the math on the same dashboard we do.
>
> If now's wrong, no offense taken. Otherwise I'm around this week or next.
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

---

## Notes

- "Sales-pitch-in-a-trench-coat" (#3) is the one Michael-flavored line in the set — it's the brand-voice fingerprint that signals we know what bad partner workshops look like.
- Co-host is genuinely on offer for this archetype (per canon §7.1 and the research report). Don't promise it without meaning it.
- Bootcamp coaches will read every word of these. Avoid throat-clearing harder here than for any other archetype.
- Authority frame: AESDR was built by people who carried bags. Coaches respect carriers; they smell consultants from a mile away.

## Open

- Whether the co-host gets billed as `AESDR × [bootcamp]` for the session itself (per canon §6.6) or stays single-host for first pilots. Default: single-host workshop with the coach doing intro + Q&A moderation, lockup on registration page only.
- Whether AESDR offers a coach-only enablement layer (e.g., they get the `5 takeaway tools` free for their teaching reference) — useful sweetener, but check legal/cost first.
