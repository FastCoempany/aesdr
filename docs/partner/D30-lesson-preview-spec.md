# D30 — Lesson Preview Clip + Transcript Spec

**Deliverable:** Production specification for AESDR's canonical lesson-preview asset: one 10–15 minute clip from a single lesson, plus a verbatim transcript, plus three takeaway bullets. The asset partners can lift to give their audience a feel for the actual caliber of instruction. One canonical clip per lesson, produced once, used across every pilot.
**Audience:** Three audiences, same source asset, three render targets:
- **Partners** — embed the clip on their newsletter / Substack / podcast notes / member channel during the promotion window. Lifted from the partner kit (D40 file `08-lesson-preview-clip-and-transcript/`).
- **Prospects** — the clip appears on the workshop registration page (D26) above-the-fold for visitors who want a "what does the actual program feel like" pre-decision watch.
- **L&D approvers** — the transcript is attached to the L&D-approver brief PDF as the "see the actual material" exhibit.
**Voice ratio:** The clip *contains* AESDR voice in its native ratio (the lesson register — 75/25 Rowan/Michael per canon §3.3 workshop deck row; close enough). The *spec* itself runs 90/10 internal docs register.
**Format:** This document is the spec; the deliverable is a 10–15 min video file (MP4, 1080p, captioned), a transcript (markdown + PDF), and a three-bullet takeaway block (markdown, embeddable). Per canon §6.5 + §8.5.
**Canon refs:** `AFFILIATE_BRAND_CANON.md` §1.1 (workshop-first), §1.5 (operator over guru), §3.3 (voice ratios), §6.4 (iris reservation — applies to clip thumbnail), §6.6 (co-branding zone), §6.8 (custom iconography — for the clip's title card), §6.9 (visual QA), §7.2 (host casting profile), §13 (honesty discipline), §16 (approval gates — host ad-lib that becomes a recorded asset).

## Why one preview clip, not twelve

The temptation is to build a preview per lesson. Resist it. Per canon §1.1 (workshop-first, not link-first) and §1.5 (operator over guru): one clip, made well, is the asset. Twelve clips, made fast, are content. The partner audience needs to feel the operating-manual register once; they don't need a content library to browse.

**The chosen lesson is Lesson 04 — Pipeline Integrity.** Reasons in §2 below.

## 2. Why Lesson 04 — Pipeline Integrity

The canonical preview lesson is **Lesson 04 — Pipeline Integrity**, drawn from the Foundations cluster (D31). Selection rationale:

- **Legibility at 10 minutes.** Lessons 1, 2, 3 build the operating frame; their value compounds across the cluster. Lessons 5+ assume the foundation is in place. Lesson 4 sits at the junction — it has its own self-contained framework (forecasted vs actual pipeline, the integrity gap) that lands in 10 minutes without requiring earlier-lesson context.
- **Pain match.** Pipeline integrity is the operating problem most early-career SaaS reps recognize the second the framework names it. Per the affiliate-seeding research report ICP matrix, "the gap between activity and judgment" is the central pain across SDR, AE, and career-switcher personas. Pipeline integrity is the most legible expression of that gap.
- **Visual anchor.** The forecasted-vs-actual funnel diagram from D09 slide 11 is on-brand visual content (canon §6.3 white-panel-on-cream pattern), photographs cleanly at thumbnail size (canon §6.9.1 thumbnail test), and produces a single-frame social-share image when the partner clips a still.
- **Voice mix sample.** Lesson 04 carries Rowan-heavy framework narration with one Michael interlude (the "I sent a screenshot of an empty spreadsheet and wrote 'minimalist aesthetic'" anecdote, canonical from canon §3.2). The 75/25 ratio shows up correctly proportioned in the preview.
- **Honesty mode.** The lesson includes the canonical "your forecast accuracy was 25%, what you told your VP was 85%" beat — the kind of honest, named-number, plain-prose mode that distinguishes AESDR from motivational content. Per canon §13.

If pilot data shows a different lesson outperforms Lesson 04 as a preview, this spec gets a v2 — but **only one canonical lesson at a time.** Multiple parallel previews dilute the pattern.

## 3. Clip structure (the 10–15 minute arc)

The clip is **not** the full Lesson 04 video re-cut. It is a purpose-built composition that uses Lesson 04 source material to demonstrate the operating-manual register in 10–15 minutes.

| Time | Segment | Status |
|---|---|---|

| 0:45 – 2:30 | **The diagnosis** — the framing setup (most reps' pipeline is two pipelines) | Locked source from Lesson 04 |

Re-shooting for "production polish" introduces artificiality the brand cannot afford.

## 5. Audio + visual production discipline

### 5.1 Audio
- Host audio at `-16 LUFS` integrated, `-1 dB true peak`. Plain spec, broadcast-broadcast-adjacent, comfortable on phone speakers and good headphones equally.
- No music bed under the host. Per canon §6.5, music-as-tone-control is the SaaS-norm move we counter-position against.
- One-frame fade-in at clip start, one-frame fade-out at clip end. No swooshes, no whooshes, no riser stings.

### 5.2 Visual
- Camera framing: medium close-up, eyes upper-third, host facing camera on neutral cream-toned backdrop (canon §6.1 palette extension). No virtual background, no Zoom-blur, no logo-watermark loop.
- Cuts: minimal. The clip's editorial style is the Rowan register — verdict-shaped, pause-respecting, no "engaging cut every 3 seconds" energy.
- Captions burned in or available as SRT — accessibility non-negotiable.
- Framework-slide insert (around 2:30–5:30): full-screen slide replaces the host shot during framework narration, returns to host for self-assessment and close. Slide content lifts from D09 slide 11 verbatim.

### 5.3 Forbidden production moves
- B-roll stock footage of "office life" (handshakes, laptop closeups, sunset cityscapes). Forbidden per canon §6.5 + §6.9.1 (thumbnail test).
- Animated infographics for "engagement." Per canon §6.5 — icons must read as custom AESDR; SaaS-norm infographic libraries fail this immediately.
- Subscriber-callout end card ("subscribe for more!"). Canonical AESDR doesn't run a content engine; the close points to the offer, not to a YouTube subscribe button.
- Music swell at the close. The verdict-shaped outro carries on its own.
- "[HOST_FIRST_NAME]'s top 3 tips" thumbnail framing. Per canon §1.5, AESDR is operating manual, not listicle content.

## 6. Transcript spec

The transcript is **verbatim**, not paraphrased. Includes spoken words, host pauses (marked `[pause]`), and visual cues (marked `[diagram: forecasted-vs-actual funnel]`). Per canon §13, the transcript is the lesson on the page; if the host stumbled, the stumble is in the transcript.

### 6.1 Format

Markdown source renders to PDF (canon §8.5 PDF anatomy):
- Cream background, 24mm margins, mono eyebrow `AESDR · LESSON 04 PREVIEW · TRANSCRIPT v1`.
- `--display` italic 36pt headline: *Pipeline Integrity — Lesson 04 Preview Transcript.*
- `--serif` 14pt body, line-height 1.6.
- Timecodes in `--mono` 11pt `--muted` at the start of each speaker turn or visual cue.
- Visual-cue lines indented in `--serif` italic 13pt, `--muted`.

### 6.2 Required transcript discipline

- **Word-for-word** including filler ("uh," "you know") on first instance, then optionally cleaned starting at the third instance per session if the editorial pass demands it. Default: keep all of it.
- **Mark visual cues**, not just speaker words. The framework diagram is part of the lesson; the transcript names it.
- **Time-code every speaker turn** at minimum every 30 seconds. Easier search, easier accessibility.
- **No editorial summarization in-line.** Section headers within the transcript are allowed (`## The framework`, `## Reality interlude`, `## The four questions`) but the body under each header is the host's words.

### 6.3 Required disclaimers

Top of transcript document:

> *Verbatim transcript of AESDR's canonical Lesson 04 preview clip ([CLIP_DURATION_MINUTES] min). Timecodes reference the source video. This transcript is part of the AESDR lesson preview asset and may be lifted in full or in pull-quote form for partner promotion, with attribution to AESDR and disclosure per canon §10.1.*

## 8. Approval gate (canon §16 + §7.2)

Per canon §7.2 host casting profile + §16 approval gates, **a host ad-lib that becomes a recorded asset** is one of the five approval-gated surfaces. The Lesson 04 preview clip is a recorded asset by definition. Approval discipline:

1. **Source recording approval.** Before the source Lesson 04 recording is treated as the basis for the canonical preview, the founder approves the host's delivery against the §3 structural arc above. If a beat doesn't land, re-record before edit.
2. **Cut approval.** The 12-minute editorial cut is reviewed by founder before the file is shipped to the partner kit folder. Iteration cycle: one round of named edits, then approve or re-record.
3. **Partner co-branding variant approval.** Each partner who lifts the clip with their lockup gets a static-thumbnail render approved per canon §6.6.

Per canon §6.9.3, **first instance of any new layout pattern: founder pre-approval before first use.** The lesson-preview clip's title card extends the editorial-split pattern from D09 slide 01 — same pattern, new context. Per the §6.9.3 reading, that's pattern-extension, not new pattern, so no separate pre-approval gate beyond the §1 source-recording approval above.

## Visual treatment notes

**Layout pattern:**
- **Title card / static thumbnail:** editorial split per canon §6.3 + §8.6 (deck title-card pattern). Crimson left, cream right, ghost numeral, AESDR wordmark.
- **Framework slide insert** (around 2:30–5:30 of the clip): white panel on cream with `--light` border, mono header, framework diagram lifted from D09 slide 11.
- **Transcript PDF:** canon §8.5 PDF anatomy.
- **Takeaway block:** content-only; the block embeds into surfaces designed elsewhere (D26 §4 adjacent, partner newsletters).

**Palette:**
- Title card: `--cream` right panel, `--crimson` left panel, white type on crimson, `--ink` on cream.
- Framework slide: `--cream` background, `--ink` body, `--light` 1px border on the white panel.
- Transcript PDF: `--cream` background, `--ink` body, `--muted` for timecodes.
- Takeaway block: inherits surface (cream when on D26).
- No other tokens used.

**Type tokens:**
- Title card: `--display` italic (700, 56pt for title; 900, 64pt for wordmark), `--cond` (11pt mono eyebrow; 13pt hosted-by line), `--serif` italic (16pt context line), `--mono` (10pt subtag).
- Framework slide: `--cond` 14pt header, `--mono` 11pt diagram axis labels, `--display` italic 18pt quadrant labels.
- Transcript: `--display` italic 36pt headline, `--serif` 14pt body, `--mono` 11pt timecodes, `--serif` italic 13pt visual cues.
- Takeaway block: `--serif` 16–17pt body, `--display` italic 700 18pt for bold-anchor terms.

**Iconography:** Per canon §6.8 — seed-inventory ghost numerals on title card; the framework diagram is `--light` 1px lines and `--cond` labels (no clipart). No imported icons. No emoji in the takeaway block.

**Iris usage:** None on the title card or thumbnail. Per canon §6.4, iris is reserved for primary CTAs — and the preview clip does not carry a CTA on its visual surface (the CTA in §3.1 outro is *spoken* by the host, and the URL is a lower-third overlay in `--mono` `--ink`, not iris).

## Open

- **Whether to ship a 60-second cut** alongside the 12-minute clip for social-platform-native distribution. Default: **not in v1.** Short-form social cuts encourage the SaaS-content-engine motion AESDR explicitly counter-positions against (canon §1.1 + §1.5). If pilot data shows partners are clipping their own 60-second versions and the cuts drift off-brand, ship a canonical 60-second cut to anchor the pattern.
- **Whether to make the transcript searchable as a standalone web page** at `aesdr.com/preview/lesson-04`. Default: **not in v1.** A searchable transcript page becomes worthwhile once organic search starts contributing meaningful registration volume — pre-pilot, it's premature.
- **Per-pilot host headshot vs canonical host frame.** Default: **canonical frame.** Per canon §7.2, the host's job is to carry the brand, not their personal brand. A single canonical frame across pilots reads more operating-manual; per-pilot variation reads more guru-content.
- **Host stumble policy in the cut.** Default: **light editorial trim only — false starts cut, mid-sentence resets cut, but pauses kept.** Per canon §13, the verbatim transcript catches what's edited out; the cut respects the host's natural cadence.
- **Partner ability to clip a still from the framework slide for social.** Default: **yes**, with the AESDR × Partner lockup auto-applied per canon §6.6. The static framework slide is a pre-cleared social asset (canon §6.9.3 first-instance approval already covered by §8 of this spec).
