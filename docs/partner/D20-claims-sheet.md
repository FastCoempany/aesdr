# D20 — Approved & Forbidden Claims Sheet

**Deliverable:** Reference doc listing exactly which claims AESDR, the host, and partners may and may not make. Required reading for every partner before they post; required reading for the host before every workshop.
**Audience:** Partners (operators, coaches, creators, alumni); the AESDR host; founder; legal review.
**Voice ratio:** 90 Rowan / 10 Michael. Compliance is plain.
**Format:** Markdown source; renders to PDF one-pager per canon §6.5. May also be embedded in the partner enablement kit and the host contract.
**Canon refs:** `AFFILIATE_BRAND_CANON.md` §10.2–10.3 (claims), §10.6 (host legal status), §13 (honesty).

> **Why this matters legally.** Under FTC rules, anything a partner or host says about AESDR becomes AESDR's claim for substantiation purposes. We have to be able to prove every approved claim. Forbidden claims are forbidden whether or not we could substantiate them — they're not how we sell.

## B. Forbidden claims (never, by anyone, even with substantiation)

These claims are off-limits in every channel, surface, and voice — regardless of whether we could prove them. They are forbidden as a matter of brand and risk policy, not just compliance.

### B1. Income claims

Forbidden — examples of what nobody says:

- ❌ "Reps who take this course make $X more."
- ❌ "AESDR pays for itself in your first commission cycle."
- ❌ "Average AE income increase: $Y."

Even *true* income claims become legally fraught the moment they go on a public surface. We don't make them.

### B2. Job-placement claims

- ❌ "Graduates get hired at top SaaS companies."
- ❌ "AESDR helps you get promoted from SDR to AE."
- ❌ "X% of students land their next role within Y months."

We are a course, not a placement program. We do not promise outcomes that depend on the rest of the rep's life.

### B3. Performance / outcome promises

- ❌ "Your conversion rate will go up by Z%."
- ❌ "You'll close more deals."
- ❌ "Your forecast accuracy will improve."

Promises like these tie our marketing to a number we don't control. We talk about *what we teach*, not what the student's results will be.

### B4. Superlative / category claims

- ❌ "The best sales course on the internet."
- ❌ "The only course built by quota-carriers."
- ❌ "The #1 program for early-career SaaS reps."

We don't compete by claim. We compete by being.

### B5. Certification / accreditation language

- ❌ "AESDR-certified."
- ❌ "Earn your sales certification."
- ❌ "Add this to your LinkedIn credentials."

We are explicitly not a certification. Per canon §5, badge-hustle is anti-AESDR.

### B6. Guarantee / "results in X days" language

- ❌ "Guaranteed results in 30 days."
- ❌ "Money-back if it doesn't work in your first quarter."

Refund is 14-day, no-questions-asked. Anything beyond that is a forbidden guarantee.

### B7. Banned vocabulary anchors (any context)

Per canon §4.1 — `crush it`, `game-changer`, `unlock your potential`, `mindset`, `rise and grind`, `hustle harder`, `thought leader`, `lead with value`, `trusted advisor`, `sales rockstar / ninja / superstar`. These are forbidden as both vocabulary and as conceptual claims. If a partner's promo copy includes any of these, we ask for an edit before they post.

## D. Approval workflow

1. Partner drafts post / story / video / live workshop intro.
2. Partner sends draft to AESDR for pre-approval (per FTC monitoring rule).
3. AESDR reviews against this sheet.
4. AESDR replies with one of:
   - **Approved.** Ship as written.
   - **Approved with edits.** Inline tracked-change suggestions.
   - **Not approved — edit and resend.** With reason and an example of an approved alternative.
5. Partner ships approved version. Records draft + approval in shared folder.

This workflow is on-brand because it protects both sides. It is also legally protective: documented pre-approval is the gold-standard FTC defense if a claim is ever challenged.
