# D04 — Micro-Creator / Alumni-Ambassador Outreach Scripts

**Deliverable:** Three scripts each for two related archetypes — micro-creators (small-but-relevant audience on YouTube, niche newsletter, podcast) and alumni ambassadors (admin/leadership roles in alumni circles of named programs).
**Voice ratio:** 60 Rowan / 40 Michael. More peer-energy. Less "operator-to-operator" reverence than D02–D03; more "we're in the same trench."
**Sender:** Founder, by name.
**Length target:** 80–100 words each.
**Canon refs:** `AFFILIATE_BRAND_CANON.md` §3.3, §4, §5, §11.1, §12.

## Use note

These two archetypes share scripts because the pitch shape is the same: small audience, high relevance, low cost to test. They differ on the warmth axis — micro-creators are public; alumni ambassadors are inside private circles. Subject lines and openers reflect that.

Personalization: their actual content (a real episode, a real post, a real thread), the founder's first name, and a specific signal that you've actually paid attention.

## A. Micro-creator scripts

### 1. Cold — micro-creator

> Subject: Your audience looks unusually relevant
>
> [Name] —
>
> Caught [specific episode / post / thread]. The way you framed [specific point] is one of the few places I've seen the actual ramp problem named in plain English instead of LinkedIn vocabulary.
>
> I run AESDR — a 12-lesson sales survival course for first-1-to-2-year SDRs and AEs. We're piloting a workshop-first partner motion with a small number of aligned creators. One workshop into your audience, tracked attribution, no exclusivity, commission on net revenue.
>
> Worth a 15-minute call to see if the fit is real?
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

### 2. Warm — micro-creator

> Subject: Founding pilot — one workshop, one offer
>
> [Name] —
>
> Following up on [specific signal — a DM exchange, a comment thread, a mutual intro]. The audience you've built is unusually specific; that's the whole reason I'm asking.
>
> The pilot is small on purpose: one live workshop into your audience, one offer at the close, 30-day attribution, real numbers. If it works, we extend. If it doesn't, we part as adults — no awkward email chain six months later.
>
> Free this week or next for a quick call?
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

### 3. Reply-to-no-reply — micro-creator

> Subject: Bumping once, then I'll go quiet
>
> [Name] —
>
> One bump on the AESDR pilot note. The format is small enough to be honest about: one workshop, one offer, real tracking, no exclusivity, no carousel.
>
> If timing's wrong, no offense. Otherwise I'm around.
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

## B. Alumni-ambassador scripts

### 4. Cold — alumni ambassador

> Subject: Pilot fit — early-career reps in your circle
>
> [Name] —
>
> [Mutual context — how you found them: an alumni event, a Slack mention, a mutual friend]. The reps in [alumni network] are exactly who AESDR was built for: first 1–2 years in startup SaaS, more pressure than coaching, more activity than judgment.
>
> Rather than pitch a referral arrangement, I'd rather test one workshop into your circle. Cohort-specific, geo-specific if useful, tracked end-to-end. If it works for your folks, we keep going; if not, we don't pretend.
>
> Worth a quick call?
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

### 5. Warm — alumni ambassador

> Subject: Small pilot, real tracking, your room
>
> [Name] —
>
> Thanks for [specific signal]. The thing you said about [specific phrase] is the gap AESDR's first six lessons exist to close.
>
> The pilot's small on purpose. One workshop into [alumni network], a referral code or stipend structure if commission feels weird inside an alumni context, and a 30-day attribution window. We see your folks' results; you see ours. No exclusivity.
>
> Available for a quick call this week?
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

### 6. Reply-to-no-reply — alumni ambassador

> Subject: Last ping on the pilot
>
> [Name] —
>
> One nudge, then quiet. The pilot fit for [alumni network] is one of the best on my list — small reach, high trust, exactly the cohort.
>
> If now isn't right, totally fine. If later is, the door stays open.
>
> — [Founder first name], AESDR

## Notes

- The "no carousel" line shows up in micro-creator #3 because the LinkedIn overlap is highest there — many micro-creators publish on LinkedIn-adjacent surfaces. The line draws a clear "we're not that" boundary up front.
- Alumni ambassadors get a softer commission framing (#5 — "stipend if commission feels weird") because alumni-network politics often disallow commercial arrangements. Don't push commission on a context that won't tolerate it.
- Both archetypes are low-volume. Don't chase them past the bump.
- Michael's voice flicker is in #5 ("you see your folks' results; you see ours") — peer-frame, not pitch-frame.

## Open

- For alumni ambassadors specifically: do we offer a non-commercial co-host structure (free workshop, no money exchanged, just attribution)? Useful for tightly-bound networks. Default: hold until first request.
- Subject-line variant for warm cold-out from podcast guests (where AESDR's founder appeared on their show): different opener, same skeleton. Hold until that situation arises.
