# L&D-Approver Brief

**Deliverable type:** Sub-deliverable — not numbered as a D-file. Referenced as a forward dependency in D16 (checkout-abandon email PS), D17 V3 (high-intent DM, checkout-started variant), D23 Q06 (FAQ), and D31 (curriculum map L&D-approver audience). This document closes the four-piece forward dep.
**What it is:** A one-page PDF AESDR sends to a prospect (or that the prospect forwards to their L&D approver / manager) when the buyer needs internal approval before enrolling. The document the approver reads to make a yes/no in 3 minutes — formatted for the conversation, not for the buyer.
**Audience:** L&D / Learning & Development approvers, sales managers reviewing reimbursement requests, finance approvers stamping a small training-budget line item. Often skim-readers; sometimes circulated to one more reviewer.
**Voice ratio:** 95 Rowan / 5 Michael. Verdict register dialed for corporate-acceptable plainness — the approver should read this and feel it's a credible, professional document. Michael appears in a single closing line (the disqualification anchor) that reads as honesty, not informality.
**Format:** Markdown source. Renders to a 1-page (single-side, letter or A4) PDF per canon §6.5 + §8.5. The discipline is *fits on one page printed*. Reviewers do not scroll.
**Send mechanism:** Two paths — (a) prospect requests it via `admissions@aesdr.com` after starting checkout (referenced in D16, D17), or (b) prospect downloads it directly from the FAQ Q06 path (referenced in D23). AESDR ops sends a per-prospect-customized version where the *prospect's name* is in the salutation block — the brief is "for [PROSPECT_NAME]'s manager," not generic.
**Canon refs:** `AFFILIATE_BRAND_CANON.md` §1.6 (honesty discipline), §3.3 (voice ratios), §6.5 (PDF render), §8.5 (PDF anatomy), §10.2 (approved claims), §10.3 (forbidden claims — no income claims), §13 (honesty discipline).

> **Placeholder convention:** `[PROSPECT_NAME]`, `[PROSPECT_ROLE]` (e.g., "SDR," "first-year AE"), `[PROSPECT_COMPANY]`, `[PROSPECT_TENURE]` (in months/years). `[ENROLLMENT_TIER]` (SDR / AE / Team). `[PRICE]` (matches the tier — list, not pilot, since L&D approval often outlasts the pilot window). `[FOUNDER_FIRST_NAME]` for the closing signature. ### What AESDR is

A 12-lesson curriculum built specifically for early-career SaaS sales reps in their first 0–3 years. Hosted by `[HOST_FIRST_NAME] [HOST_LAST_NAME]`, who has [HOST_TENURE_YEARS] years across SDR, AE, and sales-management roles. **Unconventional in tone, conventional in shape:** a defined 12-lesson curriculum (36 sub-units) with documented outcomes, a refund window, and a cohort community. AESDR is upfront that several lessons name parts of the role most sales-training programs avoid — drinking culture, financial reality, manager dynamics, the personal cost of being on. That candor is the program's distinguishing feature, not a footnote.

### What [PROSPECT_NAME] would learn

For an early-career rep in [PROSPECT_ROLE], the program targets four specific operating outcomes:
- **Honest close-rate math.** Lesson 8 walks the 30/40/30 segmentation managers already use whether they say so or not — and the self-assessment that lets [PROSPECT_NAME] read their own pipeline without the optimism bias most reps carry.
- **Comp-plan literacy.** Lesson 10 covers reading the comp plan, including the parts that aren't written down (clawbacks, accelerator triggers, what counts as "closed-won" for commission). Plus the financial-discipline practice for variable income.
- **Manager-reading and managing-up framework.** Lesson 4 names the ten manager archetypes and the framework for working productively with whichever one [PROSPECT_NAME] actually has — versus the one the org chart claims they have.
- **The role's operating cost on the person.** Lessons 11 and 12 cover the parts of the role most courses avoid: alcohol culture in sales, home-office burnout, the impact on relationships, the long-term identity question. These lessons are not optional, not buried in an appendix; they are part of the operating manual.

These are operating-skill outcomes, not income outcomes. AESDR does not promise revenue lift, deal-size lift, promotion timing, or any specific dollar number. Per canon §10.3 (forbidden claims), AESDR does not make income claims about its program — and we'd flag any one-page-summary that did.

### Time commitment

12 lessons. ~45–60 minutes per lesson (video + worksheet + tool). Recommended pace: 1–2 lessons per week across 6–8 weeks. The rep's weekly time investment is **~1–2 hours**, mostly in evenings. The Untamed Discord community is asynchronous — optional reading, no mandatory office hours.

### Refund policy

14-day, no-questions-asked. If the program does not deliver value, AESDR processes the refund in 3 business days. The literal policy: *"If it doesn't deliver value, we don't want your money."*

### Why now (versus later)

Most reps lose ramp not from missing pipeline but from not knowing *why* they missed. AESDR's first four lessons give the diagnostic frame for that question; reps with the frame ramp faster than reps without it. Per AESDR's positioning brief (D21), the operating gap is widest in months 6–18 of a SaaS sales role. If [PROSPECT_NAME] is in that window, the cost of waiting is one quarter's worth of compounding judgment.

### What this is not

- **Not a sales-introduction course.** AESDR assumes [PROSPECT_NAME] has the seat. It is not "how to break into sales."
- **Not industry-specific.** Calibrated for early-career SaaS sales generally; not vertical-specific tactics.
- **Not a credential program.** No certificate, no badge, no hiring-weight credential. The line on a LinkedIn profile would be "AESDR · 12-lesson operating curriculum" — honest and accurate, not credentialed.
- **Not a coaching program.** No live cohort sessions, no 1:1 calls, no scheduled office hours. Self-paced curriculum + asynchronous community.

## Operational notes for ops

When sending the brief:

- **Customize fields per prospect.** Generic-named briefs get filed as junk. Per-prospect customization requires the prospect's first name, role, company, and tenure (often available from registration data + LinkedIn lookup, sometimes from the prospect's reply directly).
- **Send timing.** Within 4 business hours of the prospect's request. Per canon §12.2 founder lurk-and-listen, the founder owns the speed; ops owns the customization.
- **File the send.** Each L&D brief sent goes into `docs/partner/pilots/[PARTNER_SLUG]/L-and-D/[PROSPECT_NAME]--[date].pdf` for audit and for D33 postmortem signal.
- **Follow-up.** No automated follow-up after the brief sends. Per canon §13 + §9.2 (anti-fake-urgency), the brief is a door, not a sequence. If the prospect doesn't enroll within 14 days, the contact closes silently — same protocol as D17 V3.

## Open

- **Whether the brief should mention the L&D approver's name** when the prospect provides it. Default: **yes if available** (e.g., "for [PROSPECT_NAME]'s manager [APPROVER_NAME]"). Reads as more researched, less form-letter. If not available, omit cleanly.
- **Whether the brief includes a "what other companies have approved this" line.** Default: **never.** Per canon §13, fake social proof contradicts the brand. If real customer-company data accumulates and a partner specifically requests, AESDR may add a line per canon-revision PR. Do not invent it.
- **Whether the brief should be available as a self-serve download** at `aesdr.com/l-and-d-brief.pdf`. Default: **not in v1.** A self-serve download removes AESDR's chance to customize per prospect, which is where this brief earns its 1-page real estate. Reconsider once L&D-approval volume crosses ~10 sends per month.
- **Translation.** Default: English only in v1.
- **Page count discipline.** If a future content addition pushes the brief past one page, the rule is: **cut content, don't add a page.** A two-page brief is a different deliverable for a different audience. Recommend: if v2 needs more space, fork a separate "team-buyer brief" rather than expand this one.
- **Whether to ship a Team-tier variant** of the brief specifically for manager-approves-cohort scenarios. Default: **not in v1.** The Team tier in §"What it costs" is mentioned; the prospect-led approval flow this brief serves is the dominant volume. If Team-tier sales increase, ship a separate Team-buyer brief with different framing.
