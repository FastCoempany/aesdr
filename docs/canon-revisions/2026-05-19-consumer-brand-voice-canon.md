# Consumer brand-voice canon

> **Status:** Active canon. Supersedes ad-hoc voice rules scattered across
> CLAUDE.md, AGENTS.md, and individual page-level overrides. Anything
> shipped to a buyer-facing surface — consumer landing, /about, emails,
> dashboard, course UI — must conform to the rules below.
> **Scope:** Consumer-side. The B2B subsidiary at /enterprise has its own
> register (see AESDR_ENTERPRISE_CANON.md); for /enterprise pages, prefer that.
> **Owner:** Antaeus Coe.
> **Last revised:** 2026-05-19.

This is the voice document I wish I'd had when I first started writing
copy under stress. Reading it should take seven minutes; running every
new piece of consumer-side copy past it should take under one.

---

## 1. The reader

The reader is an AE or an SDR in their first eighteen months. They are
tired, slightly defensive about how their job is going, and have read too
many sales blogs. They are not looking for permission to feel better.
They are looking for the tool they should have had at week one.

They are also smart. Don't write down to them. The phrase "let me explain"
is banned because they can read.

## 2. Two-line house style

1. **Operating manual, not motivation engine.**
2. **If it doesn't deliver value, we don't want your money.**

Every paragraph either advances the operating manual or names a real
out-clause. If it does neither, cut it.

## 3. The blocklist

Words and phrases that do not appear in consumer-side copy. If a draft
contains them, rewrite before shipping.

- **rep** — always *AE*, *SDR*, or *AEs and SDRs*. Founder direction:
  "rep is a passive naming convention to me; we use AE and/or SDR."
- **crush it / crush your quota / smash your number** — guru framing
- **best-in-class / next-generation / world-class** — marketing-grade
  intensifiers; if it's true, the sentence demonstrates it instead
- **unleash / unlock your potential** — motivation-engine register
- **proven framework / proven methodology** — claim without evidence
- **revolutionise / disrupt / game-changing** — overclaiming verbs
- **think outside the box** — anti-canon
- **the wedge / our positioning / the operating system** — internal
  positioning language that leaked from strategy docs into buyer-facing
  surfaces in the May 2026 audit. Rewrite to the buyer's vocabulary.
- **let me explain / here's the thing** — filler that doesn't land
- **literally** — almost always replaceable with nothing

## 4. The substitution table

| Don't write | Write instead |
|---|---|
| crush your quota | hit number / get out of statistic |
| our proven framework | the four-archetype map (or whatever the named tool is) |
| unleash your potential | get the eighteen-month accident out of your career |
| best-in-class training | what good actually looks like |
| revolutionary | unfamiliar to most of the industry |
| game-changer | the lesson most AEs/SDRs read twice |
| game-changing insight | the line that lands with most managers |
| the wedge | the part of the job most courses skip |
| our methodology | this lesson / this tool / this framework |
| reach out | email |
| at the end of the day | (delete) |
| literally | (delete) |

## 5. Register and rhythm

- **Sentence length: short, but uneven.** A run of four short sentences
  reads like a checklist. Mix one long, one short, one half-long.
- **Em dashes are the AESDR punctuation mark.** Use them to interrupt
  yourself. Two per paragraph is the ceiling.
- **Contractions are fine.** *Don't, we're, you'll*. The avoiding-
  contractions register reads as corporate legalese.
- **British / American English: American spelling.** Brand surfaces
  (aesdr.com, /enterprise, emails) are American — *colour → color*,
  *behaviour → behavior*. Match the buyer's market.
- **Em-dash > en-dash > hyphen.** Don't use the wrong one. `—` not `--`.

## 6. The seven moves

The voice has seven recurring rhetorical moves. Most copy uses one or two.

1. **The diagnosis.** Name the problem in a sentence the reader has
   thought but not said out loud. *"You're talking too much. That's not
   an opinion — it's a diagnosis."*
2. **The math.** When in doubt, substitute a number. *"Eleven hours
   yesterday. Four of them were selling."*
3. **The dignity-preserving out-clause.** When asking for an action,
   offer the way out at the same time. *"If it doesn't deliver value,
   we don't want your money."*
4. **The named tool.** Specific artefact names build credibility faster
   than abstract claims. *"The Friday five-line."* *"The 72-hour strike
   plan."* *"The manager OS one-pager."*
5. **The cited stat.** When you state a market fact, link the source
   (Bridge Group, Gartner). Never invent percentages.
6. **The Rowan Pope line.** Two sentences. The first is the threat. The
   second is the resolution. *"They will say no. The question is what
   you do in the next four seconds."*
7. **The micro-honesty.** Acknowledge what we're not. *"No guru stuff.
   No 'crush it' motivation. No LinkedIn-friendly badges."*

## 7. Surface-specific notes

### Hero copy (consumer landing)
- Headline: 6-12 words, no comma, no semicolon. Iris-shimmer accent on
  one phrase, never the whole line.
- Sub-headline: one sentence. Names the reader and the promise.
- Eyebrow mono-label above headline: 3-6 words, all caps, mono font.

### CTA buttons
- Verb-first, max 4 words. *Get access* / *Continue Lesson 3* /
  *Email me the PDF*. Never *click here* / *learn more* / *submit*.

### Section headings
- Editorial italic Playfair Display. Lowercase first letter unless
  the section name is a proper noun.

### Body paragraphs
- Source Serif 4 at 17-18px. Line-height 1.65-1.7. Max line length
  ~640px. Long-form is allowed; cramped is not.

### Email subject lines
- 40-60 characters. Lower-case first letter. No emoji. Statement, not
  question, unless the question is the move (*"Is this still useful — or
  should we close the loop?"*).

### Microcopy / placeholders
- Use the specific over the generic. *"my desk at home"* not
  *"location"*. *"you@work.com"* not *"email address"*.

### Error states
- Short, plural, blame-free. *"That doesn't look like a valid email."*
  Not *"Invalid email. Please try again."*

## 9. The five quick tests

Before shipping any consumer-side string, run it past these:

1. **Did I write *rep*?** Replace with AE/SDR.
2. **Did I write *crush*, *unleash*, *world-class*, *best-in-class*, or *revolutionary*?** Rewrite.
3. **Did I make a numerical claim without a citation?** Add the citation or delete the number.
4. **Could this sentence appear on three other SaaS sales courses?** Rewrite to be specific.
5. **Is there a buried out-clause I forgot to surface?** Add it.

If all five pass, ship it.

## Appendix A — Founder voice vs. brand voice

The brand voice above is the default. Antaeus's voice — the founder
register used in dashboard copy, the about page, founder-signed emails
— is a tighter subset:

- First person singular ("I", not "we") in founder-signed emails
- Sign-off: *— Antaeus*, never *— AESDR*
- Allowed to swear in private founder-signed surfaces (about page,
  founder emails). Not in marketing copy.

Use founder voice when: it's an email signed by Antaeus, the /about
page, or any founder-written annotation in the course UI.
Use brand voice when: anywhere else.

## Appendix B — How this canon enforces

- New consumer copy: run past the five quick tests in §9 before merging.
- Edits to consumer copy: cite this doc in the PR description if the
  edit was driven by it.
- Audits: the May 2026 behavioral audit revisits §3 (blocklist) and
  §4 (substitutions). Add entries when new violations are caught.
- Drift: if a blocklist word reappears across more than one surface in
  a month, the team has lost the canon. Re-read it.
