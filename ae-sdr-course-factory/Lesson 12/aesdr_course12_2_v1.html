Section 01 — The Disconnect
Presence vs. Proximity

Working from home gives the illusion of availability. You’re in the house all day, so your spouse assumes they’ll see more of you. But when your attention is locked into Zoom calls and Slack threads, your physical presence means nothing.

  • 01The Illusion: Home for lunch breaks, sitting in the same room, sharing a workspace.
  • 02The Reality: Too distracted to engage, glued to notifications, rarely sharing meaningful moments.
  • 03The Cost: Being physically close doesn’t matter if you’re not emotionally present.
The Reality Check
"Working from home should make life easier, right?"
The always-on culture bulldozes through those benefits. The result? You’re physically there but emotionally absent.

Section 02 & 03 — The Burnout Trap
The Emotional Black Hole & Digital Overload

Your job demands constant emotional energy. Every pitch, demo, and rejection takes something out of you. By the time you’re done, you have nothing left for your partner.

  • 01The Leftovers: You give your best self to prospects, leaving your spouse with the exhausted, disengaged version of you.
  • 02The Perception: To them, it feels like a choice—like work comes first and they’re just an afterthought.
  • 03The Digital Thief: Slack messages, LinkedIn updates, and CRM tasks never stop. Every digital distraction steals time and attention from your spouse.
The Bottom Line
"When you’re always half-plugged into work, it sends the message that they’re not your priority."
Even if that's not your intention, the constant pings and scrolling erode the connection you should be building at home.

Section 06 — Homework
Define Your Work-Life Rules
Task 1: Write Your Shutdown Ritual
  • What’s one action you’ll take to signal the end of your workday?
  • Examples: closing the laptop, leaving the office, changing clothes.
Task 2: Set Non-Negotiable Quality Time
  • Identify two blocks of time each week where you’ll be 100% present with your spouse.
  • Treat it with the same level of importance as a client meeting.
Task 3: Turn Off Notifications & Share Boundaries
  • Commit to disabling Slack, email, and LinkedIn notifications during personal time.
  • Discuss these rules with your spouse and ask for feedback.
