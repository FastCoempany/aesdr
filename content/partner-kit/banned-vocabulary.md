# Banned Vocabulary

The words and phrases AESDR doesn't use — and asks partners not to use
in copy that bears the AESDR mark.

This isn't pickiness. The audience for this curriculum has been getting
sold to in this register for their entire career. They can spot it in
30 seconds, and the moment they do, the credibility of the
recommendation collapses.

## The list

- "Crush it" / "crushing it" / "crush your quota" / "crush your number"
- "Game-changer" / "game-changing"
- "Unlock your potential"
- "Mindset" (used as anything other than literal psychology)
- "Rise and grind" / "grindset" / "hustle culture"
- "Thought leader" / "thought leadership"
- "Lead with value" / "trusted advisor"
- "Synergy" / "leverage" (as a verb)
- "Amazing" / "incredible" / unearned superlatives
- "Empower" / "empowerment"
- "Sales superstar" / "rockstar" / "ninja" / "wizard"
- Generic hype emojis (🚀 💪 🔥) on any surface bearing the AESDR mark

## Why each one is out

The pattern under all of them: each phrase rewards saying-not-doing.
Each suggests a feeling-state matters more than an operating-state.
Each comes loaded with context the audience has already filed under
"performative."

The brand AESDR is building wins by being plain, specific, and
operationally true. Anything that performs expertise rather than
installs it works against that — including in copy a partner writes
about us.

## What we ask

If a piece of partner copy uses any of the above and AESDR's mark is on
it, we'll ask for an edit before publishing. Refusal is rare, but it
does end pilots — partner-fit and buyer-fit are the same fit here, and
the register is the canary.

## When this list is irrelevant to you

If you're already writing in a register that wouldn't trigger any of
these words naturally, this whole list is a no-op. You read it, nodded,
moved on. That's already a strong fit signal both ways.
