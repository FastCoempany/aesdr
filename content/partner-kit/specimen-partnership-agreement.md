# Specimen Partnership Agreement

A plain-English read of the terms AESDR currently uses in its partner
agreements. Provided so prospective partners can see the operating, economic,
and legal commitments before applying.

> **SPECIMEN — not for signature.** This document is a written representation
> of the deal terms; it is not the executed agreement. The agreement of record
> is a separate PDF reviewed by counsel and counter-signed by both parties
> after partner approval. Where this specimen and the executed PDF differ,
> the executed PDF controls. Nothing here is legal advice; partners are
> encouraged to have their own counsel review the executed agreement before
> signing.

---

## 1. Parties

**AESDR** — the AESDR curriculum and its operating entity (the "Company").

**Partner** — the individual, newsletter, podcast, community, or business
that has been approved into the AESDR partner program and counter-signed the
executed agreement (the "Partner").

## 2. Term and termination

- The agreement runs for one **time-boxed pilot** — typically a 30-day
  attribution window plus the payment-clearance period (see §5). Roughly
  90 days end-to-end from the Partner's first promotional send.
- Either party may terminate the agreement at any time, in writing, with
  30 days' notice. Termination does not extinguish commissions already
  earned on enrollments inside the attribution window.
- The Partner agreement is **non-exclusive**. The Partner may promote other
  programs (including competing ones); AESDR may work with other partners.
- Renewal beyond the pilot is by mutual agreement in writing. There is no
  auto-renewal.

## 3. What the Partner does

- Sends two emails to the Partner's audience on mutually agreed dates,
  using copy AESDR provides (the Partner may rework copy in their voice;
  reworked copy is reviewed before send).
- Provides a brief (~2-minute) introduction to the live workshop using a
  script AESDR provides.
- Includes a clear, FTC-compliant affiliate disclosure on every promotional
  surface (email, social, post, audio, video).
- Submits any paid placement, sponsored post, or paid story bearing the
  AESDR mark for review before publication. Standard turnaround is 48 hours.
- Shares the Partner's send-side analytics (open rate, click rate) within
  one business day of each send so both sides see the same numbers.
- Honors the [Banned Vocabulary](/partners/kit/banned-vocabulary),
  [Approved Claims](/partners/kit/approved-claims), and
  [Disclosure Language Pack](/partners/kit/disclosure-language-pack)
  in copy bearing the AESDR mark.

## 4. What AESDR does

- Builds a Partner-attributed registration page with tracking in place
  within 24 hours of agreement execution.
- Runs the live 60-minute workshop. Hosts, presents, handles Q&A. Replay
  stays live for 72 hours.
- Handles every audience touch after registration — confirmations,
  reminders, replay link, post-workshop follow-up, soft re-engagement.
- Sends the Partner a one-page Friday report each week of the pilot
  showing clicks, registrations, attendance, enrollment, refunds, and
  projected commission.
- Pays commissions and the sign-on bonus (where applicable) per §5 and §6.

## 5. Commission

- **Rate.** 30% of net revenue on every enrollment attributed to the Partner
  during the attribution window.
- **Net revenue.** Gross revenue minus (i) refunds issued within the 14-day
  buyer-refund window, (ii) payment-processor fees (Stripe at approximately
  2.9% + $0.30 per US-card transaction; international and ACH may differ),
  and (iii) any sales or use tax collected on the Company's behalf. Net is
  what the Company actually keeps after those deductions. 30% of that goes
  to the Partner.
- **Attribution window.** 30 days from a visitor's first qualifying click
  on the Partner's tracked URL. First-touch attribution. A registered
  visitor who returns within the window via Google, direct, or any other
  channel and enrolls is attributed to the Partner.
- **Refund window.** 14 days from the buyer's purchase date. Commission is
  not paid on refunded purchases. Commission math closes after the refund
  window closes for the latest in-window purchase.
- **Reporting cadence.** Friday reports during the pilot show every click,
  registration, attendance event, enrollment, refund, and projected
  commission. Final commission is calculated when the 30-day attribution
  window closes and all in-window refund windows have closed.

## 6. Sign-on bonus (Cohort 1 invited partners only)

- Cohort 1 partners whom AESDR invites directly receive a **$500 sign-on
  bonus** paid on agreement execution. The bonus is paid by the next
  business-day ACH, Wise, or PayPal cycle after the Company receives the
  counter-signed agreement.
- The sign-on bonus is **not** offered to partners who applied through the
  public application path, nor to partners outside Cohort 1, unless
  expressly stated in the executed agreement.
- The sign-on bonus is not contingent on enrollment performance and is
  not clawed back if the pilot under-performs.

## 7. Payments

- **Cadence.** Net-45 from the close of the 30-day attribution window.
  In practice, the Partner sees commission about 75 days from the first
  promotional send.
- **Methods.** ACH (US), Wise (international), or PayPal — Partner's choice
  at agreement signing. Stripe Connect partner payouts may be offered later;
  the executed agreement will reflect what's actually available at signing.
- **Minimum payout.** $50. If commission earned in a pilot does not clear
  the minimum, it rolls forward and clears in the next earned payout.
  Outstanding balance is paid at agreement termination regardless of
  minimum.
- **Currency.** USD. International payouts are sent in USD; the Partner's
  banking partner handles conversion.
- **Tax forms.** The Partner provides a current W-9 (US) or W-8BEN /
  W-8BEN-E (non-US) before the first payout. AESDR issues 1099-NEC at
  year-end for US partners paid $600 or more in the calendar year.
- **Disputes.** If the Partner disputes a Friday report figure or a final
  commission calculation, raise it in writing within 30 days. AESDR shares
  the underlying tracking + Stripe data and corrects in the next payout
  if substantiated.

## 8. Tracking and data

- AESDR's attribution stack uses first-touch attribution, a 30-day cookie,
  UTM parameters preserved through the registration → workshop → enrollment
  → payment flow, and Stripe's metadata on each transaction.
- Each Friday report and final reconciliation is built from the same
  tracking + Stripe data AESDR uses internally. There is no separate
  "Partner-facing" number.
- The Partner receives on-demand CSV exports of click, registration,
  enrollment, and commission lines on request.

## 9. Promotional restrictions

- **Pricing.** AESDR's published price is the price. The Partner does not
  offer, advertise, or imply pricing the Company has not authorized.
  No private codes, no partner-stack discounts, no flash promotions on
  the AESDR mark.
- **Outcomes.** The Partner does not promise outcomes the curriculum does
  not promise — promotions, hires, certifications, quota, or income. The
  curriculum is sold as a curriculum.
- **Disclosure.** A clear, FTC-compliant material-connection disclosure
  appears near every recommendation. Templates in the
  [Disclosure Language Pack](/partners/kit/disclosure-language-pack).
- **Paid amplification.** Paid placements, sponsored posts, and paid stories
  bearing the AESDR mark are pre-cleared. Standard turnaround 48 hours.
- **Audience.** The Partner does not promote AESDR to audiences the
  [Positioning Brief](/partners/kit/positioning-brief) identifies as
  out-of-scope (e.g., audiences who have not entered or are no longer in
  the SaaS sales-development category in a meaningful way).

## 10. Marks and intellectual property

- AESDR owns the AESDR name, the Leponeus mascot, the curriculum, all
  course content, and the AESDR × Partner lockup.
- The Partner owns the Partner's name, marks, audience list, and
  Partner-generated copy.
- AESDR grants the Partner a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable,
  revocable license to use the AESDR name and the AESDR × Partner lockup
  in promotional materials during the term, subject to the
  [Lockup Usage Rules](/partners/kit/lockup-usage).
- The Partner grants AESDR a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable,
  revocable license to use the Partner's name and, where the Partner
  supplies it, mark, in promotional materials referring to the partnership
  during the term.
- Neither party may register the other's marks, create derivative works of
  the other's content, or transfer rights to a third party without written
  consent.

## 11. Confidentiality

- Each party may receive non-public information from the other —
  audience data, Friday-report numbers, in-development course content,
  pricing experiments, or business plans (the "Confidential Information").
- Each party will (i) use Confidential Information only to perform under
  this agreement, (ii) protect it with at least the same care used for
  its own confidential information, and (iii) not disclose it to third
  parties without written consent, except as required by law or to
  professional advisors under similar obligations.
- Confidentiality survives termination of the agreement for two (2) years.

## 12. Representations and limited warranties

- Each party represents it has the authority to enter the agreement and
  to grant the licenses described.
- AESDR represents the curriculum exists, the prices are real, the refund
  policy is real, and the commission rate and attribution mechanics are
  applied as described.
- The Partner represents the Partner's audience is real, the Partner has
  the right to send to that audience, and the Partner will not represent
  AESDR in ways outside the Approved Claims.
- Beyond the express representations above, the curriculum is provided
  "as is" — no warranty of fitness for a particular professional outcome,
  career outcome, or earnings outcome is made or implied.

## 13. Indemnification

- AESDR will defend, indemnify, and hold the Partner harmless from third-
  party claims arising from (i) AESDR's curriculum content, (ii) AESDR's
  handling of buyer data after registration, or (iii) AESDR's payment
  processing.
- The Partner will defend, indemnify, and hold AESDR harmless from third-
  party claims arising from (i) the Partner's promotional copy outside
  the Approved Claims, (ii) the Partner's audience-list practices, or
  (iii) the Partner's failure to disclose the material connection.

## 14. Limitation of liability

- Neither party is liable for indirect, incidental, consequential,
  special, or punitive damages — including lost profits or lost
  audience — even if advised of the possibility.
- Each party's aggregate liability under the agreement is capped at the
  total commissions paid (or owed) under the agreement in the 12 months
  preceding the claim.
- The cap does not apply to (i) confidentiality breaches, (ii) IP
  infringement, or (iii) the Partner's indemnification obligations
  arising from misrepresentation to its audience.

## 15. Governing law and disputes

- Governing law and venue are specified in the executed agreement and
  reflect the Company's state of incorporation.
- The parties will attempt to resolve disputes by good-faith discussion
  for 30 days before initiating formal proceedings. Either party may
  seek injunctive relief at any time for IP or confidentiality breaches.

## 16. General

- **Independent contractors.** The Partner is an independent contractor.
  Nothing creates an employment, agency, joint venture, or franchise
  relationship.
- **Assignment.** Neither party may assign the agreement without the
  other's written consent, except to a successor in interest in
  connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all
  assets.
- **Notices.** Written notices go to the email addresses on the
  signature page.
- **Entire agreement.** The executed agreement (and any annexes) is the
  entire agreement between the parties and supersedes prior discussions.
- **Amendments.** Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.
- **Severability.** If any provision is unenforceable, the rest stands.
- **No waiver.** A delay in enforcing a right is not a waiver of it.
