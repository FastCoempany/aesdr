# Disclosure Language Pack

Most affiliate programs handwave FTC disclosure. AESDR doesn't. If you have a
material connection to AESDR — commission, comp, free access, anything — you
disclose it, in plain language, near the recommendation, every time.

Below is the language we ask every partner to use. Lift verbatim. The rules
are not ours; they're the FTC's. We're just specific about how to satisfy them.

## By surface

| Surface | Language |
|---|---|
| Newsletter / blog | `Affiliate disclosure: I earn a commission if you enroll through this link.` Place near the link, not in the footer. |
| Social post / story | `#ad` or `#sponsored` near the top of the caption or in the first text frame. Plain text, not buried in 30 hashtags. |
| Reel / video | Verbal disclosure within the first 10 seconds **and** on-screen text that stays for the duration of the recommendation. |
| Live workshop | Verbal disclosure during the partner's intro **and** on the registration page. |
| DM with affiliate link | One line in the same message: `Heads up — I get a commission if you enroll.` |

## Rules of thumb

- **Clear** — language a reasonable reader understands without a glossary.
- **Conspicuous** — visible without scrolling, hovering, or expanding.
- **Close** — adjacent to the recommendation, not banished to a footer.
- **Consistent** — the same disclosure on the same surface every time.

## What happens if you can't or won't disclose

We end the pilot. This is a fit signal, not a punishment. Disclosure isn't
optional for partners under FTC 16 CFR Part 255, and the brand we're building
isn't compatible with shortcuts on it.

## What we don't ask you to do

We don't ask you to perform humility about earning the commission. The
commission is an honest exchange — you brought the audience, we delivered the
program, the buyer got fair value at list price. The disclosure says exactly
that. There's nothing to apologize for.
