Section 01 — The AE Ego Problem
The Root of the Tension

AE ego isn't always intentional, but it is incredibly damaging. From micromanagement to blaming SDRs for failures, these behaviors create toxic dynamics that hurt productivity, trust, and team morale.

  • 01Why It Exists: High-pressure roles can breed ego and defensiveness. AEs often feel undervalued or misunderstood, leading them to overcompensate.
  • 02Impact on SDRs: SDRs feel disrespected, frustrated, and burnt out.
  • 03Impact on Results: Collaboration and trust break down. Leads are lost due to misalignment and distrust. Poor collaboration equals poor results.
The Reality Check
"AE ego doesn't just affect SDRs—it hurts the entire team's performance."
Resentment builds, opportunities are missed, and prospects feel the lack of alignment.

Section 02 — Addressing the Ego
Framework for Addressing AE Ego

Step 1 is recognizing the problem. AEs and SDRs need to identify specific behaviors—without assigning blame—so you can begin to address them together.

  • 01Diagnose the Behavior: Identify micromanaging, undermining teams, or skipping meetings.
  • 02Open Honest Communication: SDRs should use "I" statements and focus on impact. AEs should ask for input and acknowledge mistakes.
  • 03Establish Mutual Expectations: AEs must collaborate, trust SDR qualification, and attend meetings. SDRs must own prospecting, share feedback, and communicate proactively.
The Fix
"Approach these conversations with transparency and a focus on solutions."
Blame doesn't fix anything—communication does. Addressing AE ego isn't about blame—it's about building a better working relationship.

AE Self-Awareness Checklist
  • Do I trust my SDR's work, or am I micromanaging?
  • Am I respecting my SDR's time and contributions?
  • Do I offer constructive feedback instead of blame?
Actionable Homework
  • For SDRs: Track Key Pain Points—Write down 2–3 examples of AE behaviors that impact your work.
  • For SDRs: Schedule a 1:1—Propose a conversation to share feedback constructively.
  • For AEs: Self-Assess Your Behaviors—Reflect on how you interact with SDRs and other teams.
  • For AEs: Commit to One Improvement—Choose one behavior to improve this week (trusting SDR qualification, showing up for meetings, etc.).
