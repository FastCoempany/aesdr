Section 01 — The Truth
Meetings You Set Are Worth More Than Meetings You're Handed
The Meetings You Book Define Your Reputation

SDR-sourced meetings are useful. But when you walk into a meeting you booked yourself — off your own research, your own outreach, your own relationship — the dynamic is completely different. You're not catching up on context. You're the context.

  • 01You own the backstory. You know the pain point that surfaced, the trigger that made them respond, the thing you said that landed. An SDR hand-off gives you a name. A self-sourced meeting gives you leverage.
  • 02Trust starts before the meeting does. They already know you did the work. That signals investment — and investment is contagious in a sales conversation.
  • 03SDR performance fluctuates. When the pipeline slows — because it will — the AEs who survive are the ones who never stopped building their own. Self-sourced pipeline is your insurance policy.
The Hard Truth
"The meetings you're handed belong to the company. The meetings you set belong to you."
When you're 100% dependent on SDRs, you've handed control of your quota to someone else's activity. Self-sourcing is how you take that control back — not occasionally, but as a non-negotiable part of the job.

Any SDR can book a meeting. The SDRs who get promoted are the ones who book meetings that actually convert — qualified leads with real pain, real budget, and real urgency. Your reputation is built on what happens after the hand-off.

  • 01Qualification is everything. A meeting with an unqualified lead wastes your AE's time and destroys your credibility. Every meeting you book should pass a clear qualification bar — pain, authority, timeline.
  • 02Context is your currency. The SDR who passes a lead with detailed notes, pain points, and next steps gives the AE a running start. That's how you become the SDR every AE requests.
  • 03Volume without quality is a trap. Booking 20 meetings that go nowhere is worse than booking 8 that convert. Track your meeting-to-opportunity rate and optimize relentlessly.
The Hard Truth
"Meetings booked is a vanity metric. Meetings that convert is the one that gets you promoted."
When your AE closes a deal from a meeting you sourced, you get the credit. When your AE wastes an hour on a bad lead you pushed through, you lose trust. Quality is how you manage up and build your path to AE.

Homework — This Week
  • Audit your current pipeline. Calculate what percentage is self-sourced. If it's below 30%, that's your number-one priority to fix this month — not next quarter.
  • Block 60–90 minutes daily this week for prospecting. Protect it like a customer meeting. 15 minutes research, 30 minutes outreach, 15 minutes follow-up.
  • Identify 3 target accounts to pursue this week. Write personalized outreach for each one — one real insight per contact, not a template. Track: calls made, emails sent, meetings set.
  • Run the 4-part method on at least one account this week from start to booked meeting. Document every step. Review it. That's your repeatable system.
Homework — This Week
  • Review your last 10 meetings booked. How many converted to qualified opportunities? If it's below 60%, your qualification process needs work — fix that before chasing more volume.
  • Ask your AE for honest feedback on your last 3 hand-offs. What context was missing? What would have made the meeting better?
  • Build a pre-call research checklist and use it before every cold call this week. Track how it changes your connect-to-meeting ratio.
  • Run the 4-part qualification method on every prospect this week. Document your notes. Review conversion rates on Friday. That's your repeatable system.
