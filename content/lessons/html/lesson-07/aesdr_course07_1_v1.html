Section 01 — The Mindset
Prospecting Isn't Optional
Outbound Is Your Lifeline

If you're an AE sitting back and waiting for SDRs to hand you everything, you're setting yourself up to fail. Prospecting is part of your job, and the best AEs know they have to stay sharp, stay relevant, and stay in the grind.

  • 01No AE is Above It: Senior or new, no AE gets a free pass on prospecting.
  • 02Don't Be a Liability: Relying only on SDRs makes you a liability to the team.
  • 03The Best Grind Hardest: The AEs who win consistently are grinding just as hard as their SDRs.
The Reality Check
"The best AEs aren't tourists showing up for the close."
They're grinding alongside their SDRs, staying sharp, and proving they deserve every deal they win.

As an SDR, outbound is your bread and butter. Cold calls, sequences, and prospecting volume are what separate top performers from the rest. Nobody is going to hand you a pipeline — you have to build it yourself, every single day.

  • 01Volume Is Non-Negotiable: Consistent outbound activity is the engine that drives your entire pipeline.
  • 02Quality Follows Quantity: The more calls and emails you send, the faster you learn what resonates.
  • 03Top SDRs Outwork Everyone: The SDRs who book the most meetings are the ones who never take their foot off the gas.
The Reality Check
"The best SDRs don't wait for inbound leads to save them."
They're dialing, emailing, and prospecting relentlessly — building pipeline that earns them a seat at the table and a path to AE.

Section 02 — The Dangers
Coasting Kills Careers
Inconsistency Kills Momentum

When you coast, everyone sees it—your SDR, your manager, your peers. Coasting isn't just lazy; it's a red flag that you're replaceable.

  • 01Pipeline Gaps: Relying 100% on SDR-sourced leads guarantees you will miss quotas eventually.
  • 02Skill Decline: Ignoring cold outreach dulls your competitive edge and reduces long-term success.
  • 03Loss of Respect: SDRs notice who's working and who's resting. Coasting damages team trust and morale.
The Fix
"Pipeline ownership = results ownership."
AEs who balance self-sourced leads with SDR-sourced opportunities outperform every time. Stay in the trenches to keep your skills sharp.

When your outbound drops off, the pipeline dries up two weeks later. Inconsistency is the number one killer of SDR careers — your manager sees it, your AE sees it, and your numbers make it impossible to hide.

  • 01Empty Calendar = Empty Pipeline: Every day you skip prospecting is a day without meetings booked two weeks from now.
  • 02Skill Erosion: Cold calling is a muscle. Stop using it and your talk tracks get rusty, your confidence drops, and your connect rates collapse.
  • 03Lost Trust: Your AE and your manager are counting on you. Inconsistent output signals you can't be relied on — and that stalls your promotion to AE.
The Fix
"Activity consistency = career momentum."
SDRs who hit their daily activity targets every single day — not just on good days — are the ones who earn the promotion, the respect, and the pipeline that speaks for itself.

Evaluate Your Efforts
  • How many opportunities am I self-sourcing each month?
  • Are my cold outreach skills sharp, or do they need work?
  • What can I do to lead by example for my SDR?
Evaluate Your Output
  • Am I hitting my daily dial and email targets consistently?
  • How many qualified meetings did I book this week vs. my target?
  • What is my connect-to-meeting conversion rate, and how can I improve it?
Partnering with Your SDR
  • Share Insights: Work together to identify key target accounts.
  • Support Prospecting: Split outreach tasks on high-value leads.
  • Celebrate Wins: Publicly recognize SDR contributions when deals close.
Managing Up & Partnering with Your AE
  • Share Context: Brief your AE on the prospect's pain points before every meeting you book.
  • Ask for Feedback: After every meeting you set, ask the AE how the lead quality was and what to adjust.
  • Track Your Wins: Document every meeting booked and deal sourced — build your case for promotion to AE.
