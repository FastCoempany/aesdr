Section 01 — Introduction
The 30% Rule Applies to You Too

Most AEs think they’re above average. Newsflash: if you’re not consistently delivering, you’re just another replaceable cog. The same principles that apply to SDRs—coachability, effort, and results—apply to AEs.

  • 01The Reality: If you’re not in the top 30% of AEs, you’re replaceable.
  • 02The Expectation: Success comes from owning your performance and delivering results.
  • 03The Choice: Top performers create results, middle performers coast, and bottom performers complain. Which one are you?
The Mirror Test
"If you're not self-sourcing, coachable, and driving results, you're at risk."
The 30% rule isn't just a management tool for SDRs. It's the standard you must hold yourself to as an AE.

Section 03 — The Breakdown
Are You the Problem?

It’s easy to point fingers at SDRs when your pipeline is weak. But if your entire strategy relies on SDR-set meetings, that’s on you.

  • 01Are You Uncoachable? Uncoachable AEs think they’ve figured it all out. They say "I know what I'm doing" and blow off pipeline reviews. The top 30% crave feedback, adapt fast, and improve constantly.
  • 02Are You Blaming SDRs? Relying 100% on SDRs for meetings leads to a fragile pipeline. Complaining about lead quality wastes time instead of fixing it.
  • 03Are You Wasting Energy? If you’re chasing bad-fit deals to look busy, or avoiding prospecting because it’s hard, you’re sabotaging your own success.
The Fix
"Top AEs don't wait for success—they create it."
The top AEs source their own meetings, drive their own success, and partner with SDRs—not blame them.

Section 06 — Homework
Audit Yourself as an AE
Task 1: Audit Yourself
Self-Reflection AreaKey Questions
CoachabilityWrite down the last 3 pieces of feedback you received. Did you apply them?
Pipeline OwnershipHow many self-sourced meetings did I set in the last month?
FocusList 3 tasks you spent the most time on this week. Were they revenue-driving?
Task 2: Set a Plan to Level Up
  • Identify one area you’ve been avoiding (prospecting, self-sourcing, or taking feedback).
  • Set a 2-week measurable goal to address it.
  • Track your progress daily.
Task 3: Ask for Real Feedback
  • Schedule a pipeline review with your manager or SDR.
  • Ask: "Where am I underperforming right now?"
  • Ask: "What’s one thing I need to change immediately?"
