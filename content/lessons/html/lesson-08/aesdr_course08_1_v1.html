Section 01 — Introduction
Not All SDRs Will Make It—and That's Okay
Your Manager Is Already Sorting You—Here's How

Here's the reality: not every SDR is going to make it. And that's fine. As a leader, your energy is limited, and the best way to drive results is to focus on the top 30%—the ones who want to learn, grow, and crush quota.

  • 01The Brutal Reality: 70% of SDRs are uncoachable or uninterested.
  • 02The Energy Drain: Trying to save everyone drains your energy and lowers overall performance.
  • 03The 30% Rule: Focus on those who want to succeed, not those who need to be dragged there.
The Mindset Shift
"Stop trying to save everyone."
Focus on the SDRs who show up, grind, and want to improve. When you do that, your team wins, and you build a culture of excellence.

Here's something nobody tells you on day one: your manager is already sorting the team into tiers. Top 30%, middle 40%, bottom 30%. Where you land determines how much coaching, attention, and opportunity flows your way. This isn't personal—it's how every high-performing sales floor operates.

  • 01The Invisible Sort: From week one, your manager is watching who grinds, who coasts, and who complains.
  • 02Attention Is Earned: Managers invest their limited energy in the people who prove they want it.
  • 03Your Move: Understanding the 30% rule lets you position yourself where the coaching and the opportunities live.
The Mindset Shift
"Your manager isn't ignoring you—they're prioritizing."
If you want more coaching, more mentorship, more opportunities, you have to signal that you belong in the top tier through your actions, not your words.

Section 02 — Identification & Investment
Identifying and Investing in the Top 30%
How to Signal You Belong in the Top 30%

The top 30% SDRs stand out because they ask the right questions, hustle without being pushed, and show resilience when things get tough. These are the people you coach, nurture, and develop.

  • 01Curiosity & Hustle: Watch for SDRs asking "How do I sound?" and booking meetings fast.
  • 02Resilience & Ownership: They handle rejection without giving up and take accountability for their numbers.
  • 03Coaching Strategy: Go all-in. Give personalized feedback, run role-play scenarios, set stretch goals, and celebrate wins.
The ROI
"Invest in them relentlessly."
The top 30% will take your coaching and run with it. They'll far outperform everyone else. Focus 80% of your energy here.

The top 30% aren't the loudest in the room—they're the most consistent. They ask for feedback before they're told to. They track their own numbers. They treat every call like it matters. And their managers notice all of it.

  • 01Ask Before You're Told: "How did that call sound?" and "What should I change?" are the phrases that unlock coaching time.
  • 02Own Your Numbers: Don't wait for your manager to tell you you're behind. Know your metrics cold and course-correct before anyone asks.
  • 03Be Resilient, Not Dramatic: Everyone gets rejected. The top 30% bounce back quietly and dial again. The bottom 30% sulk and vent in Slack.
The Payoff
"Top 30% SDRs get 80% of their manager's energy."
That means more coaching, better leads, stronger references, and a faster path to AE. Position yourself there by being undeniable in your effort and attitude.

Section 04 — Homework
Prioritizing Your Time and Energy
Position Yourself in the Top 30%
Segment Your Team
SegmentFocus / Action
Top 30%High potential, coachable, proactive. Invest 80% of energy here.
Middle 40%Steady performers. Keep them consistent.
Bottom 30%Uncoachable, uninterested. Set boundaries and let them figure it out.
Create a Coaching Plan for the Top 30%
  • Choose 2 SDRs in the top 30%.
  • Write out one stretch goal to challenge them.
  • Identify one area of focus for improvement (e.g., tone, pacing, objection-handling).
  • Create a plan to celebrate their next big win publicly.
Set Boundaries With the Bottom 30%
  • Stop chasing uncoachable SDRs.
  • Write down a process for addressing poor performance objectively and moving on if needed.
Audit Where You Stand
Tier SignalHonest Self-Assessment
Top 30% SignalsAsking for feedback, owning metrics, bouncing back fast, self-correcting.
Middle 40% SignalsHitting quota but not exceeding it, waiting for direction, doing the minimum.
Bottom 30% SignalsMaking excuses, skipping prep, blaming leads, disengaging in meetings.
Build Your Top 30% Action Plan
  • Identify one behavior you can start this week that signals top-tier effort (e.g., asking for call feedback daily).
  • Track your own metrics for 5 straight days before your manager asks.
  • After your next rejection, write down what you'll change on the next call — then do it.
Advocate for Yourself
  • Schedule a 15-minute check-in with your manager to ask: "What do your top performers do differently?"
  • Ask your manager for one specific thing you can improve this week — then follow through and report back.
