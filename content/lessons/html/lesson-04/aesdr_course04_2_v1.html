Section 01 — The Reality
Stop Overthinking Company Culture

Culture isn't built through offsites, mission statements, or motivational Slack channels. For AEs, culture is built — or destroyed — in how you treat the SDRs feeding your pipeline, how you handle a rough quarter, and whether you step up when things get hard. When the AE-SDR handoff works, the whole company feels it.

  • 01You Set the Tone: As an AE, you're the visible face of sales success. How you treat your SDR partner, share credit, and handle losses defines the floor's culture more than any exec keynote.
  • 02Pipeline Health = Team Morale: When deals close, everyone celebrates. When pipeline stalls, finger-pointing starts. Your relationship with your SDR is the first place that tension shows up.
  • 03The Ripple Effect: An AE who invests in their SDR partnership creates a flywheel — better leads, better demos, better close rates. An AE who ignores it creates resentment that poisons the whole team.
The Diagnosis
"Company culture isn't a mysterious vibe — it's what happens between the people who generate revenue."
As an AE, you have outsized influence on culture because your performance is visible and your partnerships are foundational. Small, intentional actions compound fast.

Culture isn't built through surveys, BBQs, or mission statements. It's built — or destroyed — by the daily interactions between AEs and SDRs. When those teams work in sync, culture thrives naturally. And as an SDR, you're closer to the front line than anyone.

  • 01Sales Teams Are the Engine: The AE-SDR relationship sets the tone for the entire company. And you're half of that equation.
  • 02Sales Performance = Vibe: When sales is solid, morale is up. When sales is down, tension and finger-pointing emerge. SDRs feel it first because you're on the phones when the pressure hits.
  • 03The Ripple Effect: When SDRs and AEs are in sync, meetings convert, deals close, and energy is high. When that alignment breaks, your hard-booked meetings die on the vine and resentment builds.
The Diagnosis
"Company culture isn't a mysterious vibe you brainstorm in Slack threads."
The sales team is the heartbeat of the company. Focus on small, intentional actions to fix the real culture — starting with how you and your AE work together every day.

Section 02 — Three Small Acts
Three Small Acts That Create Big Culture

Culture isn't a 'nice-to-have' — it directly affects your close rate. When your SDR feels supported, lead quality goes up. When partnership is real, pipeline is real. These three acts take minutes but compound over quarters.

  • 01Acknowledge Your SDR: A quick "Good morning" or "Nice booking yesterday" costs you nothing and signals that you see their work. AEs who do this get better leads. It's that simple.
  • 02Build a Helper Document: Share your best email frameworks, the objections you hear on demos, and the messaging that resonates with decision-makers. Give your SDR the ammo to book you better meetings.
  • 03Step Into the Dirt: Once a week, pick up the phone and cold call alongside your SDR. Fifteen minutes. It shows solidarity, sharpens your own skills, and gives you real-time insight into what prospects are saying at the top of funnel.
The Benefit
"Why should an AE invest in these acts?"
Because the AE-SDR handoff is the most critical junction in your pipeline. When your SDR trusts you and has context from you, they book meetings that actually close. The investment is minutes; the return is deals.

Culture isn't a 'nice-to-have' — it's essential for team trust and performance. Simple actions like helping, acknowledging, and partnering drive results. And as an SDR, you can initiate all three.

  • 01Start with Small Acknowledgments: Kick off the day with a "Good morning" or "Let's win today" to your AE. Small gestures create connection and make you memorable as a partner, not just a lead source.
  • 02Share What You're Hearing: You're on the phones all day — you know what prospects care about right now. Share those insights with your AE. That's a Helper Document in reverse, and it makes you indispensable.
  • 03Ask Your AE to Step Into the Dirt: Invite them to cold call alongside you for 15 minutes. When they hear what you face daily, alignment skyrockets and they'll invest more in your partnership.
The Benefit
"Why do these three acts matter?"
They build respect, create alignment, and reinforce teamwork. When AEs trust your lead quality and you feel supported, your meetings convert at a higher rate and your confidence grows.

Your Helper Document Starter Kit
What to Ask Your AE For
  • Top 3 email frameworks that get replies from your target personas.
  • Common demo objections + the rebuttals that work — so your SDR can pre-handle them.
  • Key talking points for your top 5 accounts so outreach is targeted, not generic.
  • Ask for their top email frameworks — adapt them for cold outreach.
  • Ask what objections come up in demos — so you can pre-qualify around them.
  • Ask which accounts are highest priority — so you can focus sequences where they matter most.
Actionable Homework for AEs
Actionable Homework for SDRs
  • Create a helper document for your SDR this week. Include email frameworks, objection rebuttals, and account priorities.
  • Cold call alongside your SDR for 15 minutes. Hand off a booked meeting if you can — it changes the dynamic immediately.
  • Send one specific piece of deal feedback to your SDR: "That meeting you booked converted because of X."
  • Self-Reflection: When was the last time I invested in my SDR's development? What would change if I did it weekly?
  • Start the day with a quick acknowledgment to your AE. Make it specific: "Thanks for the feedback on that call — I used it today."
  • Share one prospect insight or blocker you're facing. Align on what you're hearing in the market.
  • Ask your AE to cold call with you for 15 minutes this week. Frame it as a learning opportunity for both of you.
  • Self-Reflection: What do I know from my calls this week that my AE doesn't? How can I share that proactively?
