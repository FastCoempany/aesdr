Why Sales Survival Needs Rules

The AE/SDR relationship is critical for pipeline success, but misalignment leads to chaos. Common problems—ego, lack of communication, blame—fuel frustration and missed goals.

  • 01The Challenge: Missteps like unchecked egos, poor communication, and blame kill team performance.
  • 02The Solution: Survival commandments provide a shared framework for mutual respect and success.

Section 02 — The Ten Commandments
The Essential Rules of Survival

The Ten Commandments of AE and SDR Survival provide the essential rules to keep the relationship productive.

  • IDrop the Ego: AEs aren't Steve Jobs, and SDRs shouldn't expect a parade for one meeting. Practice humility—acknowledge team contributions publicly.
  • IICommunicate Like Adults: Before critiquing Marketing or Product, talk to them. Schedule a 10-minute daily huddle to align on priorities.
  • IIIShow Up and Respect Time: AEs—show up for SDR meetings. SDRs—schedule at reasonable times. Use shared calendar tools with clear time blocks.
  • IVStop Micromanaging: Trust SDRs to qualify leads and avoid unnecessary CCs. Agree on weekly touchpoints to review progress without hovering.
  • VAcknowledge Contributions: AEs—shout out SDRs when you close big deals. SDRs—speak up when you need AE support. Publicly recognize each other's wins.
  • VIAvoid the Blame Game: Own your pipeline and commit to post-mortems for missed deals. Find solutions, not scapegoats.
  • VIIKeep Ambitions Realistic: AEs—master your role before eyeing VP. SDRs—earn promotions by overperforming, not politicking.
  • VIIIShare Information: AEs—share client insights that help Marketing or Product. SDRs—update lead notes to avoid duplicate efforts. Transparency wins.
  • IXDevelop Self-Awareness: If SDRs avoid working with you, reflect on your behavior. If every AE feels "difficult," the problem might be you.
  • XKeep a Sense of Humor: Sales is chaotic—embrace the absurdity. Laughter builds camaraderie and reduces stress. Don't take yourself so seriously.
The Stakes
"What happens when these rules are ignored?"
Blame and frustration run rampant. Pipeline and targets suffer.
