Section 01 — The Diagnosis
Why AE Managers Are the Way They Are

AE Managers sit between two incompatible pressures: leadership demanding numbers and a sales team demanding support. The behaviors that look like circus acts — the obsessive dashboards, the endless meetings, the blame deflections — are all coping mechanisms for that impossible middle position.

Understanding why they behave that way doesn't mean tolerating it. It means you stop reacting emotionally and start managing up strategically.

  • 01Their quirks are structural, not personal. Insecurity, KPI obsession, outdated training — all of it maps back to the pressure they're absorbing from above.
  • 02You can't change the archetype. But you can change how you navigate it. The leverage is always in your own behavior, not theirs.
  • 03The cost of not managing up is yours. Missed targets, burnout, wasted selling time — you pay that tax whether or not you chose to understand the dynamic.
The Reframe
"You can't manage the circus, but you can stop being surprised by it."
Name the archetype. Map the behavior. Then respond with a strategy instead of a reaction. That's the whole playbook — applied consistently.

Section 02 — Real Costs
What Bad Management Actually Costs

Poor management isn't an inconvenience. It has a direct line to productivity loss, pipeline damage, and team churn. Naming the cost turns it from a frustration into a problem you can address.

Section 03 — The Framework
The 3-Step Managing-Up Framework

Three steps, in order. Skip any one and you're reacting instead of managing.

  • 01Identify Their Style. Write down which archetype your manager fits. Name the specific behaviors that create friction. You can't navigate what you haven't named.
  • 02Align Goals and Expectations. Set a weekly 15-minute alignment meeting. Share your KPIs before they ask. Ask one clear question per meeting: "What's the one thing I can do this week that helps you most?"
  • 03Communicate Upward, Effectively. Share context alongside every metric. Name your blockers before they surface in a pipeline review. Frame everything as "here's what I'm doing about it" — never just the problem.
The Accountability Check
"Are you managing up — or just complaining with structure?"
Managing up means taking a specific action to change how the dynamic plays out. If you're frustrated but haven't scheduled the alignment meeting, you're not managing up yet. You're describing a problem.

Homework — This Week
  • Identify your AE manager's archetype from the 10 in this lesson. Write down two specific behaviors that create friction for you — be precise, not vague.
  • Schedule a 15-minute weekly alignment meeting with your manager this week. Use the template above. If they say no, send a written version via Slack every Friday.
  • Implement one boundary script from the library this week. Pick the situation that maps most closely to your current friction point. Use it exactly as written the first time — then adapt.
  • Self-reflection: Are you responding emotionally to your manager's quirks, or are you managing up effectively? Write down the last three times you felt frustrated. What specific action could you have taken instead?
