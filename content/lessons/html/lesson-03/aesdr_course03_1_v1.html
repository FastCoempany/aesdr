Section 01 — The Diagnosis
Why Performance Pitfalls Hit Your Pipeline
Why Addressing SDR Challenges Matters

Every deal in your pipeline started as an SDR conversation — which means SDR performance pitfalls aren't just their problem. They're your problem. Weak qualification wastes your prep time. Low proactivity thins your pipeline. Generic outreach produces meetings that go nowhere.

But these pitfalls hit AEs directly too. Over-reliance on inbound, sloppy discovery, and failure to adapt your demo to what the prospect actually said — these are the AE versions of the same patterns. This lesson is about recognizing them in yourself and in your team.

  • 01Poor qualification — whether from your SDR or your own discovery — means hours spent on deals that were never going to close.
  • 02Lack of proactivity means you're waiting for pipeline to come to you instead of building it. That's how quarters go sideways.
  • 03Generic approach — running the same demo for every prospect, the same follow-up for every deal — produces mediocre results at scale.
The Growth Frame
"The best AEs don't just close well — they diagnose pipeline problems before they compound."
Understanding these pitfalls makes you a better closer and a better partner to your SDR. When you can name the pattern, you can fix it — in yourself and in your team.

SDRs are the backbone of the sales pipeline — every opportunity an AE closes started as an SDR conversation. Which means unresolved SDR challenges don't stay contained. They cascade: missed targets, wasted AE time, and eroded team confidence.

Addressing performance pitfalls isn't about blame. It's about identifying the gaps between where you are and where you need to be — and closing them systematically.

  • 01Poor qualification passes weak leads to AEs who then spend hours on conversations that go nowhere. That erodes their trust in you.
  • 02Lack of proactivity means fewer opportunities generated — the pipeline thins quietly before anyone notices.
  • 03Generic outreach returns low engagement. Low engagement produces low ROI on every hour of outreach effort.
The Growth Frame
"Every pitfall is a solvable problem — not a fixed trait."
The SDR who acknowledges where they're stuck and attacks one thing at a time will outgrow the one who waits for conditions to improve. Growth starts with awareness and gets done through action.

BANT Qualification Checklist
CriterionQuestion to AskPass If...
Budget"Do they have the budget allocated for this?"They have a budget range and it covers your minimum deal size.
Authority"Are we speaking with the decision-maker or an influencer?"You're talking to someone who can champion or approve the deal.
Need"What specific problem are we solving for them?"They've articulated a real, pressing business problem — not just curiosity.
Timeline"When do they need a solution in place?"They have an active initiative with a real deadline in the next 90 days.

Homework — This Week (AE)
  • Identify which of the four pitfalls you see most in your own deal management — not just in your SDR. Be honest about where your process gets lazy.
  • Review the last 5 leads your SDR passed you against the BANT checklist. Score each one. Share the results with your SDR — with specifics, not just "these were weak."
  • Schedule a feedback session with your SDR this week. Pick one area. Give specific, written feedback — not general encouragement. Track whether they implement it.
  • Personalize your next 3 follow-up emails with specific references to what each prospect said in discovery. Not templates — actual listening applied in writing.
Homework — This Week (SDR)
  • Write down the one pitfall from the four explored in this lesson that you recognize most in yourself. Be honest. Then implement one specific fix from the toolkit this week.
  • Use the BANT checklist on every lead you're about to pass to your AE this week. If you can't answer all four, don't pass it — ask for more information first.
  • Add one genuine, research-based personalization element to every outreach sequence you send this week. Not "I saw you're at [Company]" — something specific.
  • Ask your AE for one specific piece of feedback on your qualification quality this week. Not "how am I doing" — "what's one thing I could improve about the leads I pass you?"
