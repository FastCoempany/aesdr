Section 01 — The Anatomy of Your OTE
What OTE Really Means

OTE (On-Target Earnings) is a fantasy. Base pay is your reality. The recruiter dangled $250K OTE, but your actual take-home might look very different once clawbacks, split deals, and quota accelerators (or decelerators) hit. AE comp plans are designed to look generous — and to claw back aggressively.

  • 01Base Salary: Often 50% or less of your OTE. This is the only guaranteed paycheck — everything else is a bet on your pipeline.
  • 02Variable Pay: Commission tied to closed-won revenue. But the payout depends on quota attainment, deal size thresholds, and whether the deal sticks past the clawback window.
  • 03Hidden Deductions: Clawbacks if a customer churns within 90 days, multi-year deal discounting, split credits with overlay reps, and quota inflation every quarter you overperform.
The Reality Check
"You're promised $250K, but pocketing $160K."
AEs who budget around their OTE are one bad quarter from financial stress. Plan around your base. Commissions are a bonus — not a paycheck.

OTE (On-Target Earnings) is a fantasy. Base pay is your reality. The recruiter dangled $75K OTE, but your take-home pay might look very different once variables and deductions hit. SDR comp plans are structured to motivate volume — but the fine print often works against you.

  • 01Base Salary: 60-70% of your OTE if you're lucky. This is the only guaranteed paycheck — everything else depends on booking meetings that actually convert.
  • 02Variable Pay: Commission tied to meetings set, qualified opportunities, or even downstream revenue you can't control. If your AE can't close the deal, your commission might still be at risk.
  • 03Hidden Deductions: Meetings that don't convert get clawed back. Quota inflation punishes your best months. And payout delays mean you might wait 60-90 days to see the cash.
The Reality Check
"You're promised $75K, but pocketing $52K."
SDRs who plan their life around OTE are one slow month from scrambling. Budget around your base salary and treat commissions as bonuses — not guaranteed income.

Section 02 — Understanding Your Comp Plan
Read the Fine Print

Before you assume you're getting paid on that deal, you need to understand the mechanics of your comp plan. AE plans are complex by design — accelerators, decelerators, splits, clawbacks, and thresholds all exist to protect the company, not you.

  • 01Payout Timelines: Is commission paid on booking or on cash collected? Some plans don't pay until the customer pays — that could be 60-90 days after you sign the deal.
  • 02Clawback Rules: How long does a customer need to stay before your commission is secure? If they churn in 90 days, you might owe the money back. Know the cancellation window cold.
  • 03Accelerators & Caps: What happens when you exceed quota? Some plans have generous accelerators; others cap your upside. Know whether overperformance actually rewards you — or just raises next quarter's target.
The Bottom Line
"Know every line of your comp plan — or it will cost you."
The AEs who get surprised by their paychecks are the ones who never read the fine print. Budget around your base. Commissions are earned twice: once when you close, and again when the customer stays.

Before you assume you're getting paid on that meeting, you need to understand the mechanics of your comp plan. SDRs often get the short end of the stick — especially when meetings are tied to downstream closures you can't control.

  • 01Payout Timelines: Are commissions paid monthly? Quarterly? When do you actually see the cash? Some plans don't pay until the AE closes — which could be months after your meeting.
  • 02Clawback Rules: Does your meeting need to convert to a qualified opportunity to count? What happens if the AE disqualifies it? Understand what "qualified" actually means in your plan.
  • 03Commission Caps: Is there a limit on what you can earn if you overperform? If you're crushing it, does the plan actually reward you — or does it just raise next month's target?
The Bottom Line
"Treat your base like gold."
Stop planning your life around potential earnings. Budget your base salary. Ask your manager to walk through every detail of your comp plan until you understand exactly what gets paid and when.

Section 04 — Homework
Own Your Earnings
Task 1: Comp Plan Deep-Dive
  • Pull up your full comp plan document. Highlight every section on clawbacks, accelerators, deal splits, and payout timelines.
  • Write down at least 3 questions to clarify with your manager this week — especially around multi-year deals and overlay splits.
Task 1: Comp Plan Deep-Dive
  • Review your compensation plan and highlight anything confusing — especially what counts as a "qualified" meeting and when you actually get paid.
  • Write down at least 3 questions to clarify with your manager this week.
Task 2: Build Your Commission Tracker
  • Create a tracker to log every deal, payout, and deduction.
  • Include: Deal Name, Close Date, ACV, Commission Rate, Split %, Clawback Window, Adjustments, Final Payout.
  • Cross-reference finance's numbers against your own tracker every pay cycle. Trust, but verify.
Task 2: Build Your Commission Tracker
  • Create a tracker to log every meeting booked, its status, and your expected payout.
  • Include: Meeting Date, Account, AE Assigned, Meeting Status, Commission Earned, Adjustments.
  • Never rely solely on finance's numbers. Track your own and flag discrepancies immediately.
Task 3: Financial Planning Exercise
  • Build a monthly budget based on your base salary alone — no commission included.
  • Create a commission allocation plan: savings, debt repayment, investments. Treat every commission check as bonus money, not expected income.
