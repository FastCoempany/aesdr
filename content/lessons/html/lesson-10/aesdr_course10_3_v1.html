Section 01 — Introduction
Why Sales Income Feels Like a Rollercoaster

The feast-or-famine cycle is the defining financial reality of an AE's life. One quarter you close a whale deal and feel invincible; the next, your pipeline dries up and you're staring at a bare commission statement wondering where it all went. Learning to handle these swings is what separates career AEs from burnouts.

  • 01Feast Quarters: A big closed-won deal hits your account. Suddenly you're upgrading your car, eating out every night, and spending like this is the new normal. It's not.
  • 02Famine Quarters: Deals push to next quarter. A champion leaves. Budget freezes. Three months of pipeline work evaporates. The stress compounds because you spent the feast like it would last forever.
  • 03The Goal: Build financial discipline that smooths out the rollercoaster. Your lifestyle should match your base salary, not your best quarter.
The Reality Check
"That $40K commission check is not your new normal — it's a windfall."
AEs who spend their commission the month it arrives are one bad quarter from financial panic. Build the habit now: base salary funds your life, commissions fund your future.

The feast-or-famine cycle hits SDRs hard — especially when your comp is tied to meetings that may or may not convert. Some months, your commissions land and you feel like you've made it; others, meetings get cancelled, leads go cold, and you're staring at a base-salary-only paycheck. Learning to handle the swings is what keeps you in the game.

  • 01Feast Months: You had a hot month — meetings booked left and right, bonuses hitting your account. Overconfidence kicks in and spending creeps up fast.
  • 02Famine Months: Connect rates drop. Leads dry up. Meetings get no-showed. Your variable comp disappears and you regret every dollar wasted during the feast.
  • 03The Goal: Handle the income swings like a pro by building financial habits that outlast any single hot or cold month.
The Reality Check
"Commission is not your paycheck — it's a bonus."
If you spend every dollar you earn during a feast month, you'll suffer when the famine hits. Live off your base. Bank the variable. Build a buffer.

Section 03 & 04 — Knowing Numbers
Stop Living in Delusion

OTE is a fantasy. Your real income is your base salary plus a conservative commission estimate based on historical performance — not your best quarter ever. Stop guessing and start tracking.

  • 01Track Your Earnings: Log every deal, every payout, and every clawback for at least 6 months. Find your actual average monthly income — not the one you tell yourself about.
  • 02Plan for the Worst: Budget your lifestyle off your base salary alone. That whale deal was great — but it's not repeatable every quarter. Don't upgrade your life based on one close.
  • 03Build Stability: Create a financial buffer that covers 3-6 months of expenses. When you're not financially desperate, you negotiate better, walk away from bad deals easier, and sell from a position of confidence.
The Bottom Line
"Famine quarters are when you feel the feast quarter mistakes."
The AEs who survive long-term in this game are the ones who save aggressively during hot quarters and live lean when the pipeline dries up. Financial stability makes you a better closer — desperation does the opposite.

Building Your Safety Net
Task 1: Track Your Numbers
  • Log all earnings for the past 6 months: base, commissions, clawbacks, splits, and bonuses.
  • Calculate your real average monthly income — not your OTE, your actual take-home.
  • Identify your worst month and build your budget around that floor, not your ceiling.
Task 1: Track Your Numbers
  • Use a spreadsheet to log all earnings for the past 3 months: base salary, meeting bonuses, and variable payouts.
  • Identify your average monthly income and your worst month.
  • Factor in deductions: taxes, any clawbacks for meetings that didn't convert, and payout delays.
Task 2: Build a Safety Net
  • Target an emergency fund covering 6 months of living expenses — AE income swings are bigger and less predictable than most roles.
  • During feast quarters, redirect at least 50% of commission to savings before spending a dollar on lifestyle upgrades.
Task 2: Build a Safety Net
  • Start building an emergency fund with at least 3 months of living expenses.
  • Slash the fat: Identify non-essential spending you can cut immediately during dry months.
Task 3: Set Spending Rules
  • Cap commission spending at 20% max. No new car, no vacation upgrade, no lifestyle inflation until the safety net is funded.
