Section 01 — The Culture Problem
Work Hard, Drink Harder?

For years, alcohol has been an unspoken cornerstone of sales culture. Happy hours, closing celebrations, and client dinners are all centered on drinking. But the sharpest sales reps are realizing that booze isn’t helping them close deals—it’s holding them back.

  • 01The Old Way: Bonding happens over drinks. Alcohol is the ultimate social lubricant. Burnout is just the norm.
  • 02The New Way: Bonding happens through mutual success. Clarity is a competitive advantage. Longevity matters more.
  • 03The Reality: Authentic relationships don’t need alcohol. Value, respect, and shared goals matter more than shots at midnight.
The Mindset Shift
"Clients trust you more if you drink with them."
False. Trust comes from value and delivery, not alcohol. Drinking feels like a shortcut to building relationships, but it’s just a crutch.

Section 02 & 03 — Focus and Longevity
Sharper Thinking and The Long Game

Alcohol might loosen you up, but it kills your clarity. Reps who stay sharp outperform their hungover competition every time. A sharp mind catches details that close deals.

  • 01Sober Selling = Sharper Thinking: Nuanced insights and stronger pitches replace sloppy conversations. Energized days beat sluggish mornings.
  • 02Longevity Over Burnout: The 'play hard' lifestyle catches up eventually. Prioritize health, energy, and resilience for consistent performance over years.
  • 03Playing the Long Game: Sobriety isn’t about saying no to fun; it’s about saying yes to long-term dominance. Short-term wins aren't worth long-term burnout.
The Bottom Line
"Team camaraderie happens at the bar."
Actually, team bonds are built through shared wins and mutual success, not forced drinking sessions.

Section 04 — Homework
Create Your Sobriety Strategy
Task 1: Identify Alcohol Pressure Points
  • Write down the scenarios where you’re most likely to feel pressured to drink (e.g., happy hours, client dinners, SKO).
  • Acknowledge these triggers ahead of time.
Task 2: Craft Your Go-To Response
  • Come up with a confident, simple line for turning down alcohol.
  • Example: “I’m taking a break tonight—sparkling water’s perfect for me.”
  • Practice delivering it without apologizing.
Task 3: Suggest an Alternative
  • Plan 1-2 team bonding activities or client meetings that don’t involve alcohol.
  • Suggest coffee, lunch, or an activity-based event instead of drinks.
