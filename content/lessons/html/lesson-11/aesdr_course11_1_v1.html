Section 01 — The Culture Problem
Work Hard, Drink Harder?

For years, alcohol has been woven into AE culture. Client dinners, closing celebrations, and executive happy hours all revolve around drinks. But the AEs who consistently crush quota are realizing that booze isn't sealing deals—it's sabotaging them. Every foggy morning is a demo you're not sharp for. Every sloppy dinner is a relationship you damaged.

  • 01The Old Way: Bond over bourbon with the VP of Sales. Close the deal at the steakhouse bar. Celebrate every signed contract with rounds.
  • 02The New Way: Bond through delivered value and consistent follow-through. Clarity during negotiations is a competitive weapon. Longevity in the role beats burning bright and flaming out.
  • 03The Reality: Your best client relationships are built on trust, not tequila. The deals that stick come from preparation and presence, not liquid courage at dinner.
The Mindset Shift
"Clients trust you more if you drink with them."
False. Clients trust you when you remember what they said, follow up on time, and deliver what you promised. Alcohol makes all of that harder.

For years, alcohol has been baked into SDR culture. Team happy hours after hitting dials, post-SKO bar crawls, and "you earned it" drinks on Friday. But the SDRs who actually get promoted are realizing that booze isn't fueling their grind—it's draining it. Every hungover morning is a call block you're sleepwalking through.

  • 01The Old Way: Celebrate every booking with the team at the bar. Drinks are how you bond with your pod. Burnout is just part of the grind.
  • 02The New Way: Bond through shared wins and friendly competition. Clarity on cold calls is your edge. Longevity in the role means you actually get promoted instead of burning out.
  • 03The Reality: Your best outbound days happen when you're sharp, hydrated, and locked in. Nobody ever booked a meeting because they were hungover.
The Mindset Shift
"The team bonds better over drinks."
False. Teams bond over shared wins and mutual respect. Drinking together might feel like connection, but it's just a shortcut that doesn't build real trust.

Section 02 & 03 — Focus and Longevity
Sharper Thinking and The Long Game

Alcohol might loosen you up before a client dinner, but it kills your clarity during the conversation that matters. AEs who stay sharp catch the buying signals, navigate objections cleanly, and remember every detail for the follow-up. That's the edge.

  • 01Sober Selling = Sharper Demos: You catch the subtle hesitation in a prospect's voice. You pivot your pitch in real time. You remember exactly what the CFO said about budget timing. None of that happens hungover.
  • 02Longevity Over Burnout: The AEs who hit President's Club year after year aren't the ones closing deals at the bar—they're the ones who show up energized, prepared, and consistent. The 'work hard, play hard' crowd flames out by year three.
  • 03Playing the Long Game: Sobriety isn't about missing the celebration dinner. It's about being the one who remembers the conversation, sends the recap email at 7 AM, and closes the deal Monday morning.
The Bottom Line
"Client dinners require drinking to build rapport."
The best rapport comes from being genuinely present—listening, asking sharp questions, and following through. The AE who remembers the details always beats the one who bought the rounds.

Alcohol might take the edge off after a brutal day of cold calls, but it wrecks the next morning. SDRs who stay sharp hit their dials with energy, handle objections with precision, and maintain the consistency that gets them promoted. That's the real edge.

  • 01Sober Selling = Sharper Outreach: Your voice has energy on cold calls. Your sequences are tighter. You personalize emails instead of copy-pasting because your brain is actually working. That's the difference between booking and bombing.
  • 02Longevity Over Burnout: The SDRs who get promoted aren't the ones closing out the bar on Thursday—they're the ones who show up Friday with energy, hit their numbers consistently, and earn the AE seat. The party crew washes out in six months.
  • 03Playing the Long Game: Sobriety isn't about skipping team happy hour. It's about being the SDR who's still sharp at 4 PM on a Friday, booking that last meeting while everyone else is mentally checked out.
The Bottom Line
"Team camaraderie happens at the bar."
Real camaraderie comes from hitting targets together and celebrating wins that matter. The SDR pod that pushes each other to grow will always outperform the one that just drinks together.

Section 04 — Homework
Create Your Sobriety Strategy
Task 1: Identify Alcohol Pressure Points
  • Write down the scenarios where you feel most pressured to drink (e.g., client dinners, closing celebrations, executive happy hours, SKO events).
  • For each scenario, note whether the drinking actually helped close a deal or build a relationship—or just felt expected.
  • Write down the scenarios where you feel most pressured to drink (e.g., team happy hours, post-blitz celebrations, pod outings, SKO events).
  • For each scenario, note whether the drinking helped your performance or team bond—or just felt like the default.
Task 2: Craft Your Go-To Response
  • Come up with a confident, simple line for turning down alcohol.
  • Example: "I've got an early client call—sparkling water tonight."
  • Example: "Morning call block starts at 8—I'm staying sharp tonight."
  • Practice delivering it without apologizing.
Task 3: Suggest an Alternative
  • Plan 1-2 client meetings or team bonding activities that don't involve alcohol. Suggest a coffee meeting, a working lunch, or an activity-based outing.
  • For your next client dinner, decide your drink limit before you arrive. Write it down and commit.
  • Suggest a team bonding activity that doesn't revolve around drinking. Pitch it to your manager or pod—lunch, bowling, a group workout, or a competitive outing.
  • Next time the team celebrates a win, suggest a non-alcohol option. See how it goes.
