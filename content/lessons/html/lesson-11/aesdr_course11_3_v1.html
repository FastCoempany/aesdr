Section 01 — First Impressions
Your Drink Is Part of Your Brand

As an AE, every client interaction is a branding exercise. How you dress, how you present, and yes—what you order at the bar—all shape how prospects and executives perceive you. At a client dinner or partner event, your drink choice is a subtle signal about your judgment.

  • 01What You Think It Says: "I'm relaxed and easy to work with." What It Actually Says: "I'm not taking this meeting seriously."
  • 02What You Think It Says: "I'm matching the client's energy." What It Actually Says: "I can't control my own choices—how will I manage this deal?"
  • 03What You Think It Says: "It's just a fun cocktail." What It Actually Says: "I don't understand executive norms."
The Reality Check
"At client-facing events, your drink is part of your personal brand."
Executives notice. A classic cocktail or simple choice signals confidence and control. An umbrella drink at a partner dinner signals you don't belong in the room.

As an SDR, conferences and team events are your chance to be seen by leadership and prospects. Everything you do gets noticed—including what's in your hand. You're building a professional reputation from scratch. Your drink choice is a small detail that sends a big signal about your maturity and judgment.

  • 01What You Think It Says: "I'm relaxed and fitting in." What It Actually Says: "I'm not taking this opportunity seriously."
  • 02What You Think It Says: "I'm just having fun with the team." What It Actually Says: "I'm immature and not ready for more responsibility."
  • 03What You Think It Says: "It's just one shot with the pod!" What It Actually Says: "I lack the self-control to manage myself—how will I manage a book of business?"
The Reality Check
"At team events and conferences, leaders are watching."
Your manager, AE team leads, and VPs all form opinions at these events. A club soda with lime signals maturity. Shots at the bar signal you're not ready for the next level.

Section 02 — The Cheat Sheet
Your Professional Event Drink Guide

At client dinners and partner events, keep it simple. Your drink choice should signal that you're confident, in control, and focused on the conversation—not the cocktail menu. The best AEs order without hesitation and never make it a topic of discussion.

  • 01Safe Choices: Beer (Light lagers, pilsners), Cocktails (Old Fashioned, gin and tonic), Wine (Red or white to match the meal), Non-Alcoholic (Club soda with lime—nobody questions it).
  • 02Avoid These: Umbrella drinks (Mai Tai, Piña Colada), Shots (Tequila, Jägerbombs), Dessert cocktails (Appletini). These undermine the executive presence you've built.
  • 03Also Avoid: Overly strong craft beers (>9% ABV) and complicated custom orders. You're here to close a deal, not curate a tasting flight.
The Bottom Line
"At a client dinner, your drink order is a micro-signal."
Executives form opinions on small details. Order something classic, keep the focus on the deal, and never let your drink become the story.

At team events and conferences, keep it simple. Your drink choice should signal that you're professional, composed, and worth investing in. The SDRs who get promoted treat every social event as an audition—because it is.

  • 01Safe Choices: Beer (Light lagers, pilsners), Cocktails (Old Fashioned, gin and tonic), Wine (Red or white), Non-Alcoholic (Club soda with lime—confident, clean, and nobody cares).
  • 02Avoid These: Umbrella drinks (Mai Tai, Piña Colada), Shots (Tequila, Jägerbombs), Dessert cocktails (Appletini). These make you look like you're at spring break, not a professional event.
  • 03Also Avoid: Overly strong craft beers (>9% ABV) and complicated custom orders. You're trying to impress your future AE manager, not the bartender.
The Bottom Line
"At a team event, leadership is always watching."
You want to be remembered for your energy, your questions, and your professionalism—not for being the one who ordered a fishbowl cocktail.

Section 04 — Homework
Build Your Professional Event Strategy
Task 1: Choose Your Drink
  • Write down your "go-to" safe drink option before your next event.
  • Ensure it reflects executive-level composure (e.g., sparkling water, a classic cocktail, or a glass of wine that matches the setting).
  • Ensure it reflects professional maturity (e.g., sparkling water, a light beer, or club soda with lime).
Task 2: Craft Your Exit Line
  • Prepare a polite phrase for when you need to decline a shot or leave early.
  • Example: "Early call with a prospect on the East Coast—let's reconnect at breakfast."
  • Example: "Morning call block starts at 8—I want to crush it tomorrow. See you at breakfast!"
Task 3: Plan for Situations & Set Your Limit
  • What will you say if someone pressures you to drink? Practice it.
  • Decide beforehand exactly how many drinks you'll have—then stick to it.
  • Before your next client dinner or conference, write down your drink limit and your top 3 conversation goals. Review them before you walk in.
  • Before your next team event or conference, write down your drink limit and one person you want to impress professionally. Review it before you walk in.
