Section 01 — The Diagnosis
Why Your Coaching Isn't Working
Why Most Coaching Fails Before It Starts

Most companies under $50M ARR don't have the time or budget for real coaching. You get training modules, quarterly reviews that never happen, and managers distracted by bad numbers. If you want your SDR to perform, you have to build the coaching yourself.

  • 01Lack of Time. You're overwhelmed with your own pipeline and deprioritize coaching — even though an hour a week saves 20+ hours of bad meetings downstream.
  • 02One-Size-Fits-All. Generic coaching doesn't move the needle. An SDR great at cold calls but struggling with emails needs a different approach than one who's the inverse.
  • 03Fear of Competition. A misplaced belief that coaching your SDR somehow threatens your position. It doesn't. It scales your success.
The Brutal Truth
"Real coaching doesn't exist unless you decide to build it."
No manager, no process, and no training module will fill the gap. Your SDR's performance is a direct reflection of what you invest in them.

Section 02 — What Real Looks Like
What Coaching Your SDR Actually Looks Like
What Good Coaching Actually Looks Like

Real coaching is human-centered and tactical. Not a generic script. Not a quarterly review. It's specific, consistent, and calibrated to the individual in front of you.

  • 01Human-Centered. Understand this SDR's specific strengths and weaknesses. What's their natural style? Where does confidence break down? Start there.
  • 02Tactical. Break down specific areas: call intros, objection handling, email structure, qualification. Not vibes — mechanics.
  • 03Consistent. Weekly feedback and small, achievable adjustments. The compound effect of consistent small improvements outperforms any single big coaching session.
The Frame
"Coaching your SDR doesn't mean you're building competition. It means you're scaling your success."
Great SDRs get you more qualified meetings. More qualified meetings get you better close rates. Their success is your pipeline.

Real coaching is human-centered and tactical. Not a generic script. Not a quarterly review. It's specific, consistent, and calibrated to you as an individual — not a one-size-fits-all playbook.

  • 01Human-Centered. Good coaching meets you where you are. Your AE should understand your strengths and where confidence breaks down — but you have to surface that, not hide it.
  • 02Tactical. Demand specifics: call intros, objection handling, email structure, qualification. "You're doing great" is not coaching. Mechanics are coaching.
  • 03Consistent. Weekly feedback and small, achievable adjustments. One coaching session doesn't change anything. The compound effect of consistent small improvements does.
The Frame
"The SDRs who get the best coaching are the ones who demand it — with preparation, not complaints."
Show up to your 1:1 with a call recording and a question. That's how you get coaching that actually moves the needle instead of surface-level encouragement.

Homework — This Week
  • Schedule 1 hour for coaching this week. Review 2 SDR calls and deliver written, not verbal, feedback on each.
  • Ask your SDR: "What's one area where you feel you need the most coaching right now?" — then actually address it.
  • Bring 1 call recording and 1 email sequence to your next 1:1. Walk your AE through what you think went wrong before they tell you.
  • Self-evaluate in writing before every feedback session. What went well? Where do you need help? Don't wait for the AE to set the agenda.
  • Run the 60-minute coaching framework this week — call review, email feedback, role-play, Q&A. Own the agenda.
  • Prepare for the 60-minute coaching framework: bring a call recording, an email draft, and one specific question. Own your side of the session.
