Section 01 — The Diagnosis
Why Most Coaching Fails Before It Starts

Most companies under $50M ARR don't have the time or budget for real coaching. You get training modules, quarterly reviews that never happen, and managers distracted by bad numbers. Real coaching doesn't exist unless AEs and SDRs decide to make it happen themselves.

  • 01Lack of Time. AEs are overwhelmed and deprioritize coaching — even though an hour a week saves 20+ hours of bad meetings downstream.
  • 02One-Size-Fits-All. Generic coaching doesn't move the needle. An SDR great at cold calls but struggling with emails needs a different approach than one who's the inverse.
  • 03Fear of Competition. A misplaced belief that coaching your SDR somehow threatens your position. It doesn't. It scales your success.
The Brutal Truth
"Real coaching doesn't exist unless you and your SDR decide to build it."
No manager, no process, and no training module will fill the gap. The only people who can make this real are you and the person next to you in the org chart.

Section 02 — What Real Looks Like
What Coaching Actually Is

Real coaching is human-centered and tactical. Not a generic script. Not a quarterly review. It's specific, consistent, and calibrated to the individual in front of you.

  • 01Human-Centered. Understand this SDR's specific strengths and weaknesses. What's their natural style? Where does confidence break down? Start there.
  • 02Tactical. Break down specific areas: call intros, objection handling, email structure, qualification. Not vibes — mechanics.
  • 03Consistent. Weekly feedback and small, achievable adjustments. The compound effect of consistent small improvements outperforms any single big coaching session.
The Frame
"Coaching your SDR doesn't mean you're building competition. It means you're scaling your success."
Great SDRs get you more qualified meetings. More qualified meetings get you better close rates. Their success is your pipeline.

Homework — This Week
  • AEs: Schedule 1 hour for coaching this week. Review 2 SDR calls and deliver written, not verbal, feedback on each.
  • AEs: Ask your SDR: "What's one area where you feel you need the most coaching right now?" — then actually address it.
  • SDRs: Bring 1 call recording and 1 email sequence to your next 1:1. Walk your AE through what you think went wrong before they tell you.
  • SDRs: Self-evaluate in writing before every feedback session. What went well? Where do you need help? Don't wait for the AE to set the agenda.
