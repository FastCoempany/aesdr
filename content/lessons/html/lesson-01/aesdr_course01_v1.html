Section 01 — The Foundation
Why Lack of Structure Kills SDR Success
Why Structure Is the Difference Between Surviving and Succeeding

Without structure, an SDR has no clear understanding of what's expected or where to focus. They end up guessing — which leads to poor results, frustration, and attrition. As an AE, building structure isn't optional. It's the job.

  • 01Confusion about priorities leads to unfocused outreach and missed opportunities that compound daily.
  • 02Disengagement follows when SDRs feel lost. Structure removes the guesswork before it becomes resentment.
  • 03The cost is never just performance — it's attrition. A struggling SDR who isn't supported leaves.
The AE's Responsibility
"Structure starts on Day 1. It starts with you."
Implement the framework. Align daily. Commit to the process — because no one else will build it for your SDR if you don't.

Without structure, you're guessing — what to prioritize, what "good" looks like, and whether you're actually on track. Nobody hands you a playbook that works. The best SDRs don't wait for one. They build it.

  • 01No clear daily structure means scattered effort — 50 dials with no plan behind them won't convert to meetings.
  • 02Without a system for self-evaluation, you can't improve what you can't measure. You need to own your own feedback loop.
  • 03The SDRs who survive past month 3 aren't the most talented — they're the ones who built structure before anyone told them to.
Your Responsibility
"Don't wait for your AE to hand you the playbook. Build it yourself."
Own your schedule. Track your numbers. Ask for feedback before it's offered. The SDRs who take initiative with structure are the ones who get promoted.

Build Structure for Your SDR
Build Your Own Structure
  • Schedule 15 minutes daily for call review. Pick one call, give one specific, actionable adjustment. Not five. One.
  • Run the Weekly Alignment Agenda this Friday. Pull last week's numbers before the meeting — not during it.
  • Write out the 3 most common product questions your SDR hears. Review their answers — are they sharp enough to earn a meeting?
  • Write out the 3 most common questions you hear about the product. Practice answering them in 3 minutes flat with your AE today.
  • Log 10 cold calls per day this week. Bring your top challenge — one specific thing — to each feedback session.
  • Start your daily log today: What did I learn? What confused me? What do I need to ask tomorrow?
