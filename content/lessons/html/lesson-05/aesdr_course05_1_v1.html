Section 01 — The Opportunity
Why Coaching SDR Promotion Makes You a Better AE
Why the Senior SDR Role Actually Matters

The best AEs don't just close deals — they build the pipeline machine around them. When you actively coach your SDR toward Senior SDR, you get a sharper partner who books higher-quality meetings, researches accounts deeper, and fights harder for your pipeline.

Understanding the SDR promotion recipe isn't optional for AEs. It's how you build a reliable feeder system for your deals — and how you earn the reputation as a closer who develops talent, not just a quota-hitter.

  • 01Better pipeline quality. A Senior SDR sends you meetings that are pre-qualified, researched, and positioned. That means fewer wasted demos and a higher close rate for you.
  • 02Stronger deal support. When your SDR operates at a senior level, they provide competitive intel, account research, and objection prep that makes your presentations sharper.
  • 03Leadership visibility for you. AEs who develop their SDRs get noticed. Coaching talent is the fastest signal to management that you're ready for enterprise accounts or a team lead role.
The AE Advantage
"The AEs who build the best SDR partnerships close more, stress less, and get promoted faster."
Your SDR's promotion isn't just their win — it's proof that your partnership model works. AEs who invest in SDR development consistently outperform those who treat SDRs as meeting machines. All six steps in the recipe strengthen your pipeline.

Senior SDR isn't a participation trophy. It's the first signal in your career that leadership sees you as someone who performs without being managed — someone building toward AE or a leadership track, not just hitting a number.

The move from SDR to Senior SDR isn't magic. It's a recipe. Six specific moves, executed consistently, that make you impossible to ignore.

  • 01Higher visibility with leadership. Senior SDRs get pulled into conversations that regular SDRs aren't in. That's the value of the title beyond the pay bump.
  • 02More challenging, more rewarding work. The best accounts, the most complex sequences, the real feedback loops — those go to the people who've proven they can handle them.
  • 03Fastest path to AE or management. If you want to be an AE or a team lead in 18 months, Senior SDR is the checkpoint you must hit first. There's no shortcut around it.
The Real Standard
"Senior SDRs aren't just top performers — they make the people around them perform better."
The promotion isn't for the person who quietly hits quota. It's for the person who out-hustles, shares what they know, elevates the AE relationship, and makes their impact visible. All six steps in the recipe point to this.

Homework — This Week (AE)
  • Schedule a 15-minute coaching sync with your SDR. Review their top 3 target accounts and give specific guidance on positioning and pain points that match your demo strategy.
  • Share one piece of deal intelligence with your SDR — a competitive insight, a pricing objection pattern, or a champion's buying criteria — that they can use in outreach this week.
  • Review your SDR's meeting quality from last week. For each meeting that converted to pipeline, tell them why it worked. For each that didn't, give one specific improvement.
  • Update your pipeline forecast and proactively brief your manager with deal context before they ask. Include one specific ask for support on a stalled deal.
Homework — This Week (SDR)
  • Write down 3 product pain points and 3 benefits you can deliver confidently on a cold call — from memory. If you can't, that's your week-one priority before anything else.
  • Book at least 1 meeting this week and tell your manager before they see it in the CRM. Proactive visibility is the whole point.
  • Post one market insight in the team Slack this week. Two sentences, your take on a trend, relevant to positioning. Not a shared article with no comment — your actual perspective.
  • Build one tangible value-add for your AE: a 5-account research doc, a rebuttal guide for the top 3 objections, or a list of 10 high-fit leads with a one-line rationale for each.
