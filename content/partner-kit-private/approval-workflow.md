# Approval Workflow

When you write something that isn't pre-cleared in the [Promotional Copy Pack](/partners/kit-private/promo-copy-pack),
this is how to get it through.

## When you need to submit

You need an approval round if you're publishing any of:

- **Reworded copy** — anything beyond the bracketed-placeholder edits in
  the pre-cleared templates.
- **Net-new copy** — a post, video, or email AESDR hasn't seen before.
- **Ad-hoc paid placement** — sponsored posts, boosted posts, podcast
  ad reads not in the pre-cleared scripts.
- **Ephemeral content** — stories, lives, reels not on the agreed plan.
- **Pull-quotes from registrants** — even with their permission. We
  check the claim against AESDR's approved-claims list.
- **A platform we didn't agree on** — if your pilot agreement covers
  newsletter and podcast, a TikTok push needs an approval round.

You don't need a round for:

- Any of the six pre-cleared social posts, lifted with placeholders
  filled.
- The two newsletter templates, lifted with placeholders filled.
- The live intro and podcast intro scripts, used within their length
  caps.
- Plain replies to your own audience's comments — in your voice, staying
  clear of [banned vocabulary](/partners/kit/banned-vocabulary).

## How to submit

Email `partner@aesdr.com`. Subject line: `Approval — [Partner Slug] —
[Surface] — [Date you want to publish]`.

Body needs:

1. **The draft itself** — pasted as plain text, not screenshotted.
2. **Where it's going** — newsletter, LinkedIn, podcast, etc.
3. **When you want to publish** — date and ideally time.
4. **What's locked vs. flexible** — if a date or framing is fixed (e.g.,
   tied to a newsletter send), say so.

That's it. No fancy template, no review checklist on your end.

## Turnaround

**24 business hours** for first response. Business hours are
Monday–Friday, US business day. A submission that lands Friday afternoon
is a Monday-morning answer.

The first response is one of:

- **Approved as written** — publish on your stated date.
- **Approved with edits** — we'll send a redline; replace the flagged
  language and publish.
- **Needs a deeper look** — we'll come back inside 48 hours with a real
  answer rather than rubber-stamp something we're not sure on.
- **Declined** — the draft makes a claim or uses a register the brand
  can't carry. We'll explain why; sometimes the explanation reveals an
  edit that gets it approved.

## What gets edited most often

Five patterns we walk back regularly. Catch these in your own draft and
the round is faster:

1. **Soft outcome claims** — "more AEs and SDRs are closing deals after AESDR"
   gets walked back to "more AEs and SDRs are reading their pipeline more
   honestly after AESDR" or similar (an observation, not an outcome).
2. **Banned vocabulary** — anything in the [banned list](/partners/kit/banned-vocabulary)
   gets edited. Catch this yourself.
3. **Pricing language anywhere except the registration page** — pricing
   on social, in podcasts, in newsletters gets walked back to "list
   pricing on the program."
4. **Implied urgency** — "only X seats left," "last chance," countdown
   timers. AESDR doesn't run that register; the deadline framing in
   pre-cleared post 6 is the canonical version.
5. **Founder name-drops in audience-facing copy** — AESDR is host-fronted
   to the audience; the founder is visible to you, not to them. "Built by
   AESDR" lands; "built by [name]" doesn't.

## What gets declined outright

Three things we won't approve:

- **Income or hiring claims** — any version of "you'll close more deals,"
  "you'll get promoted," "you'll get hired."
- **Discount claims** — any framing that suggests pricing your audience
  can't actually get.
- **Workshop content extracts** — screenshots, quotes, or paraphrases of
  the live workshop's frameworks. The recording is gated; the workshop is
  the offer.

## After approval

Approval is for the surface, audience, and date you submitted. If any of
those change materially — different platform, different audience segment,
significantly later date — submit again. We track approvals against the
specifics.

Once published, no need to re-confirm with us. We'll see it on our side
through the audit log and Friday reporting.
