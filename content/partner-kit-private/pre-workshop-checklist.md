# Pre-Workshop Checklist

Three short lists for the windows where partners get most nervous on
their first AESDR pilot: the 24 hours before the workshop, the live
itself, and the 72 hours after. If the items below all clear, you've
done your job — the rest is on AESDR.

## 24 hours before

**Registration page**
- [ ] Open your partner-specific registration URL in an incognito window.
      Confirm it loads, the form submits, and the confirmation email
      arrives.
- [ ] Confirm the **AESDR × Your Partner Mark** lockup is visible at
      top-center of the page.
- [ ] Confirm the workshop date / time / timezone are right.

**Audience comms**
- [ ] Reminder send (template 09b) scheduled or sent. (3–5 days out is
      ideal; 24h is acceptable.)
- [ ] At least one social post (template 09d post 1, 2, or 3) live in
      your channels.
- [ ] Calendar invite confirmed in your own calendar with the right
      meeting link.

**The intro**
- [ ] Read the live workshop intro script (the 2-minute one) out loud
      once. Time it. If it runs long, cut a sentence — don't run long on
      stage.
- [ ] Confirm you have the host's first name down. (You'll be saying it
      twice in the intro.)
- [ ] Confirm you know the meeting URL, your camera setup, your audio
      setup. Don't troubleshoot at T-minus-5.

## Day-of

**T-minus-30 minutes**
- [ ] Be at your computer. Test the meeting URL one more time.
- [ ] Mute notifications, close anything that can ping you.
- [ ] Glass of water within reach.

**During the live**
- [ ] Read the intro. Don't ad-lib new content; the script is the deal.
- [ ] Hand off to the host. Cleanly. Plain "over to you, [name]."
- [ ] Stay on camera with mute on. The audience seeing the partner
      who invited them is a trust signal.
- [ ] Drop one or two non-leading questions in the chat during the talk
      if helpful — modeling that the chat is open.
- [ ] **Do not** screenshot or quote the host's words during the live.
      Workshop content is gated; live attendance is the offer.
- [ ] **Do not** discuss pricing in the chat. Even if asked. "AESDR
      will cover that at the close" and move on.

**The close**
- [ ] Stay until the end of Q&A. The audience seeing you stay for the
      offer-window matters.
- [ ] Drop one closing message in chat: "Thanks for being here — replay
      will be in your inbox within 10 minutes if you need it."
- [ ] Don't pile on the offer with your own framing. The host runs the
      close; you don't co-pitch.

## The 72 hours after (replay window)

**Within 4 hours of the live ending**
- [ ] Confirm the replay email landed in registrants' inboxes. (Spot-check
      one or two if you can.)
- [ ] Post template 09d post 4 (post-workshop variant) on your social.

**Day 1 after**
- [ ] Reply to inbound questions from your audience in your voice — staying
      clear of the [banned vocabulary list](/partners/kit/banned-vocabulary)
      and not making any [forbidden claims](/partners/kit/approved-claims).
- [ ] Forward to AESDR (`partner@aesdr.com`) any audience questions you
      can't answer cleanly. We respond within 24 business hours.

**Day 2 after**
- [ ] Post template 09d post 5 (replay-window framing).

**Day 3 after — replay closes**
- [ ] Post template 09d post 6 (deadline-window framing) at T-minus-24h
      from replay close.
- [ ] After replay closes: silence. No "one final extension" — that's
      not how AESDR pilots run.

## What goes wrong, and what to do

**Workshop tech failure (host's audio drops, etc.)**
- AESDR has the operational backup. You don't. Don't try to fix it from
  your end.
- If you're on screen during the issue, say plainly: "We're working on
  it — sit tight."
- The recording resumes once the issue is resolved. The replay email
  goes out as planned.

**Lower-than-expected attendance**
- Don't post about attendance numbers publicly during or after.
- Replay is the buffer — most enrollments come from replay watchers
  anyway. The Friday report will show you the real conversion math.
- If attendance is materially below your expectations (e.g., <50% of
  registered), email `partner@aesdr.com` after the live. We'll do a
  joint debrief on what to adjust for the replay window.

**Audience complaint about the workshop content**
- Don't engage publicly. Forward the message to `partner@aesdr.com`
  with the original audience member's permission.
- AESDR responds to substantive complaints within 24 business hours,
  with a CC to you so you have the response in writing.

**Refund spike during the 14-day window**
- Some refunds are normal. >15% within the window is the threshold for
  a joint debrief.
- Refunds don't affect your commission until the 14-day window closes
  and net is calculated. Don't panic in the first week.

**Anything else**
- Email `partner@aesdr.com`. We'd rather hear it once than have it sit.
