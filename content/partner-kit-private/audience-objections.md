# Audience Objections — Honest Answers

When you promote AESDR to your audience, the predictable objections will
come back at you. Below are the 15 most common questions a SaaS rep
asks before paying $249 — plus the honest answer, in language you can
adapt to your voice.

The answers here match the live FAQ at `/syllabus`. If you see a
mismatch, the live one wins — email the founder so we can resync.

## On the program itself

### "Is this just another sales course?"

No. AESDR isn't a course on selling — there are a thousand of those.
It's the operating manual: how to read your own pipeline honestly, what
to do when the script runs out, what "good" actually looks like Monday
morning. The closest thing to a comparison: it's what you'd want your
manager to teach you in 1:1s if they had the time and the framework.

### "What's actually in it?"

Twelve interactive lessons, three sub-units per lesson, ~144 sections
total. Five takeaway tools (worksheets you actually use — alignment
contract, strike plan, ROI tracker, etc.). Role-conditional content on
every lesson — SDR-flavored exercises hide if you marked yourself as AE,
and vice versa. Private Discord (Untamed) for ongoing conversation with
other AESDR enrollees. Lifetime access including future updates.

### "Is this for me if I've been selling for 8 years?"

It depends on whether you're open to revisiting fundamentals. The first
five lessons are foundational and will be familiar to a veteran — they
exist to make sure everyone has the same vocabulary. Lessons 6–12 go
deeper on prospecting strategy, pipeline math, financial resilience,
and career-level relationship building. If you're a veteran who thinks
you've already figured it out, this isn't for you. If you're a veteran
who knows the gaps in your own game and wants a clean reset, it is.

### "Does it teach cold calling?"

Yes — Lesson 6 is heavily phone-based. Dial blocks, openers,
gatekeepers, the "I don't know" framework on live calls, energy on the
phone. The course assumes you're picking up the phone, not just sending
emails.

### "Is there a certificate or badge?"

No. AESDR doesn't issue credentials. The "I took AESDR" line on a
resume is just a line on a resume — it doesn't carry hiring weight at
any specific company, and we don't pretend it does. The takeaway is the
operating capability, not the credential.

### "How long does it take?"

Self-paced. Most people finish a lesson in 90 minutes including the
interactive exercises. So roughly 18 hours of focused time for all 12
lessons, spread however you want. Most enrollees do one lesson per week.

## On the workshop (the live event you'll be hosting them at)

### "Is the workshop just a sales pitch?"

No, but there's an offer at the end. The workshop is 60 minutes of
actual teaching — three core ideas from the curriculum, applied to
real-shaped pipeline problems. The last 5 minutes name the program and
the offer. Plus 10–15 minutes of live Q&A after.

### "Will I get the slides / a recording / a replay?"

Yes — a 72-hour replay window. After 72 hours the replay closes. (No
evergreen "watch it whenever" — AESDR doesn't run those.) Slides go to
registrants who attend the live; not posted publicly afterward.

## On the price

### "Why $249?"

It's priced so that one SDR ramping faster pays for it in their first
month. The 12-lesson interactive build is the work of dozens of operators
over a decade. The math is: if the program saves one rep from one bad
quarter, it paid for itself many times over. If you don't think the
program is worth $249 for what's inside, the 14-day refund is real.

### "Can I get a discount through [your name]?"

No. AESDR doesn't run discount codes, partner-stack pricing, or
audience-specific promotions. The price is the price everywhere. If a
discount existed, you'd be getting it from someone you trust — and we'd
rather your recommendation be that the program is worth the list price.

### "Can I expense this?"

Almost always yes. AESDR provides a receipt and invoice on purchase.
Most L&D budgets cover this easily, especially the $1,499 Team plan.

### "What if I hate it?"

14-day, no-questions-asked refund. Email `hello@aesdr.com`, a real
person processes it within 3 business days. No fight, no exit
interview, no "but did you really try the lessons" — just refund. We'd
rather lose the $249 than have someone enrolled who isn't getting
value.

## On the community

### "Is there a community or is it self-paced only?"

Both. The course is self-paced. The Discord (Untamed) is included with
every license — real AEs and SDRs in similar situations, talking about
what's working. Not a hype channel. Not a motivation channel. Operator
talk.

### "Is the founder active in the community?"

The founder reads everything, posts occasionally, doesn't dominate. The
community is for reps to talk to each other; the founder is there as a
listener who occasionally steps in when something concrete comes up.
