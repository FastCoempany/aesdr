# Escalation Contacts

Who to email for what during a pilot. Five legitimate reasons a partner
contacts AESDR — match yours to the row, get a faster answer.

## The contacts

| What | Who | Response time |
|---|---|---|
| **Anything urgent during a live workshop** (tech failure, dropped audio, host issue) | The text channel in the workshop chat — type in the live chat. The AESDR team is on it. | Real-time |
| **Copy approval** ([approval workflow](/partners/kit-private/approval-workflow)) | `partner@aesdr.com` — subject line per that page | 24 business hours |
| **Post-workshop debrief, attendance issues, refund spikes, attribution disputes** | `partner@aesdr.com` — subject `Pilot debrief — [Partner Slug]` | 24 business hours |
| **Buyer-side issue from your audience** (refund question, login problem, content question) | Forward to `support@aesdr.com` (CC `partner@aesdr.com` if you want visibility) | 48 business hours |
| **Anything contractual, scope-changing, or payment-related** | `partner@aesdr.com` — subject `Contract — [Partner Slug]` | 48 business hours |
| **Anything else** | `partner@aesdr.com` — say so plainly in the subject. We'll route it. | 48 business hours |

## What "business hours" means

Monday–Friday, US business day. A message that lands Friday afternoon is
a Monday-morning answer. We don't pretend to be 24/7 because pretending
breaks at the moment you actually need us.

The exception is anything urgent **during** a live workshop — that's
real-time via the workshop chat. The AESDR team running the live is
watching it.

## What you don't have to email about

- **Friday reports** — they arrive automatically. If one didn't arrive
  by Friday end-of-day, then email.
- **Standard registrant questions about the workshop** — the
  registration page covers them. Forward your audience there.
- **Disclosure language for a surface we already covered** — the
  [Disclosure Language Pack](/partners/kit/disclosure-language-pack)
  covers every surface; lift verbatim.
- **General positioning questions** — covered in the public kit's
  [Positioning Brief](/partners/kit/positioning-brief) and
  [Co-Promoting AESDR](/partners/kit/co-promoting-aesdr) pages.

## When to escalate beyond email

If you've emailed `partner@aesdr.com` and haven't heard back inside
**72 business hours**, something's gone wrong on our side. Reply to the
original thread with `RE: still no response` in the subject. If still
no reply within 24 hours of that, the partnership agreement names a
direct founder contact for exactly this case — use it.

## Things we'd rather hear once than have sit

- An audience comment that reads as an off-brand reaction to AESDR's
  copy or positioning.
- A platform policy concern (a flag, a removed post, a warning email
  from a platform).
- A time conflict you didn't notice in your pilot agreement until late.
- A change in your audience size, channel mix, or send strategy that
  makes the pilot terms feel off.
- Any question where the honest answer is "I don't know what to do."

We'd rather adjust mid-pilot than learn at the kill-or-keep that
something was off the whole time.
