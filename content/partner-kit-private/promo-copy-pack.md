# Promotional Copy Pack

Pre-cleared copy for the four surfaces partners promote AESDR on:
newsletter (launch + reminder), live workshop intro, podcast / video intro,
and pre-cleared social posts. Lift verbatim with placeholders filled.
Anything outside what's here needs the [approval workflow](/partners/kit-private/approval-workflow)
before publishing.

## Placeholders used throughout

| Placeholder | What to fill |
|---|---|
| `[YOUR_FIRST_NAME]` | Your first name as your audience knows it |
| `[YOUR_AUDIENCE_DESCRIPTOR]` | E.g., "BDR Club members," "Sales Bootcamp alumni" |
| `[YOUR_REASON]` | One sentence: the actual reason you came across AESDR |
| `[HOST_FIRST_NAME]` | The AESDR host's first name (sent to you separately) |
| `[WORKSHOP_DATE]` | Workshop date in your audience's preferred format |
| `[WORKSHOP_TIME_TZ]` | Time + timezone, e.g., `1pm ET` |
| `[REGISTRATION_URL]` | Your partner-attributed registration URL |

## 1. Newsletter — Launch send

**Send timing:** First send of the promotion window, 10–14 days before the
workshop. ~250–320 words including disclosure.

> **Subject:** *A workshop for `[YOUR_AUDIENCE_DESCRIPTOR]` who want operating judgment, not motivation.*
>
> **Pre-header:** *60 minutes live. Free to attend. No motivational content.*

> *(Optional 1–2 sentence personal opener — "why I'm sending this.")*
>
> *I'm hosting a live workshop for `[YOUR_AUDIENCE_DESCRIPTOR]` on
> `[WORKSHOP_DATE]` at `[WORKSHOP_TIME_TZ]`, with AESDR. The workshop is
> built for early-career SaaS SDRs and AEs in their first 0–3 years.*
>
> *Most sales-training content rewards confidence over competence and
> formatting over thinking. AESDR is the opposite: a 12-lesson operating
> manual built around the part of the role most AEs and SDRs figure out the hard
> way — what good actually looks like, how to read your own pipeline
> honestly, and the three judgment moves that separate the AE/SDR who
> survives ramp from the AE/SDR who doesn't.*
>
> ***In the workshop, you'll leave with:***
>
> *— A definition of "good" you can use Monday morning. Not a feeling, an
> operating standard.*
>
> *— The framework for distinguishing pipeline that closes from pipeline
> that's hope.*
>
> *— One specific change you'll make in your next five calls. Not a
> mindset. A move.*
>
> ***Who shouldn't attend:*** *Sales veterans 8+ years in. Anyone looking
> for motivation. Anyone who wants a LinkedIn-friendly badge. None of
> these are insults — they're filters. Save your hour for something that
> fits.*
>
> *Live workshop runs ~60 minutes plus 10–15 minutes of Q&A. There's an
> offer at the close (lifetime access to the full program, 14-day
> refund), and there's a 72-hour replay window for anyone who can't make
> the live.*
>
> ***Register for the workshop →*** `[REGISTRATION_URL]`
>
> *Affiliate disclosure: I earn a commission if you enroll through this
> link. The commission doesn't change the price you pay — it doesn't
> even change what AESDR charges. AESDR runs partner pilots, and
> `[YOUR_AUDIENCE_DESCRIPTOR]` is one of them.*
>
> *— `[YOUR_FIRST_NAME]`*

**Editable elements:** the bracketed placeholders, plus up to two
sentences of personal opener at the top.

**Locked elements:** subject line, pre-header, the three "what you'll
leave with" bullets, the disqualification block, the disclosure
paragraph.

## 3. Live workshop intro (read by partner, 2 minutes capped)

Read at the top of the live workshop, immediately before handing to the
AESDR host. Cap is 2 minutes — practice it once.

> *Hi everyone — thanks for being here. I want to introduce
> `[HOST_FIRST_NAME]` and AESDR briefly, then I'm going to step out of
> the way.*
>
> *I came across AESDR because `[YOUR_REASON]` — one or two sentences.
> It's the only sales-training resource I've seen built specifically for
> the part of the role where you're trying to figure out what good
> actually looks like and nobody's telling you. That gap was real for me,
> and from the conversations I've had with `[YOUR_AUDIENCE_DESCRIPTOR]`,
> I know it's real for a lot of you.*
>
> *Three quick rules for the next 60 minutes:*
>
> *— Drop your questions in the chat. `[HOST_FIRST_NAME]` will get to
> them at the end.*
>
> *— The replay will be available for 72 hours after we wrap.*
>
> *— There's an offer at the close that's specific to this group. That's
> it.*
>
> *Over to you, `[HOST_FIRST_NAME]`.*

**Hard rules:**

- Cap is 2 minutes. Over is a contract issue, not a stylistic preference.
- Do not pitch the offer. "There's a great offer at the end" is fine;
  pricing language is not.
- Do not discuss pricing. Even if asked. "AESDR will cover that at the close"
  and move on.
- Hand off plainly. "Over to you, `[HOST_FIRST_NAME]`" — then stop talking.

## 4. Podcast / video promotional intro (60 seconds)

Used on podcast intros, video promos, YouTube shorts, IG/TikTok pre-rolls.
Performable — script is a starting point, not a verbatim lock — but the
locked elements stay.

> *This week's episode is a quick announcement, not a full conversation.
> I'm hosting a live workshop for `[YOUR_AUDIENCE_DESCRIPTOR]` with
> AESDR — a 12-lesson sales-training program built for early-career SaaS
> AEs and SDRs. Most sales-training rewards confidence over competence; AESDR is
> the opposite. Operating manual, not motivation engine.*
>
> *60 minutes live. Replay for 72 hours after. Free to attend. There's
> an offer at the close — list pricing on the program.*
>
> *Affiliate disclosure: I get a commission if you enroll through my
> link. The commission doesn't change the price you pay.*
>
> *Link is in the show notes. `[REGISTRATION_URL]` if you're listening
> somewhere without notes.*

**Hard rules:**

- 60 seconds nominal. Audio audiences forgive a long episode; they don't
  forgive a long ad.
- Disclosure within the first 10 seconds OR clearly named within the
  intro.
- No "5-star testimonial" framing. No "I've been using AESDR for X
  months" — you haven't.
- No promising codes in audio. Pricing lives on the registration page.

### Six-post hard rules

- **Don't modify the body.** Bracketed placeholders are your only edit
  window.
- **Don't add a CTA** to a post that doesn't already carry one. Posts 1–3
  are awareness; only 5 and 6 carry deadline framing.
- **Don't repost** the same post multiple times to game the algorithm.
- **Don't boost** any post without an approval round. Paid placement is a
  separate gate.
- **Don't ask for reposts** ("@AESDR plz RT 🙏"). Borrowed amplification we
  haven't earned.

### What you may do

- **Post on a delay** — schedule 1, 2, 3 across the promotion window.
- **A/B-style** between posts 1 and 2 if your platform analytics support it.
- **Translate platform-naturally** — a Twitter/X variant may need to be
  split across thread; LinkedIn may carry the full text.
- **Reply substantively** to comments under your post, in your voice,
  staying clear of banned vocabulary.

| Platform | Where the disclosure goes |

| LinkedIn (image post) | Caption *and* in image overlay if there's any visual recommendation |

## 7. The "I'll see you Monday" warmth note

**Send timing:** Within 4 hours of someone enrolling via your link.
Triggered manually (not auto). Personal, in your voice, ~60 words.

> **Subject:** *(no subject — sent from your personal address, reply
> chain feel)*

> *Saw you enrolled via my link — appreciated. The Untamed Discord
> invite should land in your inbox within 30 minutes; if it doesn't,
> reply here and I'll get it sent.*
>
> *Genuinely useful 1–2 sentence personal nudge — what lesson to start
> with given what they've told you about their situation, OR an
> invitation to keep you posted on how lesson 1 lands.*
>
> *— `[YOUR_FIRST_NAME]`*

**Editable elements:** literally everything except the disclosure (which
isn't required on a 1:1 transactional note since the recommendation
already happened — but if you mention the program again, add it).

**Locked elements:** none. This one is personal, not a template send.

**Why this matters:** the people who buy via your link become your
audience members who now have an AESDR relationship. The "I'll see you
Monday" note doubles your retention with that audience by 2–3x at
basically zero cost. (Source: every B2C newsletter operator's hard-won
data.)
